## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

This comprehensive network design for BMS College of Engineering (BMSCE) follows the requested Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access hierarchy, utilizing the HPE Aruba Networking AOS-CX portfolio to ensure high availability, scalability, and performance.

### 2. Physical Topology & Hardware Strategy
*   **Core Layer (Bldg 1, Fl 1):** Deployed as a **VSX Pair** (Virtual Switching Extension) for sub-second failover and active-active redundancy.
*   **Distribution Layer:** Each building utilizes a distribution pair to aggregate access switches, providing 10Gbps uplinks to the core.
*   **Access Layer:** PoE+ switches deployed per floor. High-density wireless zones (Library, CSE/AI/DS) utilize **Smart Rate** ports to support Wi-Fi 6 APs (2.5GbE+).
*   **Wireless Strategy:** APs are calculated at ~30 users per AP. Total APs required: $\approx 75$ units across campus.

### 4. Technical Justification & Performance
*   **Port Density:** Total physical ports provided exceed total endpoints (Students, Staff, Admins, VoIP, IPTV, Printers) by $>20\%$.
*   **PoE Budget:** CX 6300M modular power supplies are selected to ensure the PoE budget covers all VoIP phones and high-power Wi-Fi 6 APs.
*   **Redundancy:** Core uses **VSX** for active-active redundancy. Access switches use **VSF** stacking for simplified management and link redundancy.
*   **QoS:** Implementation of **Strict Priority (SP)** queuing for VoIP and **DWRR** for IPTV to ensure zero jitter for multimedia services.
*   **Security:** 802.1X authentication and Port Security implemented at the access layer; internal ACLs restrict student access to the Admin/DC VLANs.
