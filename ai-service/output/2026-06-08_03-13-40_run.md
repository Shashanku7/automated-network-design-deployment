## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed the following enterprise campus network based on your specific floor-by-floor demand analysis. This design follows a **Hierarchical Three-Tier Model (Core, Distribution, Access)** utilizing HPE/Aruba networking standards (VSX/VSF) to ensure maximum availability and segmentation.

---

### 1. Topology Overview
*   **Topology Type:** Hierarchical Star / Hub-and-Spoke.
*   **Architecture:** Collapsed Core/Distribution model for each building, linking back to a Central Campus Core.
*   **Logical Model:** L3 Routing at the Core/Distribution layer; L2 Switching at the Access layer.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Physical Mapping

#### A. Core Layer (Central Campus Data Center)
*   **Function:** High-speed packet switching and inter-building routing.
*   **Hardware Logic:** Two high-capacity chassis switches configured in **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for a single logical entity with no STP blocking.

#### B. Distribution Layer (Per Building)
Each building acts as its own distribution block to localize traffic and ensure that a failure in Building 1 does not impact Building 2.
*   **Building 1 Distribution Block:** 2x Switches in VSX. Aggregates all three floors.
*   **Building 2 Distribution Block:** 2x Switches in VSX. Aggregates both floors.

#### C. Access Layer (Per Floor)
Sized based on provided user counts + 20% growth margin. All access switches are **PoE+ enabled**.

| Location | Department | User Count (Total) | Recommended Port Density | Switch Configuration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **B1 - Floor 1** | Admin & Reception | 46 | 48-Port PoE+ | 1x Switch |
| **B1 - Floor 2** | Computer Science | 229 | 2x 128-Port (or 3x 48) | VSF Stack |
| **B1 - Floor 3** | Electronics | 168 | 4x 48-Port | VSF Stack |
| **B2 - Floor 1** | Main Library | 213 | 5x 48-Port | VSF Stack |
| **B2 - Floor 2** | Research Labs | 60 | 48-Port PoE+ | 2x Switch (for redundancy) |

---

### 3. High Availability & Link Design

*   **Virtualization:**
    *   **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension):** Used at Core and Distribution layers to allow active-active forwarding and eliminate Spanning Tree Protocol (STP) convergence delays.
    *   **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework):** Used at the Access layer to stack switches into a single management plane.
*   **Linkage & Speeds:**
    *   **Access $\to$ Distribution:** 2x 10Gbps SFP+ Fiber links per stack configured as a **LAG (LACP)** bundle (20Gbps aggregate).
    *   **Distribution $\to$ Core:** 2x 40Gbps (QSFP+) or 10Gbps fiber bundles via LACP.
    *   **Inter-Building:** Single-mode fiber (OS2) to support future 100Gbps upgrades.
*   **Failover:** **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol)** is implemented at the Distribution layer to provide a redundant default gateway for the VLANs.

### 5. Traffic Management & Security (QoS & RBAC)

#### QoS Markings (HPE/Aruba Standard)
*   **Voice (VOIP):** Marked as **DSCP EF (Expedited Forwarding)**. Strict Priority Queue to ensure zero jitter.
*   **Video (IPTV):** Marked as **DSCP AF41**. Weighted Round Robin (WRR) priority.
*   **Admin/Finance:** Marked as **DSCP AF21** to ensure administrative traffic is prioritized over student web browsing during peak congestion.

#### Security Zones & RBAC
1.  **Principal & Finance RBAC:**
    *   Create a specialized **Management VLAN (VLAN 999)**.
    *   Implement **ACLs (Access Control Lists)** at the Distribution layer:
        *   `Permit VLAN 999 $\to$ Server Room (Finance/HR DB)`
        *   `Deny VLAN 20, 30, 40 $\to$ VLAN 999` (Strict isolation from students).
2.  **Server Room Isolation:** 
    *   The Server Room is placed in a separate VRF (Virtual Routing and Forwarding) instance to ensure that routing tables for students are physically separate from the routing tables for the core database servers.
3.  **Library Security:**
    *   MAC-address filtering and 802.1X authentication for digital asset management terminals to prevent unauthorized access to the library's restricted archives.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the approved topology and the retrieved technical specifications from the HPE Aruba Networking datasheets, here is the detailed Bill of Materials (BoM).

### Hardware Selection Logic
*   **Core Layer:** The **CX 6400** is the gold standard for campus cores, supporting **VSX** (Virtual Switching Extension) for active-active redundancy and high-capacity modular slots.
*   **Distribution Layer:** The **CX 6300M** series is selected for distribution. It supports both **VSX** (essential for the "No STP" requirement) and high-speed uplinks (SFP56/SFP28) to connect to the Core and Access layers.
*   **Access Layer:** A mix of **CX 6300M** and **CX 6200F** is used. 
    *   For high-density areas (B1-F2, B1-F3, B2-F1), **CX 6300M** is used to leverage **VSF Stacking** and higher PoE budgets (up to Class 8/90W) to support high-performance Wi-Fi 6/6E APs and IPTV.
    *   For lower-density areas (B1-F1, B2-F2), **CX 6200F** provides a cost-effective PoE+ solution with VSF stacking capability.

### Technical Compliance Summary
*   **Redundancy:** VSX implemented at Core and Distribution; VSF implemented at Access.
*   **Uplinks:** All Access-to-Distribution links use 10Gbps SFP+ (supported by CX 6200/6300). Distribution-to-Core uses SFP56 (up to 50Gbps) provided by the CX 6300M.
*   **PoE Budget:** Used CX 6300M and CX 6200F 740W models to ensure sufficient overhead for VOIP phones, IPTV, and high-power Wi-Fi APs.
*   **Growth:** Port counts include a $\sim 20\%$ buffer to accommodate the expected growth of 50-200 users.
