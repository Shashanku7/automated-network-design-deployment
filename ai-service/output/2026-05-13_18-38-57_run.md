## User Prompt

Design a campus network for an organization with 2 building(s). Across all buildings, there are approximately 2230 students/visitors, 160 staff/faculty, and 16 administrators distributed across multiple floors. Devices needed: laptops, printers, phones, cameras, wifi. Sensitive areas requiring extra security: library, server, exams, finance. Special roles to consider: Principal, Exam Controller, Hostel Warden. Uptime requirement: important. Expecting growth of 200-500.

## Phase 1: Rephrased Prompt

**Project Title:** Scalable Multi-Building Campus Network Architecture Design

**1. Objective**
Design a robust, high-availability campus network topology for an organization spanning two buildings. The architecture must support a diverse user base, ensure seamless connectivity across multiple floors, and provide strict security segmentation for sensitive administrative and academic zones.

**2. User & Device Requirements**
*   **User Population:**
    *   Students/Visitors: ~2,230
    *   Staff/Faculty: ~160
    *   Administrators: ~16
    *   **Projected Growth:** Scalability to accommodate an additional 200–500 users without requiring a core hardware overhaul.
*   **Device Inventory:**
    *   **Endpoints:** Laptops (BYOD and Managed), VoIP Phones, Network Printers.
    *   **Infrastructure:** High-density Wireless Access Points (WAPs), IP Surveillance Cameras.

**3. Performance & Availability Standards**
*   **Uptime Requirement:** Critical. Design for high availability (HA) to minimize downtime.
*   **Topology Recommendation:** Implement a hierarchical Three-Tier Model (Core, Distribution, Access) or a Collapsed Core architecture.
*   **Redundancy:** Include redundant uplinks between buildings and redundant power supplies/failover mechanisms for core switching.

**4. Security & Segmentation (VLAN Strategy)**
The network must be logically segmented via VLANs based on role and security sensitivity.
*   **High-Security Zones (Strict ACLs/Firewalling):**
    *   **Server Room:** Restricted access, management VLAN.
    *   **Finance Office:** Isolated traffic for payroll and accounting.
    *   **Exam Center:** Temporary lockdown capabilities to prevent unauthorized external access.
    *   **Library:** Public-facing but monitored access.
*   **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):** Define specific privilege levels and bandwidth priorities for:
    *   Principal (Highest priority/unrestricted access).
    *   Exam Controller (Administrative control over Exam zone).
    *   Hostel Warden (Management of residential area connectivity).

**5. Physical & Logical Constraints**
*   **Physical Layout:** Two buildings, multiple floors per building.
*   **Cabling:** Specify requirements for fiber optic backbones between buildings (Inter-building) and Cat6a/7 for horizontal distribution (Intra-building).
*   **Wireless:** Design a seamless roaming SSID strategy for students and a separate secure SSID for staff.

**6. Budgetary & Hardware Assumptions**
*   **Budget:** Enterprise-grade (prioritize reliability and scalability over lowest cost).
*   **Hardware:** Assume a mix of Layer 3 switches for the core/distribution and PoE+ switches at the access layer to power cameras, phones, and WAPs.

**7. Deliverables Requested**
*   **Logical Topology Diagram:** Including VLAN assignments, IP addressing scheme (IPv4/IPv6), and routing protocols (e.g., OSPF).
*   **Physical Topology Diagram:** Showing cable runs, rack locations, and building interconnects.
*   **Security Matrix:** A mapping of user roles to accessible network zones.
*   **Scalability Plan:** A brief explanation of how the network will handle the 200-500 user growth.

## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Type:** Hierarchical Three-Tier Campus Architecture (Core, Distribution, Access)
**Design Philosophy:** A "Scale-Out" approach utilizing a High-Availability (HA) Core and Distribution layer. The design employs a **Collapsed Core/Distribution** model per building, linked via a redundant High-Speed Fiber Backbone to create a unified campus fabric.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown
*   **Core Layer (Building A - Main DC):** 2x Chassis-based L3 Switches. Acting as the campus brain and routing hub.
*   **Distribution Layer:**
    *   **Bldg A:** 2x L3 Aggregation Switches (VSX/Stacking).
    *   **Bldg B:** 2x L3 Aggregation Switches (VSX/Stacking).
*   **Access Layer:**
    *   **Bldg A:** 8-12x L2 PoE+ Switches (VSF/Stacking), distributed across floors.
    *   **Bldg B:** 8-12x L2 PoE+ Switches (VSF/Stacking), distributed across floors.

---

### 3. High Availability & Performance
*   **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension):** Implemented at the Core and Distribution layers to provide a single logical entity with active-active forwarding and sub-second failover without relying on STP for convergence.
*   **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework):** Implemented at the Access layer to manage switch stacks as a single entity, simplifying management and providing link redundancy.
*   **LAG (LACP):** All inter-switch links are multi-chassis EtherChannels (MCLAG/LACP) to maximize bandwidth and provide link-level redundancy.
*   **QoS Strategy:** 
    *   **Platinum (EF):** VoIP/Video Conferencing.
    *   **Gold (AF41):** Administrative/Principal Traffic.
    *   **Silver (AF21):** Academic/Staff Traffic.
    *   **Bronze (BE):** Student/Visitor Traffic.

---

### 4. Link Design
| Link Segment | Media | Speed | Redundancy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Core** | 40G/100G Fiber | 100Gbps | Dual-homed / VSX ISL |
| **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution** | 10G/25G OM4 Fiber | 2x25Gbps (LAG) | Dual-homed to both Core nodes |
| **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access** | 10G SFP+ Fiber | 2x10Gbps (LAG) | Dual-homed to both Dist nodes |
| **Access $\leftrightarrow$ Endpoint** | Cat6a/7 Copper | 1Gbps / 2.5Gbps | Single-homed (PoE+) |

### 6. Redundancy & Failover
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol) or Anycast Gateway (via VSX) used at the Distribution layer to provide a seamless default gateway for endpoints.
*   **Routing Protocol:** OSPF (Open Shortest Path First) Area 0 for internal routing between buildings to ensure dynamic path selection and fast convergence.
*   **L2 Loop Prevention:** MSTP (Multiple Spanning Tree Protocol) as a safety fallback, though VSX/LAG eliminates blocked ports in the active path.
*   **Physical Path:** Diverse fiber paths (East/West) between Building A and B to prevent a single cable cut from isolating a building.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the provided topology and the retrieved datasheets, here is the professional Bill of Materials (BOM) for the campus network.

### Bill of Materials (BOM)

| Role | Model & SKU | Key Specifications | Qty | Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Campus Core** | **Aruba CX 6405 v2** (R0X26C) | Chassis-based, modular, supports VSX, high-speed line cards (up to 100GbE), 14 Tbps switching capacity. | 2 | High-availability "Campus Brain." Chassis design allows for future line card growth and provides the required L3 routing hub with VSX active-active forwarding. |
| **Distribution Layer** | **Aruba CX 6300M** (JL658A) | 24-port SFP+ (1G/10G), 4x SFP56 (up to 50G) uplinks, VSX/VSF support, L3 routing (OSPF/BGP). | 4 | 2 per building. Provides the aggregation point for access switches. SFP+ density handles fiber uplinks, and SFP56 ports support the 25G/50G backbone to Core. |
| **Access Layer** | **Aruba CX 6300M** (JL661A) | 48-port 1GbE Class 4 PoE+ (30W), 4x SFP56 (up to 50G) uplinks, VSF stacking support. | 20 | Distributed across floors (8-12 per building). PoE+ powers VoIP phones, cameras, and WAPs. VSF stacking simplifies management as a single entity. |
| **Access Layer (High Power)** | **Aruba CX 6300L** (S3L76A) | 48-port Smart Rate (1G/2.5G/5G) Class 8 PoE (up to 90W), 2x 50G/2x 25G uplinks. | 4 | Deployed in high-density areas or for high-end WiFi 6/6E APs requiring Class 8 PoE (90W) and multi-gigabit speeds. |

### Technical Mapping & Compliance

1.  **Core Layer (Chassis):** The **CX 6405** is selected as the chassis-based L3 switch to meet the requirement for a "campus brain." It supports the high-speed fiber backbone and ensures maximum uptime via redundant management modules.
2.  **Distribution Layer (VSX):** The **CX 6300M (SFP+ model)** is used here to act as the L3 aggregation point. It natively supports **VSX**, allowing the two switches in each building to act as a single logical entity for sub-second failover.
3.  **Access Layer (VSF & PoE+):** The **CX 6300M (PoE+ model)** provides the necessary port density and power for standard endpoints (Phones, Cameras). The **CX 6300L Smart Rate** models are added specifically for "Sensitive Areas" or high-performance zones (e.g., Server/Admin) that may need $>1\text{Gbps}$ and $>30\text{W}$ PoE.
4.  **Uplink Requirements:** 
    *   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** Handled by SFP56 ports (up to 50G) on the 6405 and 6300M.
    *   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** Handled by the 10G SFP+ / SFP56 links.
5.  **Scalability:** The use of VSF (up to 10 members) and modular chassis allows the organization to easily scale for the expected growth of 200-500 additional users without redesigning the core.
