## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Architecture:** Three-Tier Hierarchical Model (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access).
**Design Philosophy:** A collapsed-core approach within the PJ Building, utilizing a **Star-Bus Hybrid** topology. Each floor acts as an independent access block connecting to a redundant distribution pair, ensuring that a failure on one floor does not impact the rest of the campus.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Sizing

#### Core Layer (Building Main Distribution Frame - MDF)
*   **Function:** High-speed packet switching and routing.
*   **Hardware:** 2x Chassis-based switches in a Virtual Switching Framework (VSF/Stack) for a single logical entity.

#### Distribution Layer (PJ Building Block)
*   **Function:** Policy enforcement, VLAN routing, and aggregation.
*   **Hardware:** 2x Layer 3 switches configured with **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for active-active forwarding and stateful failover.

#### Access Layer (Floor IDFs)
*   **Floor 1 (Office):** 1x 48-Port PoE+ Switch (Low density, high security).
*   **Floor 2 (ML):** 2x 48-Port PoE+ Switches (Stacked) to support 197 users + APs/CCTV.
*   **Floor 3 (CS):** 3x 48-Port PoE+ Switches (Stacked) to support 328 users + high-density APs.
*   **Floor 4 (IS):** 2x 48-Port PoE+ Switches (Stacked) to support 271 users.
*   **Floor 5 (Electronics):** 1x 48-Port PoE+ Switch (Moderate density).

---

### 3. High-Availability & Link Design

| Link Path | Speed | Technology | Redundancy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution** | 40/100Gbps | LACP / Multi-Chassis LAG | Dual-homed (Active-Active) |
| **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access** | 10Gbps | LACP (2x10G Bundle) | Dual-homed to both Dist. switches |
| **Access $\leftrightarrow$ Endpoints** | 1Gbps | PoE+ (802.3at) | Single-homed (Standard) |

*   **VSF/VSX:** Eliminates Spanning Tree Protocol (STP) blocking by treating multiple physical switches as one logical unit.
*   **QoS Markings:** 
    *   **EF (Expedited Forwarding):** VoIP & Management traffic.
    *   **AF41 (Assured Forwarding):** High-compute ML/CS data streams.
    *   **BE (Best Effort):** General student Wi-Fi.

### 5. Redundancy & Failover
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol) or VSX-Sync across Distribution switches to provide a virtual gateway IP for all VLANs.
*   **LACP:** All uplinks are bundled (LAG) to ensure that if one fiber strand breaks, traffic fails over instantly without flapping.
*   **Power:** Each IDF is equipped with a UPS; Distribution/Core switches utilize dual hot-swappable power supplies.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the approved topology and the retrieved datasheets from the HPE Aruba Networking CX portfolio, here is the detailed hardware sizing and Bill of Materials (BoM) for the PJ Building.

### 1. Hardware Sizing Analysis

#### Core Layer (MDF)
The design requires a high-speed, redundant chassis-based core. The **Aruba CX 6400 Series** is selected. The 6405 chassis provides the necessary scale, redundancy, and support for 40/100Gbps uplinks and VSF stacking.

#### Distribution Layer (Block)
To implement **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for active-active forwarding and high availability, the **Aruba CX 6300M** series is selected. These switches support VSX and provide the high-speed 10G/25G/50G uplinks required to connect to the Core and the 10G downlinks for the Access layer.

#### Access Layer (Floor IDFs)
Sizing is based on the provided user counts per floor, including a buffer for the requested growth (200-500 users) and additional ports for CCTV, VoIP, and APs.
*   **Floor 1 (Office):** 35 users. 1x 48-port PoE+ switch is sufficient.
*   **Floor 2 (ML):** 197 users. 2x 48-port switches (96 ports) are insufficient for 197 users. However, following the "Approved Topology" instruction to use **2x 48-port switches (Stacked)**, this will serve as a high-density block; for full wired connectivity for all 197 users, additional switches would be needed. To adhere to the approved topology, we use the 48p PoE+ model.
*   **Floor 3 (CS):** 328 users. **3x 48-port switches (Stacked)** provide 144 ports.
*   **Floor 4 (IS):** 271 users. **2x 48-port switches (Stacked)** provide 96 ports.
*   **Floor 5 (Electronics):** 113 users. **1x 48-port switch** is specified in the topology, though a stack of 2 is recommended for this user count.

#### Wi-Fi AP Quantities
Calculated at approx. 25-30 users per AP:
*   Floor 1: 35 users $\rightarrow$ 2 APs.
*   Floor 2: 197 users $\rightarrow$ 7 APs.
*   Floor 3: 328 users $\rightarrow$ 11 APs.
*   Floor 4: 271 users $\rightarrow$ 9 APs.
*   Floor 5: 113 users $\rightarrow$ 4 APs.
