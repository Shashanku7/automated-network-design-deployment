## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Topology Type:** Hierarchical Three-Tier (Collapsed Core/Distribution)
The design utilizes a **Hub-and-Spoke** physical layout with a **Spine-Leaf** logical approach for the access layer. The Core/Distribution layer is collapsed into a high-performance redundant pair located in the Floor 1 MDF, providing a centralized gateway for all inter-VLAN routing and external connectivity.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown
*   **Core/Distribution Layer (MDF - Floor 1):**
    *   Two high-capacity Chassis switches acting as a single logical unit via **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** or **VSS**.
    *   Handles all L3 routing, Policy-Based Routing (PBR), and Firewall hand-offs.
*   **Access Layer (IDFs - Floor 1, 2, 3):**
    *   **Floor 1:** 1x 48-port PoE+ Switch (Staff/Admin/IoT).
    *   **Floor 2 (AI/ML):** 6x 48-port PoE+ Switches (Stacked via VSF). High port density for 200+ students and heavy compute loads.
    *   **Floor 3 (CS):** 9x 48-port PoE+ Switches (Stacked via VSF). High port density for 350+ students and lab environments.

---

### 3. High-Availability & Link Design
*   **Redundancy:** 
    *   **Core:** Dual-homed VSX pair; if one switch fails, the second maintains full stateful forwarding.
    *   **Access:** Each floor stack (VSF) is connected to *both* Core switches via **LACP (802.3ad) Multi-chassis EtherChannel (MEC)**.
*   **Link Speeds:**
    *   **Core to Access:** 2x 10Gbps SFP+ Fiber Uplinks per stack (20Gbps aggregate).
    *   **Horizontal Distribution:** 1Gbps Cat6a to endpoints.
*   **QoS Strategy:**
    *   **Platinum (EF):** VoIP and Video Conferencing.
    *   **Gold (AF41):** Admin/Principal/Exam Controller traffic.
    *   **Silver (AF21):** Staff/Faculty.
    *   **Bronze (BE):** Student/Guest general traffic.

### 5. Redundancy & Failover
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol) or Anycast Gateway (via VSX) ensures that if the primary core switch fails, the virtual IP for the floor gateways migrates instantly.
*   **Loop Prevention:** Rapid Spanning Tree Protocol (RSTP) enabled as a failsafe, though MEC/LACP eliminates the need for STP blocking on primary uplinks.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the provided topology and the retrieved datasheets for the HPE Aruba Networking CX series, here is the detailed Bill of Materials (BoM).

### Hardware Sizing Analysis
*   **Core Layer:** The design requires a high-availability L3 pair. The **CX 6400** series is the designated chassis switch for this role, providing the necessary VSX support and modularity for campus cores.
*   **Access Layer (Sizing):** 
    *   **Floor 1 (Admin/Staff):** 30 users + IoT. A single 48-port PoE+ switch is sufficient.
    *   **Floor 2 (AI/ML):** 218 users + IoT. 6x 48-port switches provide 288 ports, covering the current population and the requested growth buffer (up to 200 additional users).
    *   **Floor 3 (CS):** 384 users + IoT. 9x 48-port switches provide 432 ports, ensuring capacity for high-density lab environments and growth.
    *   **Switch Model:** The **CX 6300M** is selected for the access layer as it supports **VSF stacking** (up to 10 members) and provides high-performance 10G/25G/50G uplinks to the core.
*   **Wireless (AP Quantity):**
    *   Total Users: 632.
    *   Calculation: $632 \text{ users} \div 25 \text{ users/AP} \approx 26 \text{ APs}$.
    *   Distribution: Floor 1 (~2 APs), Floor 2 (~8 APs), Floor 3 (~16 APs) based on density.

### Technical Justification Summary
1.  **Core Redundancy:** The **CX 6405** chassis switches allow for **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**, ensuring that if one chassis fails, the second maintains full stateful forwarding without downtime.
2.  **Access Scalability:** Using the **CX 6300M** series enables **VSF Stacking**. Floor 2 and Floor 3 are designed as single logical units, simplifying management and providing high-bandwidth backplanes (up to 200 Gbps) for heavy compute loads in the AI/ML and CS departments.
3.  **PoE Capability:** All access switches provide **Class 4 PoE** (up to 30W), satisfying the requirements for VoIP phones, security cameras, and high-performance WiFi 6E APs.
4.  **Growth Buffer:** The port density (total 672 ports) comfortably handles the current 632 users and provides room for the projected growth of up to 200 additional users without requiring new chassis/rack installations.
