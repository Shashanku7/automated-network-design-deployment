## Phase 2: Network Topology

# Campus Network Architecture Design

## 1. Topology Overview
**Chosen Tier:** **3-Tier (Core, Distribution, Access)**

**Justification:** 
With a total endpoint count exceeding 800 (inclusive of students, staff, and IoT) distributed across two separate buildings and five floors, a 3-tier architecture is required to ensure high-performance traffic aggregation. The dedicated distribution layer allows for building-specific policy enforcement, limits broadcast domains, and provides the necessary scalability to handle the projected growth of 200+ additional users without impacting the core.

## 3. High Availability & Link Design

### **Core & Distribution Layer (The Backbone)**
*   **Technology:** **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. Two CX 8360 switches in the Core and two CX 6300/6400 switches per building in Distribution.
*   **Redundancy:** VSX Active-Gateway will be used for all default gateways to provide seamless failover without relying on VRRP timers.
*   **Links:** 
    *   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** $2 \times 25\text{Gbps}$ LACP bundles (50Gbps aggregate).
    *   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** $2 \times 10\text{Gbps}$ SFP+ LACP bundles per switch stack.

### **Access Layer**
*   **Technology:** **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacking for all access switches to simplify management and provide local redundancy.
*   **High-Density Areas:** Floors 1 (B2) and 2 (B1) utilize **HPE Smart Rate (1/2.5/5Gbps)** ports for APs to prevent bottlenecks for Wi-Fi 6/6E.
*   **Power:** PoE+ (30W) for standard devices and PoE++ (60W/90W) for high-capacity APs and PTZ surveillance cameras.

## 5. Redundancy & Security Summary

*   **Failover:** The combination of **VSX** at the Core and **VSF** at the Access layer ensures that no single cable or switch failure results in a loss of connectivity. 
*   **Logical Zoning:**
    *   **Admin Zone:** VLAN 300 is restricted via Hardware ACLs at the Distribution layer; only authorized MAC addresses/users (Principal/Finance) can access the Server Room segment.
    *   **Research Zone:** VLAN 210 implements strict East-West traffic filtering to protect intellectual property in Research Labs.
    *   **Guest Access:** Guest VLAN 900 is tunneled directly to a DMZ/Gateway and cannot route to any internal VLANs.
*   **Traffic Management:** Voice (VLAN 400) is tagged with **DSCP EF (Expedited Forwarding)** to ensure zero jitter during congested periods.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

This network design is tailored for a high-density academic and research environment. Based on the approved 3-Tier topology and a detailed analysis of the HPE Aruba Networking CX portfolio, here is the professional hardware recommendation.

### **1. Hardware Selection Strategy**
*   **Core Layer:** We utilize the **CX 8360 v2** series. These are high-performance 1U switches designed for campus cores, supporting **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for hitless failover and providing massive switching capacity (up to 4.8 Tbps) to handle all building traffic.
*   **Distribution Layer:** The **CX 6400** modular series is selected for the distribution blocks. These provide the necessary density and flexibility (modular line cards) to aggregate multiple access stacks and support **VSX**, ensuring no single point of failure between the Core and Access layers.
*   **Access Layer (High Density/Smart Rate):** For Computer Science (B1-F2) and the Library (B2-F1), the **CX 6300M** series is used. Specifically, models with **Smart Rate (1/2.5/5GbE)** are deployed to support Wi-Fi 6E APs and high-performance research workstations, preventing the 1GbE bottleneck.
*   **Access Layer (Standard):** For Administration and Electronics departments, the **CX 6200** series is used. These support **VSF stacking** (up to 8 members) and provide a cost-effective, high-reliability solution for standard endpoints.

### **3. Technical Justification & Compliance**

#### **Physical Port Density & Growth**
*   **B1-F2 (Comp Sci):** Required 286 ports. Provided: 6 switches $\times$ 48 ports = 288 ports. This meets the 20% growth margin and high-density needs.
*   **B2-F1 (Library):** Required 268 ports. Provided: 6 switches $\times$ 48 ports = 288 ports.
*   **General:** All other floors are provisioned with $\approx 20\text{--}30\%$ spare capacity to accommodate the projected growth of 200 users.

#### **PoE Budget & Power**
*   **High-Power Devices:** The **CX 6300M** and **CX 6400** series support **IEEE 802.3bt (Class 8 - 90W)**. This is critical for high-capacity Wi-Fi 6E APs and PTZ security cameras in the Library and Research areas.
*   **Standard Devices:** **CX 6200** (740W budget) is sufficient for VoIP phones and standard APs (Class 4 - 30W).

#### **Downstream & Uplink Speeds**
*   **Smart Rate:** Deployed in B1-F2 and B2-F1 to support 2.5GbE/5GbE connectivity, ensuring wireless backhaul does not bottleneck at 1Gbps.
*   **Backbone:** 
    *   **Access $\rightarrow$ Distribution:** $2 \times 10\text{Gbps}$ SFP+ LACP bundles.
    *   **Distribution $\rightarrow$ Core:** $2 \times 25\text{Gbps}$ (or 100Gbps using CX 8360) LACP bundles, providing massive throughput for the aggregate campus load.

#### **Uptime & Redundancy**
*   **Core/Dist (VSX):** The CX 8360 and CX 6400 utilize **VSX**, which synchronizes state between two switches. Unlike VRRP, VSX allows for active-active forwarding and hitless software upgrades.
*   **Access (VSF):** Access switches are grouped into VSF stacks, allowing them to be managed as a single logical entity with simplified cabling and local redundancy.
