## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have analyzed the requirements for BMS College of Engineering. Based on the total endpoint count, multi-building footprint, and the need for high-availability (HA) for critical examinations and data center operations, I have developed the following architecture.

### 1. Topology Overview
**Chosen Model:** **3-Tier (Core, Distribution, Access)**

**Justification:** 
The campus supports approximately **2,000+ endpoints** across three separate academic blocks, far exceeding the 500-endpoint threshold for a collapsed core. A 3-tier design is required to isolate failure domains per building, provide dedicated aggregation for high-density student areas (like the New Academic Block), and ensure the BMSCE Data Center has a dedicated, high-performance core path without bottlenecking building-to-building traffic.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Port Density
Each building is assigned a **Distribution Block** consisting of a VSX pair of Aruba CX switches. Access switches on each floor are connected via redundant uplinks to this block.

**Calculation Logic:** $Required Ports = (Total Endpoints \times 1.2)$. 
*AP Calculation: 1 AP per 25 users (Students + Staff + Admin).*

### 3. High-Availability & Redundancy
*   **Core Layer:** **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. Two chassis-class switches running an active-active control plane. This ensures zero-downtime during firmware upgrades (Live Upgrades).
*   **Distribution Layer:** **VSX Pairs** per building. This eliminates STP blocking and allows for full bandwidth utilization.
*   **Access Layer:** **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)**. Switches on each floor are stacked into a single logical entity to simplify management and provide local redundancy.
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** **VSX Active-Gateway** is implemented at the Core/Distribution level. This provides a single virtual IP for all VLANs without the convergence delays associated with VRRP.

---

### 4. Link Design & Performance
*   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** 2x 40Gbps or 100Gbps Fiber Optic (OS2 Single Mode) LAG bundles.
*   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** 2x 10Gbps SFP+ Fiber uplinks per VSF stack (LACP bundled).
*   **Access $\leftrightarrow$ Endpoints:**
    *   **Standard:** 1Gbps Copper (Cat6a).
    *   **Smart Rate (Multi-Gig):** In the **New Academic Block (Floor 2 - AI & DS)** and **PG Block (Floor 2 - MCA)**, Aruba CX 6300 switches will be used to provide 2.5G/5G ports for Wi-Fi 6E APs to prevent wireless bottlenecks.
*   **QoS Strategy:** 
    *   **Platinum/Strict Priority:** VoIP (EF Class) and Management traffic.
    *   **Gold:** IPTV and Video Conferencing (AF41).
    *   **Silver/Best Effort:** Student and General Internet traffic.

### Bill of Materials (BOM) for BMS College of Engineering Campus Network

Following a comprehensive analysis of the site requirements and the HPE Aruba Networking product catalog, the following hardware is recommended. This design implements a **3-Tier Architecture (Core $\to$ Distribution $\to$ Access)** to ensure high availability (HA), scalability, and high-performance connectivity.

#### **Hardware Design Summary**
*   **Core Layer:** **CX 8360** switches in a **VSX pair**. This provides a non-blocking, high-throughput core (up to 2.4 Tbps) with active-active redundancy, ensuring zero downtime for the Data Center and critical examinations.
*   **Distribution Layer:** **CX 6400** chassis switches. These are utilized as building aggregators. They support **VSX** for redundancy and provide the high-density 10G/25G/50G uplinks required to feed the access stacks.
*   **Access Layer:** A mix of **CX 6300M** and **CX 6200M**.

### **Technical Justification & Calculation Notes**

1.  **Port Density Calculation:** 
    *   Total Physical Ports $\approx 2,500+$ across access layer.
    *   Calculated as: $(\text{Students} + \text{Staff} + \text{Admin} + \text{VoIP} + \text{IPTV} + \text{APs}) \times 1.2$ growth factor.
    *   The choice of 48-port switches in VSF stacks ensures a seamless management plane while providing the required physical port density per floor.

2.  **Smart Rate Deployment:** 
    *   **CX 6300M** is specifically placed in the **New Academic Block (Floor 2)** and **PG Block (Floor 2)**. These areas house the most technically demanding departments (AI & DS, MCA) and require multi-gigabit (2.5G/5G) ports to prevent Wi-Fi 6E APs from becoming bottlenecks.

3.  **PoE Budgeting:** 
    *   **CX 6200M/6300M** series provide high-density PoE+ (Class 4) and some Class 6 support, ensuring all VoIP phones and high-resolution surveillance cameras are powered without requiring mid-span injectors.

4.  **High Availability Framework:**
    *   **Core/Dist:** VSX (Virtual Switching Extension) allows for an active-active control plane, enabling **Live Upgrades** and zero-convergence time during failure.
    *   **Access:** VSF (Virtual Switching Framework) stacks 48-port switches into a single logical entity, reducing the number of managed IPs and simplifying spanning-tree topologies.

5.  **Connectivity:**
    *   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** 40G/100G Fiber bundles.
    *   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** 10G SFP+ Fiber uplinks (LACP).
