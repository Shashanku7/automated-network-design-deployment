## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview & Architectural Selection

**Selected Model: 3-Tier Architecture (Core, Distribution, Access)**

**Justification:** 
The network supports over 700 total endpoints with high-density concentrations on the 2nd and 3rd floors (CSE/AIML). A 3-tier model is required to provide a dedicated distribution layer that aggregates high-bandwidth traffic from academic labs and enforces security policies before reaching the core, ensuring that the heavy throughput of the CSE/AIML departments does not impact administrative operations.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Port Calculations

The Main Distribution Frame (MDF) is located on Floor 1. Each floor acts as its own distribution block for physical cable management.

**Calculation Formula:** $\text{Required Ports} = (\text{Total Endpoints} + \text{APs}) \times 1.2$
*(AP Calculation: 1 AP per 25 users)*

| Floor | Users (S+St+A) | VoIP | IPTV | APs (est) | Total Endpoints | Req. Ports (+20%) | Switch Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 (Admin)** | 32 | 32 | 2 | 2 | 68 | **82** | 2 x 48-Port PoE+ |
| **2 (CSE)** | 328 | 28 | 4 | 14 | 374 | **449** | 10 x 48-Port PoE+ / mGig |
| **3 (AIML)** | 261 | 21 | 4 | 11 | 297 | **357** | 8 x 48-Port PoE+ / mGig |

*   **Access Layer:** Deployed in IDFs on each floor. High-density student floors (2 & 3) will utilize **Multi-Gigabit (Smart Rate)** ports for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs to prevent uplink bottlenecks.
*   **Distribution Layer:** Pair of redundant switches per building (collapsed into the MDF for this single-building scenario, but logically separated) to aggregate floor access stacks.
*   **Core Layer:** High-backplane capacity switches managing the routing between VLANs and the external gateway.

---

### 3. High-Availability & Redundancy

*   **Core/Distribution Layer:** Implementation of **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** or **StackWise Virtual**. This allows two physical switches to operate as a single logical entity.
*   **Default Gateway:** Use **VSX Active-Gateway**. This provides an active-active forwarding mechanism for the default gateway, eliminating the convergence delays associated with VRRP/HSRP.
*   **Access Layer:** Access switches will be deployed in **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacks.
*   **Physical Connectivity:** Each Access Stack is dual-homed to both Distribution switches via **LACP (Link Aggregation Control Protocol)** bundles (e.g., 2x10GbE or 2x25GbE), providing both increased bandwidth and sub-second failover.

---

### 4. Link Design & Performance

*   **Core to Distribution:** 40Gbps or 100Gbps fiber optics (QSFP28).
*   **Distribution to Access:** 10Gbps SFP+ fiber uplinks in a LAG bundle (20Gbps aggregate).
*   **Access to Endpoints:** 
    *   **Standard Users/VoIP:** 1Gbps Copper (Cat6a).
    *   **Wi-Fi 6/6E APs:** **Multi-Gigabit (2.5G/5G)** ports to support the high-density student population in CSE/AIML.
*   **QoS Markings:**
    *   **VoIP:** EF (Expedited Forwarding) / DSCP 46.
    *   **IPTV:** AF41 (Interactive Video) / DSCP 34.
    *   **Admin/Staff:** AF21 (Assured Forwarding).
    *   **Students:** Best Effort.

---

### 5. VLAN & Subnetting Plan

Traffic is segmented by function and floor to limit broadcast domains and enforce security via ACLs.

| VLAN ID | Name | Subnet (Example) | Sizing | QoS Priority | Access Control |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| 20 | Staff_Data | 10.1.20.0/23 | 510 | Medium | Restricted |
| 30 | Student_CSE | 10.1.30.0/22 | 1022 | Low | Internet Only |
| 40 | Student_AIML | 10.1.40.0/22 | 1022 | Low | Internet Only |

**Security Policy:** A strict Layer-3 ACL is applied at the Distribution/Core layer preventing any traffic from VLAN 30/40 (Students) to reach VLAN 10 (Admin) or VLAN 99 (Management).

---

### 6. Summary of Redundancy & Failover

| Component | Redundancy Mechanism | Failover Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gateway** | VSX Active-Gateway | Zero packet loss (Active-Active) |
| **Core/Dist** | Dual Chassis / VSX | Hardware failover in $< 50\text{ms}$ |
| **Access Uplinks** | LACP (Multi-chassis EtherChannel) | Sub-second convergence if one link fails |
| **Access Switches** | VSF Stacking | Single Management Point; member failure doesn't drop stack |
| **Power** | Dual Hot-Swappable PSUs | No downtime during PSU failure |

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the provided requirements and the detailed technical specifications of the HPE Aruba Networking CX portfolio, here is the network hardware design for the PJ Building.

### 1. Hardware Selection Logic

*   **Core/Distribution Layer:** The **CX 6400 Series** is selected. It is a modular chassis providing the required high-backplane capacity, redundancy (redundant management modules and PSUs), and **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** support for active-active gateway performance.
*   **Access Layer (Admin):** The **CX 6100 Series** is chosen for the Administration floor. It provides 10G SFP+ uplinks and sufficient PoE+ for VoIP phones and a few APs, offering a cost-effective solution where Multi-Gigabit is not required.
*   **Access Layer (CSE & AIML):** The **CX 6300M Series** is selected. These floors have high user density and require **Smart Rate (Multi-Gigabit)** ports (1/2.5/5GbE) to support Wi-Fi 6/6E APs and high-bandwidth academic labs. They support **VSF stacking** and provide the necessary PoE budget for dense AP deployments.

### 4. Redundancy & Performance Summary
*   **Core High Availability:** Two CX 6405 switches configured in **VSX**, providing an active-active gateway with sub-50ms failover.
*   **Access Resilience:** Floor 2 and 3 switches are grouped into **VSF stacks**, allowing a single management IP and simplified LACP bundling to the core.
*   **Throughput:** Distribution-to-Access links utilize 10GbE/25GbE SFP+ fiber, while the Core utilizes 50G/100G interconnects via SFP56/QSFP28 modules.
*   **Power:** All access switches selected provide IEEE 802.3at (PoE+) or 802.3bt (Class 6/8 PoE) to accommodate everything from basic VoIP phones to high-performance Wi-Fi 6E access points.
