## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed the following infrastructure for the **PJ Building**. This design follows the HPE Aruba/Cisco enterprise gold-standard hierarchical model, focusing on high availability, deterministic traffic flow, and strict segmentation.

---

### 1. Topology Overview
**Architecture:** Hierarchical 3-Tier Campus Design (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access).
**Topology Type:** Star-Wired Mesh (Physical) / Virtualized Chassis (Logical).
**Design Philosophy:** To minimize blast radius and latency, each floor is treated as a distinct access block, while the core provides a non-blocking backplane for inter-VLAN routing.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Physical Mapping

#### A. Core Layer (MDF - Ground Floor/Basement)
*   **Placement:** Main Distribution Frame (MDF).
*   **Function:** High-speed L3 routing, Inter-VLAN routing, and Perimeter Firewall handoff.
*   **Hardware Logic:** A pair of high-performance L3 switches configured in **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** to act as a single logical entity.

#### B. Distribution Layer (MDF/Building Level)
*   **Placement:** Co-located with Core in the MDF.
*   **Function:** Aggregates uplinks from all three floors.
*   **Mapping:** Each floor’s traffic terminates here.

#### C. Access Layer (IDFs - Per Floor)
*   **Placement:** Intermediate Distribution Frames (IDF) located in the utility closet of each floor.
*   **Mapping:**
    *   **Floor 1 (Admin):** 1x 48-port PoE+ Switch (Low density).
    *   **Floor 2 (CSE):** 3x 48-port PoE+ Switches (High density - 327 users + WAPs/VoIP).
    *   **Floor 3 (ECE):** 2x 48-port PoE+ Switches (Medium density - 236 users + WAPs/VoIP).

---

### 3. High Availability & Link Design

| Link Type | Technology | Speed | Redundancy Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution** | VSX / ISL | 40/100Gbps | Dual-homed links for zero-convergence time. |
| **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access** | LACP (LAG) | 2 x 10Gbps | Multi-chassis Link Aggregation (MLAG) / LACP. |
| **Access $\leftrightarrow$ Endpoint** | 802.3at (PoE+) | 1Gbps | Dual-homed for critical servers/APs (where applicable). |

*   **Virtualization:** Use **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** at the Core to eliminate STP (Spanning Tree) blocking ports and provide active-active forwarding.
*   **Failover:** **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol)** used for the default gateway of each VLAN to ensure seamless failover between core switches.

### 5. Quality of Service (QoS) & Security

Following HPE/Aruba networking standards, traffic is marked at the ingress (Access Layer) and honored throughout the core.

**QoS Marking Strategy:**
1.  **Platinum (EF / CoS 5):** VoIP Traffic $\rightarrow$ Strict Priority Queue (Low Latency).
2.  **Gold (AF41 / CoS 4):** Administrative & Management Traffic $\rightarrow$ Guaranteed Bandwidth.
3.  **Silver (AF21 / CoS 2):** Staff/Faculty Data $\rightarrow$ Weighted Round Robin (WRR).
4.  **Bronze (BE / CoS 0):** Student Data $\rightarrow$ Best Effort (Lowest Priority).

**Security Implementation:**
*   **Layer 2:** 802.1X Port-based authentication for all wired ports.
*   **Layer 3:** Access Control Lists (ACLs) on the Core to prevent "Student VLAN" from accessing "Admin VLAN" and "Management VLAN."
*   **Wireless:** WPA3-Enterprise with separate SSIDs mapped to the VLANs above.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Due to a technical limitation with the specific product search tool, I am unable to retrieve the granular SKU-level datasheet details at this moment. However, as a Network Hardware Specialist, I will apply the **standard HPE Aruba Networking CX architecture patterns** to satisfy your approved topology. 

The design requires **VSX at the Core** and **High-Density PoE+ at the Access Layer**, with a 3-tier hierarchical structure.

### Hardware Selection Logic

1.  **Core Layer (VSX):** The **Aruba CX 6400 Series** is the designated choice here. It is a modular chassis that natively supports **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**, providing the active-active redundancy and zero-convergence time required for your "important" uptime requirement.
2.  **Distribution Layer:** Since the distribution is co-located with the core in the MDF, I am utilizing the modular capacity of the CX 6400 chassis to handle distribution duties, reducing physical footprint and latency.
3.  **Access Layer (VSF):** 
    *   **Floor 2 & 3 (High/Med Density):** The **Aruba CX 6300M** is selected. It supports **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** for stacking (logical chassis) and provides high PoE+ budgets and 10G/25G uplinks to match your LACP requirements.
    *   **Floor 1 (Low Density):** The **Aruba CX 6200F** is selected. It is a cost-effective, fixed-configuration switch that still supports VSF and PoE+, ideal for the smaller Admin floor.
