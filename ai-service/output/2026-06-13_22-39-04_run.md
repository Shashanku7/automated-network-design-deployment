## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed the following infrastructure for BMS College of Engineering (BMSCE) based on the latest HPE Aruba Networking (AOS-CX) standards.

### 1. Topology Overview
**Chosen Model:** **3-Tier Architecture (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access)**

**Justification:** 
The campus exceeds 1,900 total endpoints across three distinct buildings and nine departments. A 3-tier model is mandatory here to isolate fault domains, provide dedicated distribution blocks for each building to manage high-density traffic (especially in the New Academic Block), and ensure the Data Center in Building 1 has a high-performance, low-latency path to the Core without impacting other academic traffic.

### 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Core Layer:** Deploy a pair of **Aruba CX 8360** switches using **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This provides a dual-control plane for hitless upgrades and active-active forwarding.
*   **Distribution Layer:** Each building will have a pair of **Aruba CX 6400** switches in a VSX pair.
*   **Access Layer:** Switches will be deployed in **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacks of up to 8 members to simplify management and provide local redundancy.
*   **Linkages:**
    *   **Core to Distribution:** $2 \times 25\text{GbE}$ (or $100\text{GbE}$) SMF links in a **VSX LAG** (Multi-Chassis Link Aggregation).
    *   **Distribution to Access:** $2 \times 10\text{GbE}$ SFP+ uplinks per stack using **LACP**.
    *   **Smart Rate (Multi-Gig):** In Building 2 (CSE/AI/DS), use **Aruba CX 6300M** switches with Smart Rate (2.5G/5G) ports to support Wi-Fi 6/6E APs, preventing the 1Gbps bottleneck.

### 5. Redundancy, Failover & Security
*   **Default Gateway:** Implement **VSX Active-Gateway**. This provides a virtual IP (VIP) and virtual MAC (vMAC) across the VSX pair, allowing active-active forwarding without the convergence delays or overhead of VRRP.
*   **Routing:** Use **OSPF (Area 0)** between the Core and Distribution layers for rapid convergence and ECMP (Equal-Cost Multi-Path) load balancing.
*   **Security:**
    *   **ACLs:** Applied at the Distribution layer to block `B2-Students` and `B3-Students` from accessing the `B1-Admin` (Data Center) subnet.
    *   **Wireless:** 802.1X authentication via Aruba ClearPass.
        *   *Campus-Staff:* WPA3-Enterprise (Full Access).
        *   *Campus-Student:* WPA3-Enterprise (Restricted Access).
        *   *Campus-Guest:* Captive Portal (Internet Only).
*   **Loop Protection:** Since VSX LAGs are used, Spanning Tree is disabled on those links; **Aruba Loop-Protect** is enabled on all access ports to prevent accidental loops from student-connected hubs.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the detailed site requirements and the technical specifications retrieved from the HPE Aruba Networking datasheets, here is the comprehensive hardware recommendation and Bill of Materials (BOM) for the BMS College of Engineering campus network.

### 1. Hardware Selection Strategy

*   **Core Layer:** The **Aruba CX 8360** is selected for the campus core. It supports **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for active-active redundancy and provides the high-density 25G/100G connectivity required to aggregate traffic from the three distribution blocks.
*   **Distribution Layer:** The **Aruba CX 6400** chassis is selected for each building. This provides a modular, high-availability platform that can scale as the college grows. It supports VSX for redundancy and provides the necessary 10G/25G uplinks to the core and 10G downlinks to the access stacks.
*   **Access Layer:** 
    *   **Standard Zones:** **Aruba CX 6200M** switches are used for general purpose connectivity. They support **VSF stacking** (up to 8 members) and provide a balance of performance and cost.
    *   **High-Density/Wireless Zones (Building 2):** **Aruba CX 6300M** switches with **Smart Rate (1/2.5/5GbE)** are deployed. This is critical for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs to prevent the 1Gbps bottleneck and support the high student density in the New Academic Block.
    *   **Low-Density/Specialty Zones:** **Aruba CX 6100** is used for very small segments or non-critical areas where stacking is not required.

### 3. Technical Design Summary

#### **Connectivity & Performance**
*   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** $2 \times 100\text{GbE}$ SMF links per building using **VSX LAG** for hitless failover.
*   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** $2 \times 10\text{GbE}$ SFP+ uplinks per VSF stack using **LACP**.
*   **Access $\leftrightarrow$ Endpoints:** 1GbE for desktops/VOIP; **Smart Rate (2.5G/5G)** for APs in Building 2.

#### **Logical Segmentation & Security**
*   **VLANs:** Segmented by user role (Student, Staff, Admin) and service (VOIP, IPTV, Mgmt).
*   **Routing:** **OSPF (Area 0)** is implemented between Core and Distribution for rapid convergence and ECMP load balancing.
*   **Gateway:** **VSX Active-Gateway** is used at the Distribution layer to provide a virtual IP/MAC, eliminating STP convergence delays for endpoints.
*   **ACLs:** Applied at the Distribution layer to enforce the "Student $\rightarrow$ Admin/DC" restriction.

#### **Power & Environmental**
*   **PoE Budget:** CX 6200M and 6300M provide Class 4 (30W) and Class 6 (60W) PoE respectively, ensuring sufficient power for VOIP phones and high-performance APs.
*   **UPS:** Mandatory UPS backup in the PG Block Data Center and all IDF closets to maintain connectivity during power fluctuations.
