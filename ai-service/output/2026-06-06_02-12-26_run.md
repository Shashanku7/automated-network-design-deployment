## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed the following high-availability campus network. This architecture utilizes a **Collapsed Core/Distribution model** utilizing HPE Aruba's VSX (Virtual Switching Extension) and VSF (Virtual Switching Framework) to ensure seamless failover and maximum throughput.

---

### 1. Topology Overview
The design follows a **Hierarchical Star Topology**. 
*   **Core/Distribution Layer:** A centralized redundant pair of chassis switches located in the Building 1 Server Room.
*   **Access Layer:** Building-specific distribution blocks with floor-level access switches.
*   **Logical Architecture:** A "Leaf-Spine" inspired approach where every access switch has redundant uplinks to the Core pair.

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Mapping

#### Building 1: Main Academic Block (Distribution Block 1)
*   **Floor 1 (Admin/Reception):** 1x 24-Port PoE+ Access Switch. (Low density, focused on Admin security).
*   **Floor 2 (Computer Science):** 3x 48-Port PoE+ Access Switches stacked via **VSF**. (High density: ~230 devices).
*   **Floor 3 (Electronics):** 2x 48-Port PoE+ Access Switches stacked via **VSF**. (Medium density: ~167 devices).

#### Building 2: Library & Research (Distribution Block 2)
*   **Floor 1 (Main Library):** 2x 48-Port PoE+ Access Switches stacked via **VSF**. (High density: ~213 devices).
*   **Floor 2 (Research Labs):** 1x 24-Port PoE+ Access Switch. (Low density).

---

### 3. High Availability & Link Design

*   **Core Redundancy (VSX):** The Core employs **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. Unlike traditional stacking, VSX allows for independent control planes, enabling In-Service Software Upgrades (ISSU) and eliminating the "split-brain" risk.
*   **Access Redundancy (VSF):** Floor switches are grouped into **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacks, appearing as a single logical entity to the core.
*   **Link Aggregation (LACP):** 
    *   **Access to Core:** Each VSF stack uses a **Multi-Chassis Link Aggregation (M-LAG)** bundle. Two 10GbE SFP+ fiber links per stack, distributed across the two core switches.
    *   **Inter-Building Link:** 2x 10GbE Fiber optic runs (LACP bundle) connecting Building 2 to the Building 1 Core.
*   **Failover:** **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol)** is configured on the Core for gateway redundancy.

### 5. QoS & Traffic Engineering (HPE Standard)

To ensure VOIP and IPTV stability, the following QoS policy is applied across all switches:

1.  **Voice (VLAN 100, 110):** 
    *   **Priority:** Strict Priority Queue.
    *   **Marking:** DSCP 46 (EF).
    *   **Behavior:** Guaranteed low latency; bypasses standard data queues.
2.  **Video/IPTV (VLAN 200, 210):** 
    *   **Priority:** Weighted Round Robin (WRR).
    *   **Marking:** DSCP 32 (CS4).
    *   **Optimization:** IGMP Snooping enabled on all access switches to prevent multicast flooding.
3.  **Administrative Data (Finance/Principal):** 
    *   **Marking:** DSCP 18 (AF21).
    *   **Behavior:** Higher bandwidth weight than student traffic during peak congestion.

---

### 6. Security & Access Control Logic

*   **Departmental Isolation:** L3 routing between VLANs is handled at the Core. Access Control Lists (ACLs) are applied to block `Student VLANs` from accessing `Admin VLANs`.
*   **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):** 
    *   **Principal/Finance:** Assigned to a specific "Management" subnet with MAC-address sticky binding and 802.1X authentication.
    *   **Research Zone:** Port-security is enabled on Building 2, Floor 2 switches to prevent unauthorized devices from plugging into the research lab network.
*   **Wireless Segmentation:** 
    *   **SSID-Student:** Maps to Student VLANs (L2 isolation enabled).
    *   **SSID-Staff:** Maps to Staff VLANs (WPA3-Enterprise).
    *   **SSID-Admin:** Hidden SSID, mapped to Admin VLAN with MAC filtering.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

As a Network Hardware Specialist, I have translated the approved topology into a detailed Bill of Materials. 

### Hardware Selection Logic
1.  **Core/Distribution Layer:** To meet the **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** requirement for high availability and independent control planes, the **Aruba CX 6400 series** is selected. This chassis-based system allows for the required redundancy and high-density 10G/25G uplinks.
2.  **Access Layer:** The **Aruba CX 6300M series** is chosen for the access layer. Unlike the 6100 or 6200, the 6300M supports **VSF stacking** and provides the high-performance 10G/50G uplinks necessary to connect back to the VSX core.
3.  **Wi-Fi Calculation:** Based on the requirement of 25-30 users per AP:
    *   B1 F1: 38 users $\approx$ 2 APs
    *   B1 F2: 222 users $\approx$ 8 APs
    *   B1 F3: 163 users $\approx$ 6 APs
