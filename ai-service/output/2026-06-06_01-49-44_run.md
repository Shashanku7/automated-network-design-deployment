## Phase 2: Network Topology

This design follows a **Hierarchical Modular Block Architecture**, utilizing an HPE Aruba-style framework (VSX/VSF) to ensure maximum uptime and deterministic traffic patterns for the BMS College of Engineering.

---

### 1. Topology Overview
The network utilizes a **Collapsed Core/Distribution** model feeding into a **Floor-based Access Layer**.
*   **Core/Distribution Layer:** Located in the MDF (Building 1, Floor 1). Two high-performance chassis switches operating in a virtual cluster.
*   **Access Layer:** Located in IDFs on each of the 7 floors. Switches are stacked using VSF (Virtual Switching Framework) to provide a single logical entity per floor.
*   **Physical Topology:** A "Hub-and-Spoke" fiber star topology where every floor IDF has redundant uplinks to both Core switches.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Switch Sizing

#### Core/Distribution Block (MDF - Floor 1)
*   **Hardware:** 2x Enterprise Core Switches (e.g., Aruba 6400 series).
*   **Logic:** Acting as the L3 Gateway for all VLANs. Implements **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for active-active forwarding and state synchronization.

#### Access Layer (IDFs - Per Floor)
Each floor is treated as a separate access block. Switches are sized for 120% capacity to accommodate the 20% growth constraint.

### 3. High Availability & Link Design

*   **Core Redundancy:** **VSX** is deployed at the core to eliminate Spanning Tree (STP) blocked ports, allowing all uplinks to be active.
*   **Access Redundancy:** **VSF** (Virtual Switching Framework) stacks the switches on each floor. If one member of the stack fails, the others maintain connectivity.
*   **Link Aggregation:** 
    *   **Uplinks:** Each floor IDF is connected to the Core via a **Multi-Chassis LAG (LACP)**.
    *   **Speeds:** 2x 10GbE or 25GbE SFP+ fiber runs per stack to the Core.
*   **Failure Domain:** By isolating each floor into its own VSF stack and VLAN subnet, a broadcast storm on Floor 4 (CS) will not impact Floor 1 (Admin).

---

*   **Low Density:** Assigned `/24` (254 hosts).

---

### 5. QoS and Traffic Management

To ensure VOIP and IPTV stability, the following **HPE/Aruba QoS mappings** are implemented:

1.  **Strict Priority Queue:** All traffic on **VLAN 300 (VOIP)** is mapped to the Priority Queue (PQ) to ensure zero jitter.
2.  **Assured Forwarding:** **VLAN 400 (IPTV)** is assigned to a high-bandwidth queue using Weighted Round Robin (WRR) to prevent pixelation.
3.  **Rate Limiting:** Student VLANs (120-170) are rate-limited at the access port level to prevent a single user from consuming the entire floor's 10G uplink.
4.  **Multicast:** IGMP Snooping is enabled on VLAN 400 to prevent IPTV streams from flooding the entire network.

---

### 6. Redundancy & Failover Summary

*   **L2 Redundancy:** **LACP (802.3ad)** ensures that if one fiber strand is cut, traffic fails over instantly to the second strand in the bundle.
*   **L3 Redundancy:** **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol)** or **VSX Virtual Gateway** is used. The Core switches present a single Virtual IP (VIP) as the default gateway for all VLANs.
*   **Security Isolation:**
    *   **ACLs:** Applied at the Core for VLAN 110 (Admin), restricting access to only authorized Staff VLANs.
    *   **Port Security:** MAC-limiting on student ports to prevent unauthorized switch attachments.
*   **Wireless Strategy:** For Floors 4-7, a **High-Density (HD) AP deployment** is required. APs will be placed every 15-20 meters in a honeycomb pattern, utilizing 5GHz/6GHz bands exclusively to minimize co-channel interference.
