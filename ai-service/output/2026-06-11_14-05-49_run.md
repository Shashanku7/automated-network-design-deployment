## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Selected Model:** 3-Tier Architecture (Core, Distribution, Access)

**Justification:** 
With a total endpoint count exceeding 650 (students, staff, and devices) spread across two distinct buildings and five floors, a 3-tier model is required to ensure high performance and scalability. This architecture provides a dedicated distribution layer to aggregate building-specific traffic, reducing the processing load on the core and allowing for granular policy enforcement and failure domain isolation between the Academic Block and the Library/Research center.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Port Mapping
Each building is assigned a dedicated **Distribution Block** consisting of a pair of switches in a VSX cluster.

#### **Building 1: Main Academic Block**
*   **Floor 1 (Admin & Reception):** 
    *   Endpoints: 20 (Stu) + 15 (Stf) + 3 (Adm) + 6 (VoIP) + 2 (IPTV) + 2 (APs) = 48
    *   Required Ports: $48 \times 1.2 = 57.6$ $\rightarrow$ **Two 48-port switches (or one 48-port + one 24-port)**.
*   **Floor 2 (Computer Science):** 
    *   Endpoints: 200 (Stu) + 20 (Stf) + 2 (Adm) + 5 (VoIP) + 2 (IPTV) + 9 (APs) = 238
    *   Required Ports: $238 \times 1.2 = 285.6$ $\rightarrow$ **Six 48-port switches**.
*   **Floor 3 (Electronics):** 
    *   Endpoints: 150 (Stu) + 12 (Stf) + 1 (Adm) + 4 (VoIP) + 1 (IPTV) + 7 (APs) = 175
    *   Required Ports: $175 \times 1.2 = 210$ $\rightarrow$ **Five 48-port switches**.

#### **Building 2: Library & Research**
*   **Floor 1 (Main Library):** 
    *   Endpoints: 200 (Stu) + 10 (Stf) + 1 (Adm) + 2 (VoIP) + 1 (IPTV) + 9 (APs) = 223
    *   Required Ports: $223 \times 1.2 = 267.6$ $\rightarrow$ **Six 48-port switches**.
*   **Floor 2 (Research Labs):** 
    *   Endpoints: 50 (Stu) + 8 (Stf) + 1 (Adm) + 1 (VoIP) + 0 (IPTV) + 3 (APs) = 63
    *   Required Ports: $63 \times 1.2 = 75.6$ $\rightarrow$ **Two 48-port switches**.

---

### 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Core Layer:** Deploy **HPE Aruba CX 8360** (or similar) switches in a **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** pair. This ensures a loop-free topology without relying on Spanning Tree (STP) for convergence.
*   **Distribution Layer:** Each building uses a VSX pair of **Aruba CX 6400** chassis. Access switches connect to both Distribution switches using **LACP (Link Aggregation Control Protocol)** bundles for multi-chassis etherchannel (MEC).
*   **Access Layer:** **Aruba CX 6200/6300M** switches. For the Computer Science and Library areas, **Smart Rate (Multi-Gigabit)** ports are mandatory to support Wi-Fi 6/6E APs (requiring >1Gbps backhaul).
*   **Backbone:** 
    *   Access to Distribution: 10GbE/25GbE Fiber (SFP+).
    *   Distribution to Core: 40GbE/100GbE Fiber (QSFP+).
*   **QoS Policy:** 
    *   **Platinum (EF):** VoIP Traffic (Strict Priority).
    *   **Gold (AF41):** IPTV/Video Streaming.
    *   **Silver (BE):** General Student/Staff Data.

### 5. Redundancy & Security Implementation
*   **Default Gateway:** Use **VSX Active-Gateway**. This allows both VSX peer switches to act as the active gateway for a subnet simultaneously, eliminating the slow convergence of VRRP and preventing "tromboning" of traffic.
*   **Segmentation:** 
    *   **ACLs:** Strict Layer 3 ACLs implemented at the Distribution layer to prevent `Student_Data` VLAN from accessing `Admin_Data` or `Management` VLANs.
    *   **Port Security:** Enabled on all Access ports in the Research Labs and Administration offices to prevent unauthorized device bridging.
    *   **Research Isolation:** The Research Labs in Building 2 will be placed in a separate VRF (Virtual Routing and Forwarding) instance if total traffic isolation from the Academic block is required.
*   **Failover:** Redundant fiber paths (Dual-homing) from every Access switch to the VSX Distribution pair. If one fiber link or one distribution switch fails, LACP ensures sub-second failover to the redundant path.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the approved topology and the detailed technical specifications from the HPE Aruba Networking datasheets, here is the finalized Bill of Materials (BOM) for the campus network.

### Design Analysis & Justification
*   **Core Layer:** The **CX 8360 v2** is selected for the core. It provides the necessary 4.8 Tbps switching capacity and **VSX** support for a loop-free, highly available core. It supports 100GbE uplinks to the distribution layer.
*   **Distribution Layer:** The **CX 6400** chassis is used to provide a modular, high-density aggregation point. A VSX pair per building ensures that the failure of one chassis does not disrupt connectivity.
*   **Access Layer:** 
    *   **High-Density/Smart Rate Zones:** The **CX 6300M** (specifically Smart Rate models) is deployed in the Computer Science and Library areas. This is mandatory to support Wi-Fi 6/6E APs and high-performance lab equipment via multi-gigabit (1/2.5/5GbE) ports.
    *   **General Zones:** The **CX 6200M** (48-port PoE) is used for administration and electronics departments, providing cost-effective 1GbE connectivity with a robust PoE budget.
*   **Port Calculations:** All floor counts include the 20% growth margin and calculated AP counts (1 AP per ~25-30 users), ensuring physical port density mathematically exceeds the requirements.

### Summary of Key Technical Implementations
*   **Symmetry:** All access switches are dual-homed via LACP to the Distribution VSX pair.
*   **PoE Strategy:** Using Class 4 (30W) for phones/IPTV and Class 8 (up to 90W) via CX 6300M for high-performance APs.
*   **Backbone:** 10/25GbE SFP+ from Access $\rightarrow$ Distribution; 100GbE QSFP28 from Distribution $\rightarrow$ Core.
*   **Security:** Dynamic Segmentation (Colorless Ports) and L3 ACLs implemented at the Distribution layer to isolate Student, Staff, and Admin VLANs.
