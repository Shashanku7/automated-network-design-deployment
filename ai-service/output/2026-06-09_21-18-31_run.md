## Phase 1: Rephrased Prompt

**Project Title:** Comprehensive Campus Network Topology Design for PJ Building

**Objective:**
Design a scalable, high-availability campus network architecture for a single-building academic environment. The design must support a diverse user base including students, faculty, and administrators, while integrating specialized services such as VoIP and IPTV. The infrastructure must be engineered to support current loads and a projected growth of 200 additional users.

The network must be designed based on the following precise spatial distribution to determine switch placement, cabling runs, and VLAN segmentation:

**2. Performance & Technical Requirements:**
*   **Scalability:** The design must account for a growth margin of 200 additional users (approx. 33% increase). Ensure port density on access switches and DHCP pool sizes are configured for this headroom.
*   **Uptime & Availability:** High uptime is a critical requirement. Implement redundancy at the core/distribution layer (e.g., stacking or MLAG) and utilize redundant uplinks from access switches to the core.
*   **Quality of Service (QoS):** Implement strict QoS policies to prioritize VoIP traffic (low latency/jitter) and IPTV streams (multicast management) over general student data traffic.
*   **VLAN Segmentation:** Create logically isolated broadcast domains for:
    *   Students/Visitors (Guest access)
    *   Faculty/Staff (Academic access)
    *   Administration (Secure management)
    *   Voice (VoIP)
    *   Video (IPTV)
    *   Network Management

**3. Physical & Security Constraints:**
*   **Physical Topology:** Recommend a hierarchical Three-Tier (Core, Distribution, Access) or Two-Tier (Collapsed Core) architecture based on the building's 3-floor layout.
*   **Connectivity:** Assume Cat6a cabling for horizontal runs to support 1Gbps to the desk and 10Gbps fiber backbones between floors.
*   **Security:** Implement Port Security on access switches, 802.1X authentication for staff/admin devices, and a robust firewall at the edge to segment the campus network from the external internet.

**4. Budgetary & Hardware Assumptions:**
*   **Budget:** Balanced approach—prioritize enterprise-grade reliability for the core and high-density, cost-effective PoE+ switches for the access layer to support VoIP phones and Wireless Access Points (WAPs).
*   **Wireless:** Design a high-density Wi-Fi 6 deployment strategy, specifically focusing on the 2nd and 3rd floors (CSE and AIML) to handle the high concentration of student devices.

*   An IP addressing scheme and VLAN assignment table.
*   A hardware bill of materials (BOM) suggesting switch types (Core vs. Access) and port counts per floor.

## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview & Justification
**Selected Model:** 2-Tier (Collapsed Core + Access)
**Justification:** The project is constrained to a single building (PJ Building) with a total of 691 endpoints. A 3-tier model would introduce unnecessary latency and hardware costs for a single-site deployment. A Collapsed Core architecture provides the optimal balance of high performance and simplicity, consolidating distribution and core functions into a single high-availability pair.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Port Calculations
To ensure a 20% growth margin and account for Wireless Access Points (1 AP per 25 users), the port requirements are calculated as follows:

| Floor | Dept | Users (S+St+A) | VoIP | IPTV | APs (est.) | Total Endpoints | Req. Ports (Total * 1.2) | Switch Selection |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | Ground | 32 | 5 | 2 | 2 | 41 | 50 | **2 x 48-port PoE+** |
| **2** | CSE | 377 | 5 | 1 | 16 | 399 | 479 | **10 x 48-port PoE+** |
| **3** | AIML | 269 | 3 | 0 | 11 | 283 | 340 | **8 x 48-port PoE+** |

*   **Access Layer:** Distributed across 3 IDF (Intermediate Distribution Frame) closets, one per floor.
*   **Collapsed Core:** Located in the Ground Floor MDF (Main Distribution Frame).

---

### 3. High Availability & Redundancy
*   **Core Layer:** Deployment of two chassis-class switches using **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** to create a logical unit.
*   **Default Gateway:** **VSX Active-Gateway** will be utilized. This provides a single virtual IP/MAC for the gateways across both core switches, ensuring seamless failover without the convergence delays associated with VRRP.
*   **Access Layer:** Access switches will be deployed in **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacks. This allows multiple physical switches to be managed as one and enables Multi-Chassis Link Aggregation.
*   **Link Aggregation:** All access stacks will connect to the Collapsed Core via **LACP (802.3ad) bundles** (Minimum 2x10GbE links per stack).

---

### 4. Link Design & Performance
*   **Backbone:** 10Gbps SFP+ Fiber uplinks from each floor's VSF stack to both Core switches.
*   **Horizontal Cabling:** Cat6a (1Gbps to the desk).
*   **Smart Rate (Multi-Gigabit):** Due to the high density of students in the CSE and AIML floors, **Multi-Gigabit (2.5G/5G) ports** are required for the Wi-Fi 6/6E APs to prevent the 1Gbps uplink from becoming a bottleneck for high-concurrency wireless traffic.
*   **QoS Strategy:** 
    *   **Strict Priority Queue:** VoIP (DSCP EF / CoS 5).
    *   **Assured Forwarding:** IPTV Multicast (DSCP AF41 / CoS 4).
    *   **Best Effort:** Student/Guest data.

---

### 6. Redundancy & Failover Logic
*   **Core Failover:** If Core-1 fails, VSX ensures Core-2 continues to forward traffic with zero downtime for the Active-Gateway.
*   **Access Failover:** Access switches are dual-homed to both Core switches via LACP. If one uplink or one core switch fails, the LACP hash redistributes traffic to the remaining link instantly.
*   **Loop Prevention:** MSTP (Multiple Spanning Tree Protocol) will be configured as a secondary fail-safe, though VSX/VSF eliminates most loops in the physical topology.
*   **Security:** 802.1X is enforced on VLAN 10 and 20; MAC-filtering and Port Security (max 2 MACs per port) are enforced on student ports in VLAN 30 and 40.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the provided requirements and the retrieved HPE Aruba Networking datasheets, here is the detailed network design and Bill of Materials.

### 1. Design Logic & Justification

**Collapsed Core Layer (MDF - Ground Floor):**
*   **Selected Model:** **CX 6400 Series**. Specifically, the **6405 v2 Chassis (R0X26C)**.
*   **Justification:** The requirements specify **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for the core to ensure high availability and zero-downtime failover. The CX 6400 is a modular chassis designed for this role, supporting redundant management modules and high-density 10G/25G/100G uplinks.
*   **Redundancy:** Two chassis will be deployed in a VSX pair to provide the Active-Gateway and chassis-level redundancy.

**Access Layer (IDFs - All Floors):**
*   **Selected Model (High Density/Smart Rate):** **CX 6300M Series**.
*   **Justification:** The topology requires **Smart Rate (Multi-Gigabit)** ports for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs to prevent bottlenecks and **VSF stacking** for simplified management. 
    *   **CSE & AIML Floors:** These floors have high student density and require Multi-Gigabit ports for APs. The **6300M 48p Smart Rate (JL 659A)** is selected as it provides 1/2.5/5GbE ports and supports VSF.
    *   **Ground Floor:** Low density, but requires PoE for phones and IPTV. The **6300M 48p 1GbE Class 4 PoE (JL 661A)** is selected for cost-effectiveness while maintaining VSF compatibility and high availability.
