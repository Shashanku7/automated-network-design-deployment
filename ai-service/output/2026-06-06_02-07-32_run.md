## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed a **Hierarchical Three-Tier Campus Architecture** based on your specific device density and departmental requirements. This design leverages HPE Aruba networking principles, emphasizing high availability and strict traffic segmentation.

---

### 1. Topology Overview
The network follows a **Collapsed Core/Distribution and Access layer** model. 
*   **Topology Type:** Star-Bus Hybrid (Hierarchical).
*   **Core/Distribution:** A centralized high-capacity pair of switches acting as the "Brain" of the campus.
*   **Access Layer:** Floor-specific switches providing PoE+ power to endpoints.
*   **Logical Flow:** End Device $\rightarrow$ Access Switch $\rightarrow$ Distribution Block $\rightarrow$ Core $\rightarrow$ Firewall $\rightarrow$ WAN.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Sizing

#### A. Core & Distribution Layer (The Campus Hub)
Located in the Building 1 Server Room.
*   **Hardware:** 2x High-performance Chassis Switches (e.g., Aruba 6400 series).
*   **Logic:** These operate as a single logical entity via **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** to ensure sub-second failover and eliminate Spanning Tree (STP) convergence delays.

#### B. Access Layer (The Floor Blocks)
Each floor is treated as a separate broadcast domain with dedicated switches.

| Building | Floor | Primary User Load | Recommended Switch Spec | PoE Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bld 1** | 1 (Admin) | 46 Devices | 48-Port PoE+ L2/L3 | Medium (VOIP/WAP) |
| **Bld 1** | 2 (CS Dept) | 229 Devices | 3x 48-Port PoE+ Stack (VSF) | High (Students/WAPs) |
| **Bld 1** | 3 (Electronics)| 168 Devices | 2x 48-Port PoE+ Stack (VSF) | High (Students/WAPs) |
| **Bld 2** | 1 (Library) | 213 Devices | 3x 48-Port PoE+ Stack (VSF) | High (Students/WAPs) |
| **Bld 2** | 2 (Research) | 60 Devices | 48-Port PoE+ L2/L3 | Medium (Lab gear) |

---

### 3. High Availability & Link Design

*   **Core Redundancy:** **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** is utilized. Unlike traditional stacking, VSX allows for independent control planes, ensuring that a firmware update or crash on one switch does not bring down the entire core.
*   **Access Redundancy:** **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** is used for floor stacks, creating a single management IP for multiple physical switches.
*   **Link Aggregation:** 
    *   **Access to Distribution:** Each floor stack is connected via **LACP (802.3ad) Multi-Chassis Link Aggregation (MC-LAG)**. Two 10Gbps SFP+ fiber uplinks per stack, split between the two Core/Distribution switches.
    *   **Inter-Building:** 2x 10Gbps Fiber links in an Active-Active LAG bundle.
*   **Failover:** **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol)** is configured at the Distribution layer to provide a virtual gateway for all VLANs.

---

### 4. VLAN & Subnetting Plan
To ensure security and performance, the network is segmented by **Role** and **Function** rather than just location. This prevents a student in the Library from accessing the Finance server in Building 1.

**Global QoS Marking (HPE/Aruba Standard):**
*   **Voice:** DSCP EF (Expedited Forwarding) / CoS 5
*   **IPTV/Video:** DSCP AF41 / CoS 4
*   **Management:** DSCP CS2 / CoS 2
*   **Data:** Default / CoS 0

### 5. Security & Access Control Strategy

*   **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):** 
    *   **Principal/Finance Head:** Assigned to **VLAN 10**. Strict ACLs (Access Control Lists) are applied at the Distribution layer to allow this VLAN access to the "Secure Server Zone" while blocking all Student/Staff VLANs from reaching it.
    *   **Research Labs:** A dedicated ACL is placed on the B2-Floor 2 switch to prevent Research devices from initiating connections to the Admin VLAN.
*   **Wireless Strategy:**
    *   **WAP Placement:** High-density WiFi 6 APs are deployed in a "honeycomb" pattern. 
    *   **B1-Floor 2 (CS Dept) & B2-Floor 1 (Library):** Deploy 1 AP per 25-30 users. These APs will utilize **Band Steering** to push devices to 5GHz/6GHz and **AirMatch** for automatic channel optimization to avoid interference in high-density areas.
    *   **SSIDs:** 
        *   `Campus_Secure` (WPA3-Enterprise, 802.1X mapped to Staff/Admin VLANs).
        *   `Campus_Student` (Captive Portal, mapped to Student VLANs).
        *   `Campus_Guest` (Isolated, Internet-only, rate-limited).

### 6. Summary of Redundancy Flow
`End Device` $\xrightarrow{Cat6a}$ `VSF Stack (Access)` $\xrightarrow{LACP/10G}$ `VSX Pair (Core/Dist)` $\xrightarrow{VRRP}$ `Firewall` $\rightarrow$ `Internet`

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

### Hardware Selection Logic:
*   **Core/Distribution:** The **Aruba 6405 v2 (R0X26C)** is selected for its chassis-based high availability and VSX support, providing a robust "brain" for the campus.
*   **Access Layer:** The **Aruba 6300M (JL661A)** is the primary choice for high-density floors due to its VSF stacking capabilities and PoE+ support. For lower-density areas (B1 Floor 1 and B2 Floor 2), the **Aruba 6200M (R8Q68A/R8Q70A)** is used to maintain cost-effectiveness while providing the required performance and stacking support.
*   **Wi-Fi APs:** Based on the 25-30 users per AP rule, high-density areas (CS Dept, Library) are allocated more units. WiFi 6 APs are assumed to match the high-performance switch infrastructure.

3.  **Power:** All access switches are **PoE+ (Class 4)** to support the VOIP phones, IPTV devices, and WiFi APs without needing separate power injectors.
