## User Prompt

Design a campus network for an organization with 2 building(s), 4 floor(s) per building. There are approximately 5000 students/visitors, 100 staff/faculty, and 5 administrators. Devices needed: laptops, printers, phones, cameras, wifi. Sensitive areas requiring extra security: finance, server, library. Special roles: Principal, Exam Controller. Uptime requirement: important.

## Phase 1: Rephrased Prompt

**Role:** Senior Network Infrastructure Architect
**Task:** Design a comprehensive, high-availability Campus Network Topology for a dual-building educational institution.

### 1. Project Purpose and Scope
Design a scalable, redundant, and secure campus-wide local area network (LAN) and wireless local area network (WLAN) to support academic operations, administrative functions, and public access across two buildings, each consisting of four floors.

### 2. User and Device Specifications
**User Base:**
*   **Students/Visitors:** ~5,000 (High-density wireless requirement)
*   **Staff/Faculty:** ~100 (Mixed wired/wireless)
*   **Administrators:** 5 (Priority wired connectivity)

**Device Inventory:**
*   **End-points:** Laptops, Tablets, Smartphones.
*   **Peripherals:** Network printers, VoIP phones.
*   **Infrastructure:** IP Surveillance Cameras (CCTV), Wireless Access Points (WAPs).

### 3. Performance and Availability Requirements
*   **Uptime:** High Availability (HA) is critical. Implement redundancy at the Core and Distribution layers to eliminate single points of failure.
*   **Throughput:** Design for high-density traffic, ensuring low latency for VoIP and seamless roaming for mobile users across floors and buildings.
*   **Scalability:** The architecture must allow for easy expansion of device counts without requiring a forklift upgrade of the core.

### 4. Physical and Logical Constraints
*   **Layout:** 2 Buildings $\rightarrow$ 4 Floors per building.
*   **Topology:** Propose a hierarchical design (Core, Distribution, Access layers).
*   **Cabling:** Specify fiber optic backbones between buildings and between the main distribution frame (MDF) and intermediate distribution frames (IDF) on each floor.

### 5. Security and Segmentation
Implement a robust VLAN strategy to isolate traffic based on role and sensitivity:
*   **High-Security Zones (Strict ACLs/Firewalling):** 
    *   Finance Department
    *   Server Room/Data Center
    *   Library (Digital Assets/Databases)
*   **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):** Create privileged access levels for the **Principal** and **Exam Controller** to ensure access to restricted administrative and examination data.
*   **Public Access:** A dedicated, isolated Guest WiFi network for visitors.

### 6. Budget and Implementation Assumptions
*   **Budget:** Mid-to-High range (Enterprise-grade hardware).
*   **Assumptions:** 
    *   Each floor will have at least one IDF closet.
    *   Power over Ethernet (PoE+) is required for cameras, phones, and WAPs.
    *   A centralized firewall/edge router will handle WAN connectivity.

### 7. Expected Deliverables
*   **Logical Topology Diagram:** Showing VLANs, IP addressing scheme, and routing.
*   **Physical Topology Diagram:** Showing cable runs, switch placements, and building interconnects.
*   **Hardware Recommendation List:** Suggested specifications for Core Switches, Distribution Switches, Access Switches, and WAPs.
*   **Security Matrix:** Mapping of user roles to permitted network segments.

## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Type:** Hierarchical Three-Tier Tree (Core, Distribution, Access)
**Architecture:** Collapsed Core/Distribution model per building with a redundant Inter-Building Core. The design utilizes a "Leaf-Spine" inspired approach at the edge to ensure non-blocking performance and rapid convergence.

### 2. Layer Breakdown
| Layer | Device Role | Count | Specifications |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core** | Campus Core (Bldg A) | 2 | High-backplane Chassis Switches (Virtual Switching Framework/VSX) |
| **Distribution**| Building Aggregation | 4 | 2 per Building (VSX Pair), L3 Routing to Access |
| **Access** | Floor IDF Switches | 8 | 1 per Floor (PoE+), L2/L3 Lite |

### 3. High-Availability & Performance
*   **Virtualization:** **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** at the Core and Distribution layers to provide a single logical entity with active-active forwarding.
*   **Link Aggregation:** **LACP (802.3ad)** used for all uplinks. Access switches are dual-homed to Distribution switches using Multi-Chassis Link Aggregation (MC-LAG).
*   **QoS Framework:** 
    *   **Strict Priority (SP):** VoIP and Video Conferencing.
    *   **Weighted Round Robin (WRR):** Academic Data and Administrative traffic.
    *   **Best Effort:** Guest WiFi.
*   **Spanning Tree:** MSTP (Multiple Spanning Tree Protocol) to prevent loops while allowing VLAN-based load balancing.

### 4. Link Design
| Connection | Media | Speed | Configuration | Redundancy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Core** | OS2 Single-Mode | 100Gbps | 2x100G LAG | Dual-Physical Path |
| **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Dist** | OS2 Single-Mode | 40Gbps | 2x40G LAG | Dual-Homed |
| **Dist $\leftrightarrow$ Access**| OM4 Multi-Mode | 10Gbps | 2x10G LAG | Dual-Homed (VSX) |
| **Access $\leftrightarrow$ End** | Cat6a | 1Gbps | Single Link | PoE+ (802.3at) |

### 5. VLAN & Subnet Plan
| VLAN | Segment Name | Subnet | Purpose | QoS Mark | Access Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 10 | Management | 10.0.10.0/24 | Switch/WAP Mgmt | CS3 | Admin Only |
| 20 | Admin-High | 10.0.20.0/24 | Finance/Principal | CS4 | Restricted |
| 30 | Staff-Faculty | 10.0.30.0/23 | Staff Laptops/PCs | AF21 | Internal |
| 40 | Student-WiFi | 10.0.40.0/20 | High-Density Wireless | AF11 | Internal/Isolated |
| 50 | Guest-WiFi | 172.16.0.0/20 | Visitors/Public | BE | Isolated/DMZ |
| 60 | VoIP-Phones | 10.0.60.0/23 | Voice Traffic | EF | Priority |
| 70 | CCTV-Security | 10.0.70.0/23 | IP Cameras/NVR | AF31 | Restricted |
| 80 | Server-Farm | 10.0.80.0/24 | Library/Data Center | CS5 | Restricted |

### 6. Redundancy & Failover
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol)** used at the Distribution layer to provide a virtual IP for default gateways.
*   **Control Plane:** VSX synchronizes MAC/ARP tables across the pair, ensuring sub-second failover without STP reconvergence.
*   **Physical Path:** Fiber runs are diverged (East/West paths) between Building A and Building B to prevent total outage from a single cable cut.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the approved topology and the retrieved datasheets from the HPE Aruba Networking CX portfolio, here is the detailed Bill of Materials (BoM).

### 1. Topology Analysis & Device Matching
*   **Core Layer:** The requirement for a high-backplane chassis supporting VSX and 100Gbps is met by the **CX 6400 Switch Series**. The 6405 v2 chassis provides the modularity and scalability needed for a campus core.
*   **Distribution Layer:** The requirement for L3 Routing, VSX, and 40Gbps uplinks is matched by the **CX 6300M Series**. These switches support VSX clusters for high availability and provide high-speed SFP56 ports (up to 50G) for core connectivity.
*   **Access Layer:** The requirement for PoE+ (802.3at), L2/L3 Lite, and 10Gbps uplinks is met by the **CX 6200F Series**. Specifically, the 48-port PoE models provide the necessary density for WAPs, VoIP phones, and CCTV.

### 2. Bill of Materials (BoM)

| Role | Model & SKU | Key Specifications | Qty | Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :---: | :--- |
| **Campus Core** | Aruba CX 6405 v2 Chassis (**R0X26C**) | Modular Chassis, 14 Tbps Switching Capacity, VSX Support | 2 | Provides the high-backplane capacity and redundancy required for the building inter-connect. |
| **Bldg Aggregation** | Aruba CX 6300M 24p SFP+ (**JL658A**) | 24x 1/10G SFP+ ports, 4x SFP56 (up to 50G) uplinks, VSX Support | 4 | 2 per building in VSX pair. High-density SFP+ for fiber aggregation from IDFs; 50G uplinks to core. |
| **Floor IDF Switch** | Aruba CX 6200F 48G Class 4 PoE (**S0M85A**) | 48x 1G PoE+ (30W), 4x 1/10G SFP+ uplinks, 740W PoE Budget | 8 | 1 per floor. PoE+ for CCTV, VoIP, and WAPs; 10G uplinks to Distribution for non-blocking performance. |
| **Core Transceivers** | Aruba 100G QSFP28 SR4 (**Optional**) | 100Gbps Short Range / SMF as per link design | 4 | Required for the 2x100G LAG between Core switches. |
| **Dist $\to$ Core Opt** | Aruba 40G/50G QSFP+ (**Optional**) | 40G/50G SFP56 Transceivers | 8 | Required for 2x40G LAG between Distribution and Core. |
| **Access $\to$ Dist Opt**| Aruba 10G SFP+ LC SR (**J9150D**) | 10G SFP+ LC 300m MMF | 16 | Required for 2x10G LACP uplinks from each IDF to Dist pair. |
