## Phase 2: Network Topology

This network architecture is designed as a **Hierarchical Three-Tier Model (Core, Distribution, Access)**, leveraging HPE ArubaOS-CX principles to ensure high availability and deterministic performance.

---

### 1. Topology Overview
*   **Topology Type:** Collapsed Core/Distribution Hybrid (Star-Bus)
*   **Architecture:** Modular Block Design. Each building acts as a distribution block, with floors acting as access pods.
*   **Physical Layout:** 
    *   **MDF (Main Distribution Frame):** Located in Building 1 (Core).
    *   **IDF (Intermediate Distribution Frame):** Located on every floor of both buildings.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Mapping

#### A. Core Layer (MDF - Building 1)
*   **Function:** High-speed packet switching and L3 routing.
*   **Configuration:** A pair of high-performance chassis switches (e.g., Aruba 8300 series) configured in **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for a loop-free, active-active control plane.

#### B. Distribution Layer (Building-Level)
*   **Building 1 Distribution:** Integrated into the Core (Collapsed).
*   **Building 2 Distribution:** A dedicated pair of L3 switches (e.g., Aruba 6300M) configured in **VSX**, connected to the Core via a 2x10GbE LACP Fiber Bundle.

#### C. Access Layer (Floor-Level)
Switches are sized based on port density (Students + Staff + Admin + VoIP + IPTV + APs + Cameras $\approx$ Total Ports).

| Location | User Count | Switch Sizing | Recommended Port Density |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **B1 - Floor 1** | 46 | 48-Port PoE+ | 1x 48-port switch |
| **B1 - Floor 2** | 229 | 48-Port PoE+ | 5x 48-port switches (VSF Stack) |
| **B1 - Floor 3** | 168 | 48-Port PoE+ | 4x 48-port switches (VSF Stack) |
| **B2 - Floor 1** | 214 | 48-Port PoE+ | 5x 48-port switches (VSF Stack) |
| **B2 - Floor 2** | 60 | 48-Port PoE+ | 2x 48-port switches (VSF Stack) |

---

### 3. High Availability & Redundancy
*   **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension):** Used at the Core and Building 2 Distribution. This allows for seamless failover and keeps the data plane active during software updates.
*   **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework):** Used at the Access layer to stack switches on each floor, treating a stack as a single logical entity to simplify management and provide link redundancy.
*   **LACP (Link Aggregation):** All uplinks from Access $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Core use Multi-Chassis Link Aggregation (MC-LAG) via LACP to ensure that if one fiber link or one switch fails, traffic shifts instantly without STP convergence delays.
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol) is implemented for the default gateway of each VLAN to ensure seamless failover between distribution switches.

---

### 4. Link Design & Physical Layer
*   **Inter-Building (B1 to B2):** 2x 10Gbps Single-Mode Fiber (SMF) in an LACP bundle.
*   **Inter-Floor (Vertical):** 2x 10Gbps OM4 Multi-Mode Fiber (MMF) from each IDF to the Distribution block.
*   **Horizontal (Floor):** Cat6a cabling to all endpoints (supporting 10Gbps up to 100m) to accommodate Wi-Fi 6 APs and high-bandwidth IPTV.

---

### 6. QoS & Security Policy

#### QoS Markings (HPE/Aruba Standard)
*   **Voice (VOIP):** Priority Queue (Strict Priority). DSCP **EF**. Zero-jitter policy.
*   **Video (IPTV):** Priority Queue 2. DSCP **CS4**. High bandwidth allocation.
*   **Administrative (Principal/Finance):** Guaranteed bandwidth using Weighted Round Robin (WRR) to ensure critical financial apps never experience congestion.

#### Security Implementation
*   **L2 Security:** Port-security (MAC limiting) on all student ports; BPDU Guard and Root Guard enabled on all access ports.
*   **L3 Segmentation:**
    *   **ACL 1:** Block Student VLANs from accessing Admin/Finance VLANs.
    *   **ACL 2:** Isolate IPTV/VOIP traffic from the Management VLAN.
    *   **RBAC:** The Principal and Finance Head are assigned to a "Priority-Admin" group with unrestricted access to servers and prioritized QoS marking at the switch port level.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the provided network topology and retrieved HPE Aruba Networking datasheets, here is the detailed Bill of Materials (BoM) and hardware selection.

### Hardware Selection Logic
1.  **Core Layer (B1):** The requirement specifies VSX for high availability and chassis-level performance. The **CX 6400** series is the designated choice for this role, providing a modular chassis that supports VSX for a loop-free, active-active control plane.
2.  **Distribution Layer (B2):** For Building 2, a pair of **CX 6300M** switches is selected. They support **VSX**, providing the required L3 routing and redundancy, and possess 10G/25G/50G SFP56 uplinks to connect back to the B1 Core.
3.  **Access Layer (All Floors):** 
    *   **High-Density Floors (B1-F2, B1-F3, B2-F1):** **CX 6300M** (JL661A) is selected for these pods. These switches support **VSF stacking** (essential for the topology) and provide Class 4 PoE for VOIP and APs. 
    *   **Low-Density Floors (B1-F1, B2-F2):** **CX 6200F** (JL728B) is selected. These support VSF stacking (up to 8 members) and provide high PoE budgets (740W) for the necessary endpoints.
    *   *Note: CX 6000 was considered but excluded because it does not support VSF stacking, which is a mandatory requirement of the approved topology.*

### Bill of Materials (BoM)

| Building/Floor | Role | Model & SKU | Key Specs | Qty | Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **B1 - MDF** | Core | **CX 6400 Series** | Modular Chassis, VSX Support, L3 Routing | 2 | High-availability core for the entire campus; supports VSX. |
| **B1 - Floor 1** | Access | **CX 6200F (JL728B)** | 48-port 1G, Class 4 PoE, 740W, VSF | 1 | Sufficient for 46 users + PoE devices; supports VSF. |
| **B1 - Floor 2** | Access | **CX 6300M (JL661A)** | 48-port 1G, Class 4 PoE, 4-port SFP56, VSF | 5 | High density (229 users); VSF stack for simplified MGMT and redundancy. |
| **B1 - Floor 3** | Access | **CX 6300M (JL661A)** | 48-port 1G, Class 4 PoE, 4-port SFP56, VSF | 4 | Moderate density (168 users); VSF stack for redundancy. |
| **B2 - MDF** | Distro | **CX 6300M (JL663A)** | 48-port 1G, 4-port SFP56, VSX/VSF | 2 | VSX pair for B2 distribution; 10G LACP uplinks to B1 Core. |
| **B2 - Floor 1** | Access | **CX 6300M (JL661A)** | 48-port 1G, Class 4 PoE, 4-port SFP56, VSF | 5 | High density (214 users); VSF stack for redundancy. |
| **B2 - Floor 2** | Access | **CX 6200F (JL728B)** | 48-port 1G, Class 4 PoE, 740W, VSF | 2 | Low density (60 users); VSF stack for redundancy. |

### Summary of Capacity & Growth
*   **PoE Budget:** By utilizing the 740W (CX 6200F) and Class 4 PoE (CX 6300M) models, the design ensures ample power for VOIP phones and Wi-Fi 6 APs.
*   **Growth:** The use of VSF stacks allows the organization to add additional switches to any floor's stack (up to the member limit) without needing to redesign the IP scheme or VLAN structure.
*   **Uptime:** The combination of **VSX** at the Core/Distribution and **VSF** at the Access layer, coupled with **LACP** fiber bundles, eliminates single points of failure.
