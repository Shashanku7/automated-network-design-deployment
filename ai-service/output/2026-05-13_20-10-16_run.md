## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Type:** Hierarchical Three-Tier Campus Architecture (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access).
The design utilizes a **Collapsed Core/Distribution** model within each building to minimize latency, linked by a high-speed **Optical Mesh Backbone** between buildings. The architecture implements a "Building-as-a-Block" approach to contain broadcast domains and ensure that a failure in one building does not impact the other.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Hardware Mapping

#### **Core Layer (Central Data Center/Server Room)**
*   **Hardware:** 2x Chassis-based Multi-Layer Switches (configured in **VSX/VSS** for a single logical entity).
*   **Function:** High-speed routing, Inter-VLAN routing, and connection to the Edge Firewall/Internet.

#### **Distribution Layer (Per Building)**
Each building has a dedicated Distribution Block consisting of a pair of switches (VSX/Stack) providing 10GbE/40GbE uplinks to the Core.
*   **PJ Block Dist:** Handles high-density traffic (2,400+ users). Sized for high throughput to support AI/ML and CS labs.
*   **PG Block Dist:** Handles moderate density (700+ users).

#### **Access Layer (Per Floor)**
Sized based on the specific user counts provided. Each floor utilizes **Stackable Access Switches** (PoE+ for WAPs, VoIP, and Cameras).

### 3. High Availability & Link Design

*   **VSX/VSS:** Distribution and Core layers operate in Virtual Switching Extensions (VSX) to eliminate STP blocking and provide sub-second convergence.
*   **LACP (802.3ad):** All uplinks from Access $\rightarrow$ Distribution and Distribution $\rightarrow$ Core are **Multi-chassis Link Aggregation (MLAG/LACP)** bundles.
*   **Link Speeds:**
    *   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** $2 \times 40\text{Gbps}$ Fiber Optic (Active-Active).
    *   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** $2 \times 10\text{Gbps}$ SFP+ Fiber/DAC.
    *   **Access $\leftrightarrow$ User:** $1\text{Gbps}$ Copper / Wi-Fi 6E.
*   **QoS Markings:** 
    *   **EF (Expedited Forwarding):** VoIP and Video Conferencing.
    *   **AF41/42:** AI/ML and CS Lab High-Performance Computing (HPC) traffic.
    *   **BE (Best Effort):** General Student Wi-Fi.

### 5. Redundancy & Failover Strategy
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol)** configured at the Distribution layer for each building to ensure seamless gateway failover.
*   **Physical Path Redundancy:** Dual-homed fiber paths between PJ and PG blocks via two separate physical conduits to prevent "backhoe" outages.
*   **WLC Failover:** Dual Wireless LAN Controllers in High Availability (HA) SSO mode; if the primary fails, the secondary takes over without disconnecting users.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the provided topology and hardware requirements, here is the detailed Bill of Materials. I have selected **HPE Aruba Networking CX** series hardware to meet the high-performance and high-availability (VSX/Stacking) requirements.

### Hardware Selection Logic
*   **Core Layer:** **CX 6400 Series** chassis switches provide the modularity and high-density 40G/100G uplinks required for a campus core.
*   **Distribution Layer:** **CX 6300M Series** switches are chosen for their VSX support, high throughput, and SFP56 (50G) uplinks to the core.
*   **Access Layer:** A mix of **CX 6300F** (for high-density/stacking needs) and **CX 6100** (for lower-density floors) is used. All access switches support PoE+ for APs and cameras.
*   **Wi-Fi Calculation:** Based on $\approx 25\text{--}30$ users per AP.
    *   *Example (PJ Floor 2):* $632 \text{ users} \div 30 \approx 21 \text{ APs}$.
    *   *Example (PJ Floor 1):* $30 \text{ users} \div 30 = 1 \text{ AP}$.

### Hardware Summary Justification
1.  **Port Density:** The PJ Block (CS, AI/ML, 1st Year) requires massive port density. I have deployed **CX 6300F** switches in stacks of up to 4 members per floor to accommodate the $700\text{--}800$ user spikes.
2.  **Uptime:** The use of **CX 6400 (Core)** and **CX 6300M (Distribution)** ensures that the network supports **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**, eliminating STP bottlenecks and providing sub-second failover.
3.  **Growth:** All access switches are sized with a $20\text{--}30\%$ port buffer, and the Core/Distribution layers use 40G/50G capable ports to handle the expected growth of $500$ additional users without hardware replacement.
