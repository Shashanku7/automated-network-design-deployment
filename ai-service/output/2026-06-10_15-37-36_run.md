## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed the following infrastructure based on the provided capacity breakdown and the latest HPE Aruba AOS-CX design standards.

### 1. Topology Overview & Justification
**Chosen Architecture:** **3-Tier (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access)**

**Justification:** 
The campus exceeds 600 total endpoints across two separate buildings with distinct functional requirements (Academic vs. Research/Library). A 3-tier model is required to provide a dedicated Distribution layer for each building, which isolates failure domains, optimizes traffic aggregation, and ensures high-performance routing between the academic blocks and the research labs. This ensures the requested scalability (up to 200 additional users) without impacting the Core.

### 3. High Availability & Redundancy
*   **Core Layer:** Deployed as an **Aruba CX 8360** pair utilizing **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This provides a dual control plane, preventing the "split-brain" scenarios common in traditional stacking.
*   **Distribution Layer:** Each building features a pair of **CX 6400 chassis** (or 8360s) running **VSX**.
*   **Access Layer:** Switches are deployed in **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacks. This allows multiple physical switches to be managed as one logical unit, providing link redundancy.
*   **Default Gateway:** **VSX Active-Gateway** will be utilized at the Core/Distribution levels. This allows both switches to forward traffic for the same virtual IP/MAC, eliminating the need for VRRP and reducing convergence time.

### 4. Link Design & Performance
*   **Core to Distribution:** Dual 25Gbps/40Gbps Fiber links using **LACP (Link Aggregation Control Protocol)** for high-bandwidth, loop-free redundancy.
*   **Distribution to Access:** Dual 10Gbps SFP+ uplinks.
*   **Smart Rate (Multi-Gig):** All high-density student areas (CS Dept, Electronics, Library) utilize **CX 6300M switches with HPE Smart Rate (1/2.5/5Gbps)** to support Wi-Fi 6/6E APs, preventing the 1Gbps bottleneck at the edge.
*   **QoS Policy:** 
    *   **Platinum (EF):** VoIP Traffic (Strict Priority).
    *   **Gold (AF41):** IPTV/Video Streaming.
    *   **Silver (AF21):** Management & Admin.
    *   **Bronze (Default):** Student General Traffic.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the provided network topology and the detailed specifications from the HPE Aruba Networking datasheets, here is the finalized Bill of Materials (BoM).

### **Design Strategy & Justification**
*   **Core Layer:** The **CX 8360** is selected for the core due to its high-performance 4.8 Tbps switching capacity and **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** support, which ensures zero-downtime upgrades and hardware redundancy.
*   **Distribution Layer:** **CX 6400** chassis switches are utilized. These modular switches provide the necessary high-density aggregation and support **VSX** to isolate failure domains between the academic and research blocks.
*   **Access Layer (High Density):** **CX 6300M** switches are deployed in student-heavy areas (CS, Electronics, Library). They feature **Smart Rate (Multi-Gigabit)** ports to support Wi-Fi 6/6E APs (up to 5Gbps), preventing bottlenecks.
*   **Access Layer (Low Density):** **CX 6200F** switches are used for Administration and Research Labs, providing a cost-effective solution with **VSF Stacking** for simplified management and redundancy.
*   **PoE Budgeting:** High-power PoE (up to 90W Class 8) is provided by the 6300M series to accommodate the latest APs and IPTV devices.

### **Technical Summary**
*   **Redundancy:** Core and Distribution use **VSX** (Dual Control Plane); Access uses **VSF** (Logical Stacking).
*   **Connectivity:** Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distro (100Gbps), Distro $\leftrightarrow$ Access (10/25Gbps SFP+).
*   **Wireless Support:** All high-density zones utilize **Smart Rate** to ensure APs are not bottlenecked at 1Gbps.
*   **PoE Readiness:** 740W+ budgets on access switches ensure all VoIP phones and IPTV devices are powered without oversubscription.
