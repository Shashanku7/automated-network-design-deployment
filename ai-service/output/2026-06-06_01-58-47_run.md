## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed the following high-availability campus infrastructure. This design leverages a **Hierarchical Three-Tier Model (Core, Distribution, Access)** utilizing HPE Aruba-style architecture (VSX/VSF) to ensure zero-downtime and seamless scalability.

---

### 1. Topology Overview
**Topology Type:** Hierarchical Star (Collapsed Core/Distribution Architecture).
The design utilizes a **Dual-Core** heartbeat at the Main Data Center, with dedicated **Distribution Blocks** per building. This ensures that a failure in Building 2 does not impact the academic operations of Building 1.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Port Density

#### Core Layer (Central Data Center)
*   **Hardware:** 2x High-performance Chassis Switches.
*   **Function:** Inter-VLAN routing, Policy enforcement, and Gateway for all buildings.
*   **Redundancy:** **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for active-active forwarding.

#### Distribution Layer (Building-Level)
Each building acts as its own distribution block to isolate broadcast domains and localize traffic.
*   **Building 1 Distribution:** 2x Switches (VSX Pair) acting as the aggregation point for the 3 floors.
*   **Building 2 Distribution:** 2x Switches (VSX Pair) acting as the aggregation point for the 2 floors.

#### Access Layer (Floor-Level)
Switches are sized based on the peak device count per floor + 20% growth margin.
*   **B1-F1 (Admin):** 1x 48-Port PoE+ Switch (Total users: 46).
*   **B1-F2 (CS Dept):** 3x 48-Port PoE+ Switches in a **VSF Stack** (Total users: 229).
*   **B1-F3 (Electronics):** 2x 48-Port PoE+ Switches in a **VSF Stack** (Total users: 167).
*   **B2-F1 (Library):** 3x 48-Port PoE+ Switches in a **VSF Stack** (Total users: 213).
*   **B2-F2 (Research):** 1x 48-Port PoE+ Switch (Total users: 60).

---

### 3. High Availability & Link Design

| Link Segment | Technology | Media | Speed | Redundancy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution** | LACP (LAG) | Single-Mode Fiber | 20Gbps / 40Gbps | Dual-homed (Active-Active) |
| **Dist. $\leftrightarrow$ Access** | LACP (LAG) | Multi-Mode Fiber | 10Gbps | Dual-homed to VSX Pair |
| **Access $\leftrightarrow$ User** | PoE+ / 802.3at | Cat6a | 1Gbps | N/A |

*   **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension):** Implemented at the Core and Distribution layers to allow a single logical entity with two physical switches, eliminating STP (Spanning Tree) blocked ports.
*   **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework):** Implemented at the Access layer (B1-F2, B1-F3, B2-F1) to manage multiple switches as one, simplifying management and increasing backplane throughput.

### 5. Redundancy & Failover Mechanisms

1.  **Gateway Redundancy:** 
    *   **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol)** or **VSX Gateway Sync** will be used. All VLANs will have a Virtual IP (VIP) as the default gateway. If the primary Core switch fails, the secondary takes over in milliseconds.
2.  **Loop Prevention:**
    *   Since VSX/VSF creates a logical chassis, STP is minimized. However, **BPDU Guard** and **Root Guard** will be enabled on all access ports to prevent student-introduced loops.
3.  **Traffic Engineering (ACLs):**
    *   **Finance/Principal Zone:** A dedicated ACL on the Core layer will restrict access to the `B1-Admin-Mgmt` (VLAN 30) subnet, allowing only authorized MAC addresses and specific management ports.
    *   **Library/Server Zone:** Strict "Stateful Inspection" for any traffic entering the Server Room VLAN from student VLANs.

### 6. Summary of Design Choices
*   **PoE+ (Power over Ethernet):** All Access switches are PoE+ enabled to power VOIP phones and High-Density Wi-Fi 6 APs without requiring local power adapters.
*   **WLAN:** High-density APs placed in the CS Dept and Library, mapped to the Student VLANs via 802.1X authentication.
*   **Scalability:** The use of `/23` subnets for students and the modular Distribution block allows the addition of new floors or buildings by simply adding another Distribution pair without re-addressing the Core.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the provided topology and the retrieved datasheets for the HPE Aruba Networking CX series, I have selected the hardware to match your specific user densities and high-availability requirements.

### Hardware Selection Logic
*   **Core Layer:** I have selected the **CX 6400 series**. These are chassis-based switches capable of the extremely high throughput and redundancy (VSX) required for a campus core.
*   **Distribution Layer:** The **CX 6300M** is used here. It supports **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**, allowing two switches to act as one logical entity for zero-downtime aggregation.
*   **Access Layer:** The **CX 6300M (PoE+ models)** is selected for high-density floors. It supports **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)**, allowing us to stack up to 10 switches to manage them as a single unit. For the lower-density floor (B1-F1), a single unit suffices.
*   **Wi-Fi:** Calculated at ~25-30 users per AP. 
    *   B1-F2 (229 users) $\approx$ 8 APs.
    *   B1-F3 (167 users) $\approx$ 6 APs.
    *   B2-F1 (213 users) $\approx$ 7 APs.
    *   B2-F2 (60 users) $\approx$ 2 APs.
    *   B1-F1 (38 users) $\approx$ 2 APs.
