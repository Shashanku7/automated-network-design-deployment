## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed the following high-availability campus architecture. This design leverages a **Hierarchical Three-Tier Model** (Core, Distribution, Access) optimized for HPE Aruba networking standards.

---

### 1. Topology Overview
**Type:** Collapsed Core/Distribution Architecture with a Star Topology.
*   **Core/Distribution Layer:** Centralized in Building 1 (MDF). This layer handles L3 routing, inter-VLAN routing, and security policies.
*   **Access Layer:** Distributed per floor in each building (IDFs). All access switches are PoE+ enabled to support VoIP, IPTV, and WiFi 6 APs.
*   **Physical Medium:** 10G SFP+ Fiber (OM4) for vertical/inter-building uplinks; Cat6a for horizontal distribution.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Sizing

#### Building 1: Main Academic Block (Distribution Block A)
*   **Floor 1 (Admin/Reception):** 1x 48-port PoE+ Switch. (Low density, high security).
*   **Floor 2 (Computer Science):** 2x 48-port PoE+ Switches (VSF Stacked). High density (229+ devices) requiring 10G uplinks to prevent bottlenecks.
*   **Floor 3 (Electronics):** 1x 48-port PoE+ Switch. (Moderate density).

#### Building 2: Library & Research (Distribution Block B)
*   **Floor 1 (Main Library):** 2x 48-port PoE+ Switches (VSF Stacked). High student density and high WiFi AP count for coverage.
*   **Floor 2 (Research Labs):** 1x 48-port PoE+ Switch. Optimized for low-latency throughput.

---

### 3. High Availability & Link Design

*   **Core Redundancy:** Deploy two Core switches in **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This allows a single logical entity for the core, eliminating Spanning Tree (STP) blocking and providing active-active forwarding.
*   **Access Redundancy:** Access switches on high-density floors (CS Dept, Library) will be configured in **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacks.
*   **Link Aggregation:** 
    *   **Core to Distribution:** 2x 10Gbps SFP+ LACP bundles (Multi-chassis EtherChannel).
    *   **Distribution to Access:** 2x 10Gbps SFP+ uplinks per stack for redundancy and bandwidth.
*   **Failover:** **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol)** will be used at the Core layer to provide a redundant default gateway for all VLANs.

---

### 4. VLAN & Subnet Plan (Segmentation)

To ensure security and performance, the network is segmented by **Role + Building**. Subnets are sized as `/23` for students (to allow for growth) and `/24` for staff/admin.

**QoS Marking Strategy (HPE/Aruba Standard):**
*   **VoIP:** CoS 5 / DSCP EF (Expedited Forwarding) $\rightarrow$ Strict Priority Queue.
*   **IPTV:** CoS 4 / DSCP AF41 $\rightarrow$ High Priority Queue.
*   **Management/Admin:** CoS 3 $\rightarrow$ Medium Priority.
*   **Students/Guest:** CoS 0 $\rightarrow$ Best Effort.

### 6. Design Summary for Implementation
*   **Core:** 2x Chassis/Modular switches in VSX mode.
*   **Distribution:** B1 and B2 act as distinct distribution blocks, aggregating floor switches.
*   **Access:** Stacked switches per floor to reduce management IP overhead and provide port redundancy.
*   **Wireless:** WiFi 6 APs deployed in a high-density pattern, utilizing the **Guest VLAN (800)** and **Student VLANs** via 802.1X authentication.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

As a Network Hardware Specialist, I have analyzed your hierarchical topology and user counts to select the most cost-effective yet high-performance HPE Aruba hardware.

### Hardware Selection Analysis
1.  **Core Layer:** The requirement for **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** and a modular chassis for high availability leads to the **Aruba CX 6405**. This provides the necessary L3 routing, redundancy, and scalability for the campus.
2.  **Access Layer:** 
    *   **High Density (CS Dept, Library):** I have selected the **Aruba CX 6300M** series. These support **VSF Stacking** (as requested in the topology) and provide high-performance PoE+ for a large number of devices.
    *   **Low/Moderate Density (Admin, Electronics, Research):** I have selected the **Aruba CX 6200F** series. These are more cost-effective than the 6300M while still providing the necessary PoE+ and 10G uplinks.
3.  **Wireless:** Based on the user count (approx. 25-30 users per AP), the total student/staff population across both buildings is ~690. This requires roughly **23-28 APs**. I have distributed these based on floor density.

### Technical Implementation Notes:
*   **Port Density:** All access switches are 48-port models to accommodate current user counts plus the expected growth (50-200 users).
*   **PoE Budget:** The selected `JL728B` and `S0G06A` models provide high PoE budgets to simultaneously power VoIP phones, IPTV devices, and WiFi 6 APs.
*   **Redundancy:** Core redundancy is achieved via **VSX**, and critical access floors (B1-F2, B2-F1) utilize **VSF Stacking** to ensure no single point of failure at the floor level.
*   **Uplinks:** All access-to-core links are specified as **10G SFP+** using OM4 fiber to eliminate bottlenecks for high-density areas like the Computer Science department.
