### 1. Topology Overview

### 2. Layer Mapping
*   **Core Layer:** High-speed backbone for inter-building routing.
*   **Distribution Block:** Each building will be treated as a discrete distribution block to contain broadcast domains and localize routing.
*   **Access Layer:** Switches will be sized based on your specific port density per floor (counting Students + Staff + Admin + VOIP + IPTV + 20% growth overhead).

### 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Logical Virtualization:** Use of **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** or **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** to eliminate Spanning Tree (STP) dependencies and allow for Active-Active forwarding.
*   **Link Aggregation:** LACP (802.3ad) bundles for all uplinks (e.g., 2x10GbE or 2x40GbE) to ensure no single point of failure.
*   **Redundancy:** VRRP/HSRP for gateway redundancy at the distribution layer.

### 4. Segmentation & VLAN Strategy

*   **Student VLANs:** Isolated, client-isolated.
*   **Staff/Admin VLANs:** Higher priority, access to internal servers.
*   **Voice (VOIP) VLANs:** Optimized for low jitter/latency.
*   **Multimedia (IPTV) VLANs:** Multicast-optimized.

### 5. Subnetting & QoS Plan (HPE/Aruba Standard)

*   **Subnet Masks:** Sized precisely to your user counts (using VLSM).
