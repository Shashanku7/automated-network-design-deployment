## Phase 1: Rephrased Prompt

**Role:** Expert Network Architect & Topology Designer
**Task:** Design a comprehensive, scalable, and resilient campus network infrastructure for a university consisting of five distinct buildings.

**1. Project Purpose & Objectives**
The primary goal is to create a high-availability campus area network (CAN) that provides seamless connectivity for academic staff, students, and administrative operations. The design must support a "Core-Distribution-Access" hierarchical model to ensure modularity and ease of troubleshooting.

**2. Scope & Scale (Assumptions)**
*   **Building Count:** 5 Buildings (e.g., Academic Hall, Library, Administration, Student Dormitory, and Research Lab).
*   **User Density:** 
    *   Total estimated users: 5,000–8,000.
    *   Device density: Average of 3 devices per user (Laptop, Smartphone, Tablet).
*   **Device Types:** Workstations, VoIP phones, Wireless Access Points (Wi-Fi 6/6E), IP Cameras, and IoT building sensors.

**3. Performance & Connectivity Requirements**
*   **Backbone:** Design a 10Gbps or 40Gbps fiber-optic backbone connecting the buildings to a central Main Distribution Frame (MDF).
*   **Intra-Building:** 1Gbps to the desktop with PoE+ support for APs and VoIP phones.
*   **Wireless:** High-density WLAN deployment with seamless roaming (802.11r/k/v) across all five buildings.
*   **Latency:** Optimized for real-time applications (Video conferencing, Virtual Labs, VoIP).

**4. Physical & Logical Constraints**
*   **Topology:** Implement a Star or Collapsed Core topology.
*   **Cabling:** Specify Single-Mode Fiber (SMF) for inter-building links and Category 6A (Cat6A) for horizontal cabling.
*   **MDF/IDF:** Define one Main Distribution Frame (MDF) in the Administration building and Intermediate Distribution Frames (IDFs) in each building.

**5. Security & Segmentation**
*   **VLAN Strategy:** Segment traffic by user role (e.g., Student VLAN, Faculty VLAN, Admin VLAN, Guest VLAN, and IoT/Management VLAN).
*   **Edge Security:** Implement a Next-Generation Firewall (NGFW) at the internet perimeter with Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS).
*   **Access Control:** Integration of 802.1X authentication (RADIUS/TACACS+) for secure network admission.

**6. Budget & Sustainability**
*   **Tier:** Mid-to-Enterprise grade hardware (e.g., Cisco, Aruba, or Juniper).
*   **Focus:** Prioritize a balance between high performance and long-term TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) through energy-efficient switching and scalable licensing.

**Deliverables Requested:**
1.  **Logical Network Diagram:** Showing VLANs, IP addressing scheme (IPv4/IPv6), and routing protocols (OSPF/EIGRP).
2.  **Physical Topology Map:** Detailing cable runs, switch placements, and hardware specifications.
3.  **Hardware Bill of Materials (BOM):** Suggested quantities of Core Switches, Distribution Switches, Access Switches, and WAPs.
4.  **Redundancy Plan:** Explanation of failover mechanisms (e.g., EtherChannel, HSRP/VRRP, or Spanning Tree optimization).

## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Type:** Hierarchical Star Topology (Three-Tier Architecture)
The design utilizes a centralized Core located in the Administration Building (MDF), extending to Distribution layers in each of the five buildings (IDFs), and terminating at the Access layer. This ensures a deterministic traffic pattern and isolates failure domains.

### 2. Layer Breakdown
*   **Core Layer (MDF):** 2x Enterprise Chassis Switches (Virtual Switching Framework/VSX cluster).
*   **Distribution Layer (IDF):** 2x Layer 3 Stackable Switches per building (Total: 10 switches).
*   **Access Layer (IDF):** 12–20x PoE+ Access Switches per building, depending on floor plan (Total: 60–100 switches), deployed in stacks (VSF/StackWise).

### 3. High-Availability & Performance
*   **Core/Dist Redundancy:** VSX (Virtual Switching Extension) or VSS to eliminate STP blocking and allow active-active forwarding.
*   **Link Aggregation:** LACP (802.3ad) used for all uplinks to provide increased bandwidth and sub-second failover.
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol) for inter-VLAN routing resilience.
*   **QoS Strategy:** 
    *   **Platinum:** VoIP/Video (DSCP EF / CoS 5)
    *   **Gold:** Academic Management/Virtual Labs (DSCP AF41 / CoS 4)
    *   **Silver:** General Student/Staff Web Traffic (Best Effort)
    *   **Bronze:** Guest/IoT Traffic (DSCP CS1 / CoS 1)

### 4. Link Design
| Segment | Cable Type | Speed | Configuration | Redundancy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core $\rightarrow$ Dist** | SMF (OS2) | 40Gbps | 2x 40G LACP Bundle | Dual-Homed to Core Nodes |
| **Dist $\rightarrow$ Access** | SMF/MMF | 10Gbps | 2x 10G LACP Bundle | Cross-Stacked Uplinks |
| **Access $\rightarrow$ End User** | Cat6A | 1Gbps | Single Link | PoE+ (802.3at) |

### 6. Redundancy & Failover
*   **L3 Routing:** OSPF (Open Shortest Path First) used between Core and Distribution layers for dynamic path selection and rapid convergence.
*   **L2 Loop Prevention:** Rapid Per-VLAN Spanning Tree (RPVST+) as a secondary fail-safe, though VSX/Stacking minimizes STP reliance.
*   **Edge Failover:** Dual-homed NGFW firewalls in Active-Passive mode using a virtual MAC address for internet egress.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the approved topology and the retrieved datasheets from the HPE Aruba Networking CX portfolio, here is the detailed Bill of Materials (BoM).

### 1. Topology Analysis & Hardware Mapping
*   **Core Layer:** The requirement for a high-capacity chassis with VSX support for a campus environment is met by the **CX 6400 Series**. The 6405 chassis provides the necessary modularity and 14 Tbps switching capacity to handle aggregated traffic from 5 buildings.
*   **Distribution Layer:** The requirement for L3 stackable switches with 10Gbps+ uplinks and VSX/OSPF support is met by the **CX 6300M Series**. These modular switches support the required 40Gbps uplinks to the core via SFP56 ports.
*   **Access Layer:** The requirement for PoE+ (802.3at) and VSF stacking is met by the **CX 6200F Series**. These switches provide the necessary 1Gbps downlinks and 10Gbps SFP+ uplinks for the distribution layer.

### 2. Bill of Materials (BoM)

| Role | Model & SKU | Key Specifications | Qty | Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core Switch** | **Aruba CX 6405 v2** (R0X26C) | 5-slot chassis, 14 Tbps switching capacity, VSX support, Management Module (R0X31A) included. | 2 | Forms the VSX cluster in the MDF. Modular chassis ensures scalability for the entire campus. |
| **Distribution Switch** | **Aruba CX 6300M** (S4P43A) | 48-port 1G SFP, 2x SFP28 (25G), 2x SFP56 (50G), L3 OSPF, VSX support. | 10 | 2 per building. SFP56 ports provide the required 40Gbps LACP bundles to the Core. |
| **Access Switch** | **Aruba CX 6200F** (S0M85A) | 48-port 1G Base-T, Class 4 PoE (up to 740W), 4x 10G SFP+ uplinks, VSF stacking. | 60–100 | Deployed in stacks per building. Provides PoE+ for VoIP/APs and 10G uplinks to Distribution. |
| **Core $\rightarrow$ Dist Transceiver** | **SFP56 40G/50G** (Custom) | 40Gbps SMF (OS2) Transceiver | 20 | 2x 40G LACP links per Dist switch $\times$ 10 switches. |
| **Dist $\rightarrow$ Access Transceiver** | **SFP+ 10G LR** (J9151E) | 10Gbps SMF LC LR 10km | 120–200 | 2x 10G LACP links per Access stack $\times$ approx. 60 stacks. |
| **Access $\rightarrow$ End User** | **Cat6A Cable** | 1Gbps / PoE+ 802.3at | As needed | Supports high-bandwidth academic labs and PoE for Wi-Fi 6 APs. |

### 3. Technical Justification for Selection
*   **High Availability:** The **CX 6400** (Core) and **CX 6300M** (Dist) both support **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**, enabling the active-active forwarding and sub-second failover required in the "High-Availability & Performance" section of the topology.
*   **Bandwidth Matching:** 
    *   **Core $\rightarrow$ Dist:** The CX 6300M's **SFP56** ports are used to achieve the requested 40Gbps throughput.
    *   **Dist $\rightarrow$ Access:** The CX 6200F's **SFP+** ports provide the 10Gbps LACP bundles required to prevent bottlenecks during peak student usage.
*   **Power Requirements:** The **CX 6200F (S0M85A)** was chosen specifically for its **740W** power budget to ensure sufficient PoE+ headroom for dense deployments of VoIP phones and Wi-Fi 6 Access Points across the 5 university buildings.
*   **L3 Routing:** Both the Core and Distribution layers are fully compatible with **OSPF**, facilitating the dynamic routing plan outlined in the approved topology.
