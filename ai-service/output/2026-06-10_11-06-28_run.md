## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Chosen Model:** 3-Tier Architecture (Core, Distribution, Access)

**Justification:**
The campus consists of two separate buildings with a total endpoint count of approximately 650+ (including estimated APs), exceeding the 500-endpoint threshold for a 2-tier design. A 3-tier model is required to provide dedicated distribution blocks for each building, ensuring that high-density traffic from the Computer Science and Research labs is aggregated efficiently before hitting the core, thereby reducing latency and increasing scalability for the projected growth of 200 additional users.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Port Density
Calculations include a **20% growth margin** and **1 AP per 25 users**.

#### **Building 1: Main Academic Block**
*   **Floor 1 (Admin/Reception):** 46 endpoints + 2 APs = 48. $\times 1.2 \approx 58$ ports.
    *   *Requirement:* **(2) 48-port PoE+ Switches** (To allow for future growth and physical cable runs).
*   **Floor 2 (CS Dept):** 229 endpoints + 9 APs = 238. $\times 1.2 \approx 286$ ports.
    *   *Requirement:* **(6) 48-port PoE+ Switches** (Smart Rate/Multi-Gigabit required for APs).
*   **Floor 3 (Electronics):** 167 endpoints + 7 APs = 174. $\times 1.2 \approx 209$ ports.
    *   *Requirement:* **(5) 48-port PoE+ Switches**.
*   **Distribution Block 1:** Two high-performance switches (VSX pair) aggregating all floor switches.

#### **Building 2: Library & Research**
*   **Floor 1 (Library):** 213 endpoints + 9 APs = 222. $\times 1.2 \approx 267$ ports.
    *   *Requirement:* **(6) 48-port PoE+ Switches** (Smart Rate/Multi-Gigabit required for APs).
*   **Floor 2 (Research):** 59 endpoints + 3 APs = 62. $\times 1.2 \approx 75$ ports.
    *   *Requirement:* **(2) 48-port PoE+ Switches**.
*   **Distribution Block 2:** Two high-performance switches (VSX pair) aggregating all floor switches.

---

### 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Core/Distribution Layer:** Deploy **Aruba VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This provides a distributed control plane and prevents loops without relying on Spanning Tree (STP) for convergence.
*   **Access Layer:** Use **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** to stack access switches on each floor, creating a single logical entity to simplify management and provide link redundancy.
*   **Link Speeds:**
    *   **Access to Distribution:** 2 $\times$ 10GbE LACP (Link Aggregation) bundles per stack.
    *   **Distribution to Core:** 2 $\times$ 40GbE or 100GbE Fiber optic links (LACP).
    *   **Multi-Gigabit:** All AP-facing ports in CS, Library, and Research areas must use **Smart Rate (2.5G/5G)** to support Wi-Fi 6/6E throughput.
*   **QoS:** Implement strict priority queuing for VoIP (EF Class) and IPTV (AF41), ensuring zero jitter for real-time communications.

### 5. Redundancy & Failover Logic
*   **Default Gateway:** Utilize **VSX Active-Gateway**. This allows both switches in the VSX pair to forward traffic for the same virtual IP/MAC, eliminating the "blocked port" waste of STP and providing instantaneous failover.
*   **L3 Routing:** OSPF will be used between the Core and Distribution layers to ensure dynamic path selection and rapid convergence.
*   **Anti-Loop:** Since VSX Active-Gateway is used, **VRRP is explicitly disabled** on those segments to avoid conflict and synchronization issues.
*   **Physical Path:** Diversified fiber paths between Building 1 and Building 2 to prevent a single "backhoe" incident from severing all connectivity.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the approved topology and the technical specifications from the HPE Aruba Networking datasheets, here is the detailed Bill of Materials (BOM) and hardware selection for the campus network.

### Hardware Selection Logic

1.  **Core & Distribution Layer (VSX Pair):**
    *   **Selected Model:** **CX 6400 Series (Modular Chassis)**.
    *   **Justification:** The topology requires a 3-tier architecture with high-performance VSX pairs for distribution and core. The CX 6400 provides the necessary modularity, high-density 10G/25G/40G/100G uplinks, and full VSX support for a non-blocking, highly available core/distribution backbone. It supports the 40GbE/100GbE fiber links required for inter-building and distribution-to-core connectivity.

2.  **Access Layer (High-Density/Multi-Gigabit Zones):**
    *   **Selected Model:** **CX 6300L Series**.
    *   **Justification:** The Computer Science (B1-F2) and Library/Research (B2-F1, B2-F2) areas specifically require **Smart Rate (1G/2.5G/5G)** to support Wi-Fi 6/6E APs. The CX 6300L (e.g., S3L76A) provides these multi-gigabit ports with Class 8 PoE (up to 90W), ensuring future-proof connectivity for high-throughput APs and IoT devices. These switches also support **VSF stacking** up to 10 members to simplify management.

3.  **Access Layer (Standard PoE Zones):**
    *   **Selected Model:** **CX 6200F/M Series**.
    *   **Justification:** For the Administration and Electronics departments, standard PoE+ (Class 4) is sufficient. The CX 6200 provides a cost-effective yet powerful solution with VSF stacking (up to 8 members) and 10GbE uplinks, matching the required 2x10GbE LACP bundles to the distribution layer.
