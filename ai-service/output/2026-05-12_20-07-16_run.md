## User Prompt

Design a campus network for an organization with 2 building(s), 5 floor(s) per building. There are approximately 6000 students/visitors, 100 staff/faculty, and 5 administrators. Devices needed: laptops, printers, phones, cameras, wifi. Sensitive areas requiring extra security: server, exams. Special roles: Principal, Exam Controller, Finance Head, Hostel Warden. Uptime requirement: important. Expecting growth of 200-500.

## Phase 1: Rephrased Prompt

**Role:** Senior Network Architect / Topology Designer

**Objective:** 
Design a high-availability, scalable campus network infrastructure for an educational organization consisting of two buildings, each with five floors. The design must balance high-density client access with strict security zoning for administrative and academic integrity.

**1. Scale and User Requirements:**
*   **Total User Base:** ~6,105 active users.
    *   Students/Visitors: 6,000 (High-density wireless requirement).
    *   Staff/Faculty: 100.
    *   Administrators: 5.
*   **Projected Growth:** Accommodate an additional 200–500 users without requiring a core hardware overhaul.
*   **Device Ecosystem:** 
    *   End-user devices (Laptops/Mobile devices).
    *   Shared peripherals (Network printers).
    *   Communication (VoIP phones).
    *   Security/Surveillance (IP Cameras).
    *   Connectivity (High-density Wi-Fi 6/6E APs).

**2. Physical and Logical Topology:**
*   **Physical Layout:** 2 Buildings $\times$ 5 Floors.
*   **Proposed Architecture:** A Three-Tier Hierarchical Model (Core, Distribution, and Access layers) or a Collapsed Core architecture if appropriate for the scale.
*   **Inter-Building Connectivity:** Define the fiber optic backbone (Single-mode/Multi-mode) connecting the two buildings to ensure low latency and redundancy.

**3. Performance and Reliability:**
*   **Uptime Requirement:** Critical. Implement redundancy at the Core and Distribution layers (e.g., MLAG, Stacking, or HSRP/VRRP).
*   **Traffic Prioritization:** Implement Quality of Service (QoS) for VoIP and critical administrative traffic.
*   **Throughput:** Ensure the backbone can handle high-bandwidth bursts from 6,000+ simultaneous wireless clients.

**4. Security and Segmentation:**
*   **VLAN Strategy:** Design a robust VLAN structure to isolate traffic based on user role and device type (e.g., Student-VLAN, Staff-VLAN, VoIP-VLAN, CCTV-VLAN).
*   **High-Security Zones (Micro-segmentation):**
    *   **Server Room:** Restricted access, hardened firewall policies, and dedicated management VLAN.
    *   **Exam Areas:** Temporary or permanent "Lockdown" zones with strict bandwidth throttling and restricted external access.
*   **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):** Define specific ACLs and privilege levels for:
    *   Principal
    *   Exam Controller
    *   Finance Head
    *   Hostel Warden

**5. Deliverables Required:**
*   **Logical Topology Diagram:** Showing VLANs, IP subnetting scheme, and routing protocols.
*   **Physical Topology Diagram:** Showing cable runs, switch placement per floor, and IDF/MDF locations.
*   **Hardware Bill of Materials (BoM):** Recommended switches (PoE+ for cameras/APs), routers, firewalls, and wireless controllers.
*   **Security Matrix:** Mapping user roles to their respective network permissions.

**6. Constraints and Assumptions:**
*   **Budget:** Mid-to-Enterprise grade (Prioritize reliability and security over lowest-cost options).
*   **Power:** Assume PoE+ is required for all Access Layer switches to support cameras and Wi-Fi APs.

## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Type:** Three-Tier Hierarchical Campus Design (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access).
This architecture is selected over a collapsed core to ensure localized traffic termination at the distribution layer, reducing the blast radius of broadcast storms and providing scalable modularity for the two-building layout.

### 2. Layer Breakdown
*   **Core Layer (1 Cluster):** 2x High-Chassis Modular Switches (Building A - Main Data Center).
*   **Distribution Layer (2 Clusters):** 2x Switch Pairs per building (10 switches total), functioning as the L3 boundary.
*   **Access Layer (20 Switches):** 2x PoE+ Switches per floor $\times$ 5 floors $\times$ 2 buildings.

### 3. High-Availability & Logic
*   **Core/Dist Redundancy:** **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** or **MLAG** for a loop-free topology without relying on Spanning Tree (STP) for convergence.
*   **Access Redundancy:** **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacking for floor-level switch management; Dual-homed uplinks to Distribution via **LACP (802.3ad)**.
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** **VRRP** (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol) for the default gateway at the Distribution layer.
*   **QoS Policy:** 
    *   **EF (Expedited Forwarding):** VoIP Traffic.
    *   **AF41:** Administrative/Finance Critical Data.
    *   **Best Effort:** Student/Visitor Wi-Fi.

### 4. Link Design
| Link Path | Media Type | Speed | Configuration | Redundancy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Dist** | SM Fiber | 40Gbps/100Gbps | LACP Bundle (2x links) | Dual-Homed (Active/Active) |
| **Dist $\leftrightarrow$ Access** | MM Fiber | 10Gbps/25Gbps | LACP Bundle (2x links) | Dual-Homed (Active/Active) |
| **Access $\leftrightarrow$ User** | Cat6a | 1Gbps/2.5Gbps | Single Link | PoE+ (802.3at) |
| **Inter-Bldg** | SM Fiber | 40Gbps | Diverse Path Fiber | Physical Path Diversity |

| VLAN ID | Name | Purpose | Subnet | QoS Marking | Security Zone |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 10 | MGMT-VLAN | Network Admin/SSH | 10.0.10.0/24 | CS3 | Restricted |

### 6. Redundancy & Failover
*   **L2 Redundancy:** VSX/MLAG eliminates STP blocked ports, utilizing 100% of available bandwidth.
*   **L3 Redundancy:** VRRP ensures that if one Distribution switch fails, the Virtual IP (VIP) migrates to the peer in $<1$ second.
*   **Routing:** OSPF used between Core and Distribution for dynamic path convergence and Equal-Cost Multi-Path (ECMP) routing.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the approved topology and the retrieved datasheets for the HPE Aruba Networking CX series, here is the detailed Bill of Materials.

### 1. Topology Analysis & Device Mapping
*   **Core Layer:** Requires a high-capacity modular chassis supporting VSX and 100Gbps uplinks. The **CX 6400 Series** is the designated modular chassis for this role, providing the necessary scale and availability.
*   **Distribution Layer:** Requires L3 capabilities, VSX support, and high-speed connectivity (25G/50G/100G). The **CX 6300M** series is selected here as it supports VSX (unlike the 6300L/6100) and provides the required 10G/25G/50G uplink flexibility.
*   **Access Layer:** Requires PoE+ for cameras/phones/APs and VSF stacking for simplified management. The **CX 6200M** is selected for its balance of PoE+ capability and VSF stacking support.

### 2. Bill of Materials (BOM)

| Role | Model & SKU | Key Specifications | Qty | Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core Switch** | CX 6405 v2 Chassis `(R0X26C)` | Modular Chassis, 5 slots, VSX support, 14 Tbps switching capacity | 2 | High-availability core cluster in Bldg A; supports modular growth and 100G connectivity. |
| **Distribution Switch** | CX 6300M 24p SFP+ `(JL658A)` | 24x 1G/10G SFP+, 4x 1G/10G/25G/50G uplinks, VSX support | 10 | 2 clusters per building (5 floors $\times$ 2). L3 boundary with high-speed 25G/50G connectivity to Core. |
| **Access Switch** | CX 6200M 48G PoE+ `(R8Q70A)` | 48x 10/100/1000 Base-T PoE+, 4x 1/10G SFP+, VSF Stacking | 20 | 2 switches per floor $\times$ 5 floors $\times$ 2 buildings. Provides PoE+ for VoIP, APs, and Cameras. |

| **Core Optics** | 100G QSFP28 | 100Gbps SM Fiber | 8 | LACP bundles between Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution. |

### 3. Design Justification
*   **High Availability:** The use of **VSX** at the Core and Distribution layers eliminates STP reliance, allowing active-active paths and sub-second convergence.
*   **Scalability:** The **CX 6400** chassis allows the organization to add line cards as the student population grows by the expected 200-500.
*   **Power & PoE:** The **CX 6200M** supports the 802.3at (PoE+) standard required for the deployment of modern Wi-Fi APs, IP phones, and surveillance cameras.
*   **Performance:** Link speeds are tiered (100G Core $\rightarrow$ 25G Dist $\rightarrow$ 10G Access) to prevent bottlenecks during peak usage hours in a campus environment.
