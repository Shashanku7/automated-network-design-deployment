## Phase 2: Network Topology

# Campus Network Topology Design: Academic & Research Facilities

## 1. Topology Overview
**Chosen Model:** **3-Tier Architecture (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access)**

**Justification:** 
With a total endpoint count of **643+ devices** across **two separate buildings** and **five distinct floors**, a 3-tier model is required to ensure high performance and modular scalability. A dedicated distribution layer per building prevents local traffic (e.g., high-volume Computer Science lab data) from saturating the core and allows for localized policy enforcement and failure domain isolation.

---

## 2. Layer Breakdown & Port Calculations
To ensure a **20% growth margin**, the formula used is: $\text{Required Ports} = (\text{Total Endpoints} \times 1.2)$. 
*Note: 1 Wireless AP is allocated per 25 users.*

### Building 1: Main Academic Block
**Distribution Block 1:** Aggregates Floors 1-3 via 10GbE/25GbE uplinks.

## 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Core Layer:** **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** pair. Two high-end switches acting as a single logical entity with dual control planes to ensure zero-downtime during firmware upgrades (ISSU).
*   **Distribution Layer:** **VSX** at the building level. Each building distribution pair provides a resilient gateway.
*   **Access Layer:** **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacking. Switches on each floor are stacked to simplify management and provide cross-stack EtherChannel (LAG) to the distribution layer.
*   **Link Speeds:**
    *   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** $2 \times 25\text{Gbps}$ LACP bundles.
    *   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** $2 \times 10\text{Gbps}$ LACP bundles.
    *   **Smart Rate (mGig):** The **Computer Science Dept (B1-F2)** and **Main Library (B2-F1)** will utilize **HPE Smart Rate (2.5G/5G)** ports for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs to prevent bottlenecks.

## 5. Redundancy & Failover Logic
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** Use **VSX Active-Gateway**. This provides a virtual MAC and IP for the default gateway across the VSX pair. 
    *   *Constraint Check:* VRRP is **disabled** on these segments to avoid conflict with Active-Gateway.
*   **Loop Prevention:** Standardized on **LACP (802.3ad)** for all uplinks. Spanning Tree (MSTP) is enabled as a fail-safe but is not the primary convergence mechanism.
*   **Security Zones:**
    *   **Server Room & Library:** Dedicated ACLs on the Distribution switches. MAC-filtering and 802.1X (via Aruba ClearPass) are mandated for all Research/Admin ports.
    *   **RBAC:** The **Principal** and **Finance Head** are assigned to a "Priority-User" Role via Aruba Dynamic Segmentation, ensuring guaranteed bandwidth and priority queuing in the QoS scheduler.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

To design the network hardware for the academic campus, I have evaluated the Aruba CX portfolio across all specified roles. Based on the 3-Tier Architecture (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access) and the specific port/power requirements, the following Bill of Materials (BOM) is recommended.

### Hardware Selection Strategy
*   **Core Layer:** The **CX 6400** is selected for the core to support **VSX** (Virtual Switching Extension) for zero-downtime and high-density 10G/25G/100G connectivity.
*   **Distribution Layer:** The **CX 6300M** is chosen for its capability to act as a high-performance aggregation point with **VSX** support, providing resilient gateways for each building.
*   **Access Layer:** 
    *   **General Floors:** **CX 6200F** is the best fit for standard PoE+ needs and supports **VSF stacking** for simplified management.
    *   **High-Density/mGig Zones:** For the Computer Science Dept (B1-F2) and Main Library (B2-F1), the **CX 6300L** is utilized. This provides **Smart Rate (1G/2.5G/5G)** ports to eliminate bottlenecks for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs and high-bandwidth research users.

### Technical Validation
*   **Port Density:** All floors are equipped with $\ge 20\%$ growth margin. (e.g., B1-F2 needs 286 ports; $6 \times 48 = 288$ ports provided).
*   **PoE Budget:** CX 6200F (740W) and CX 6300L (Class 8/90W) are selected to handle the combined load of VoIP phones, IPTV, and high-power Wi-Fi 6 APs.
*   **mGig Connectivity:** Smart Rate ports are strategically deployed in the **Computer Science Dept** and **Main Library** to ensure the wireless backhaul is not limited to 1Gbps.
*   **Uptime & Redundancy:** 
    *   **Core/Dist:** VSX provides a dual-control plane, removing the single point of failure.
    *   **Access:** VSF allows multiple switches to be managed as one, with cross-stack LACP for uplink redundancy.
*   **Security:** All selected models support **Dynamic Segmentation** and **802.1X** (via ClearPass) for the sensitive areas (Server, Library) and RBAC for the Principal and Finance Head.
