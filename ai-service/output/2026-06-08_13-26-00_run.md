## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed the following infrastructure for the **PG Block**. Given the density of students (up to 350 per floor) and the requirement for high availability and strict isolation, I am proposing a **Collapsed Core Architecture** utilizing **HPE Aruba-style VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for a non-blocking, loop-free environment.

---

### 1. Topology Overview
*   **Architecture:** Collapsed Core/Distribution + Access Layer.
*   **Topology Type:** Hierarchical Star / Hub-and-Spoke.
*   **Logical Design:** Every floor acts as an Access Block. These blocks dual-home into a redundant Core pair.
*   **Control Plane:** VSX (Virtual Switching Extension) at the Core to eliminate STP (Spanning Tree Protocol) blocking and allow active-active forwarding.

---

### 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Core Layer:** Two Chassis-grade switches running **VSX**. This provides a single logical entity with two physical control planes.
*   **Access Layer:** Switches on floors with high density (Floors 2, 3, 4) will be configured in **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacks. This reduces management IP overhead and simplifies cabling.
*   **Link Aggregation:** 
    *   **Access to Core:** Each floor stack connects to *both* Core switches via a **2x10Gbps LACP Bundle (LAG)**.
    *   **Inter-Switch Links (ISL):** High-bandwidth synchronization links between VSX peers.
*   **Failover:** **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol)** will be used for gateway redundancy for all VLANs.

---

### 5. QoS Strategy (HPE/Aruba Standards)
To ensure that Voice and Video traffic are not dropped during high-density student usage (e.g., Floor 4), the following QoS policy is implemented:
1.  **Strict Priority Queuing:** VoIP (VLAN 100) is assigned to the highest priority queue (Priority Queue 7).
2.  **Weighted Round Robin (WRR):** IPTV and Security cameras are assigned to the secondary queue to ensure smooth video streaming without starving the data plane.
3.  **Rate Limiting:** Student VLANs are subject to per-port bandwidth policing to prevent a single user from consuming the entire 10Gbps uplink.

---

### 6. Redundancy & Failover Summary
*   **Physical Redundancy:** Dual-homed uplinks from every floor to two separate core switches.
*   **Logical Redundancy:** VSX maintains a synchronized state between core switches; if Core A fails, Core B takes over the LACP bundle and Gateway (VRRP) instantly without flapping.
*   **Power Redundancy:** Core switches utilize Dual Hot-Swappable PSUs connected to independent UPS circuits.
*   **Loop Prevention:** LACP handles the multi-pathing; VSX removes the need for STP on the core-to-access links, enabling all 20Gbps (2x10) of bandwidth to be utilized.

*   **Core Layer:** To support **VSX** and high availability for the entire PG Block, the **Aruba CX 6400 Series** is selected. This chassis-based system provides the required redundancy, high-density 10G/25G/100G uplinks, and the power capacity to handle the aggregated load of 7 floors.
*   **Access Layer:** For high-density floors (2-4), I have selected the **Aruba CX 6300M**.
