## Phase 2: Network Topology

# Campus Network Topology Design

## 1. Topology Overview & Architectural Choice
**Selected Model:** **3-Tier (Core, Distribution, Access)**

**Justification:** 
The network supports **643 total endpoints** (exceeding the 500-endpoint threshold for 2-tier designs) across two separate buildings and five distinct floors. A 3-tier architecture is required to ensure high performance and scalability, allowing each building to function as a modular distribution block. This prevents spanning-tree diameter issues and provides a dedicated aggregation layer to enforce security policies (ACLs) before traffic reaches the core.

## 3. High Availability & Link Design
### Redundancy Strategy
*   **Core Layer:** Deploy a pair of HPE Aruba CX 8360/8400 switches utilizing **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This creates a logical chassis for a single default gateway (Active-Gateway) and eliminates STP blocking.
*   **Distribution Layer:** One pair of VSX-enabled switches per building to aggregate access switches.
*   **Access Layer:** Switches deployed in **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacks to simplify management and provide link redundancy via LACP.

### Link Specifications
*   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** 2x 25GbE or 40GbE Fiber LAG bundles.
*   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** 2x 10GbE SFP+ Fiber uplinks (LACP).
*   **High-Density Areas:** In the **CS Dept** and **Main Library**, 2.5GbE/5GbE **Smart Rate (Multi-Gig)** ports are mandatory to support Wi-Fi 6/6E APs and avoid bottlenecks.
*   **QoS:** Strict Priority Queueing for VOIP (DSCP 46/EF) and IPTV (DSCP 34/AF41) to prevent jitter during peak student traffic.

## 5. Redundancy & Failover Logic
*   **Default Gateway:** Implemented via **VSX Active-Gateway** at the Core/Distribution level. This provides a single Virtual IP (VIP) for endpoints while allowing both switches to forward traffic actively.
*   **Loop Prevention:** Since VSX/VSF eliminates most blocking ports, **MSTP (Multiple Spanning Tree Protocol)** will be configured as a failsafe backup.
*   **Inter-Building Failover:** Dual-homed fiber paths between Building 1 and Building 2 to ensure that if one fiber run is severed, the distribution blocks remain connected to the core.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the provided topology and detailed datasheet specifications for the HPE Aruba Networking CX portfolio, here is the recommended Bill of Materials.

### Hardware Selection Strategy
1.  **Core Layer:** The **CX 8360** is selected for the core. It supports **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for high availability and provides the necessary 25G/100G capacity to aggregate building distribution blocks.
2.  **Distribution Layer:** The **CX 6400** modular series is selected. It allows for a mix of high-density 1G/10G modules and supports **VSX** to ensure no single point of failure between the access and core layers.
3.  **Access Layer:**
    *   **Standard Zones:** **CX 6200M** switches are used. They support **VSF stacking** (up to 8 members), providing the required redundancy and simplified management.
    *   **High-Density Zones (CS Dept & Library):** **CX 6300L** switches are deployed. These provide **Smart Rate (Multi-Gig 1/2.5/5G)** ports essential for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs to prevent bottlenecks.
    *   **Low-Density/Admin:** **CX 6100** is used where high-performance stacking or L3 routing at the edge is not required, maintaining cost-effectiveness.

*   **Port Density:** 
    *   **B1 Floor 2 (CS):** $7 \text{ switches} \times 48 \text{ ports} = 336 \text{ ports}$ (Required: 285). $\checkmark$
    *   **B2 Floor 1 (Lib):** $6 \text{ switches} \times 48 \text{ ports} = 288 \text{ ports}$ (Required: 267). $\checkmark$
*   **PoE Budget:** The selected CX 6300L and 6200M models provide high-wattage PoE (up to Class 8 / 90W) to support the latest Wi-Fi 6E APs and IPTV devices without oversubscription.
*   **Uptime:** 
    *   **Core $\to$ Distribution:** VSX active-active pair.
    *   **Distribution $\to$ Access:** Dual-homed 10G/25G uplinks.
    *   **Access:** VSF stacking prevents a single switch failure from dropping an entire floor.
*   **Performance:** Smart Rate ports in CS and Library zones ensure that 5Gbps APs are not throttled to 1Gbps, fulfilling the "high-density wireless" requirement.
