## Phase 1: Rephrased Prompt

**Role:** Senior Network Topology Designer
**Objective:** Design a comprehensive, scalable, and resilient campus-wide network infrastructure for a university consisting of five (5) distinct buildings.

**1. Project Scope & Purpose**
The primary purpose of this network is to support a high-density academic environment. The design must facilitate seamless connectivity for students, faculty, administrative staff, and IoT devices, supporting diverse workloads including distance learning, research data transfers, and administrative operations.

**2. Site Topology & Physical Constraints**
*   **Infrastructure:** 5 Buildings (Assumed: 1 Central Administration/Data Center building and 4 Academic/Residential buildings).
*   **Campus Layout:** Design a hierarchical structure (Core, Distribution, and Access layers). 
*   **Connectivity:** Specify the backbone cabling (e.g., Single-Mode Fiber) connecting the buildings to the core.
*   **Internal Layout:** Each building is assumed to have 3-4 floors, requiring dedicated Intermediate Distribution Frames (IDFs) per floor.

**3. End-Device & User Requirements**
*   **User Density:** High. Assume an average of 500-1,000 users per building.
*   **Device Types:** 
    *   Laptops/Smartphones (Wi-Fi 6/6E enabled).
    *   Desktop workstations in labs and offices.
    *   IP Phones (VoIP).
    *   IoT devices (Security cameras, smart HVAC, access control).
    *   High-performance computing (HPC) clusters in designated research areas.

**4. Performance & Reliability Specifications**
*   **Bandwidth:** 
    *   10Gbps or 40Gbps uplinks between Distribution and Core layers.
    *   1Gbps to the desktop/end-device.
    *   High-throughput wireless coverage throughout all common areas and classrooms.
*   **Latency:** Low-latency optimization for VoIP and video conferencing.
*   **Redundancy:** Implement a "No Single Point of Failure" strategy. Use redundant core switches and multi-homed links (LACP/EtherChannel) between buildings to ensure high availability.

**5. Logical Architecture & Security**
*   **Segmentation:** Use VLANs to logically separate traffic (e.g., Student, Faculty, Admin, Guest, and IoT networks).
*   **Security:** 
    *   Implement Network Access Control (NAC) for device authentication.
    *   Deploy perimeter firewalls with Deep Packet Inspection (DPI) at the campus edge.
    *   Internal zoning to isolate sensitive administrative data from the general student Wi-Fi.
*   **IP Addressing:** Propose a scalable IPv4 subnetting scheme (VLSM) or an IPv6 transition plan.

**6. Budget & Hardware Tier**
*   **Tier:** Enterprise Grade. 
*   **Preference:** Use industry-standard hardware (e.g., Cisco, Aruba, or Juniper) capable of supporting software-defined networking (SDN) for centralized management.

**Deliverables Required:**
1.  **Logical Topology Diagram:** Showing the Core, Distribution, and Access layers.
2.  **Physical Topology Map:** Detailing fiber runs between the five buildings.
3.  **VLAN Assignment Table:** Defining the subnetting and purpose of each segment.
4.  **Hardware Bill of Materials (BOM):** Recommended switch and AP specifications.

## Phase 2: Network Topology & Bill of Materials

### 1. Topology Overview
**Type:** Hierarchical 3-Tier Architecture (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access).
The design employs a **Collapsed Core/Distribution** model in the Central Admin building and a **Distributed Access** model in the academic buildings. To eliminate Spanning Tree Protocol (STP) blocking and ensure maximum throughput, **VSX** is used at the Core/Distribution level and **VSF** is used at the Access level.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown
*   **Core Layer (Central Admin Bldg):** 2x Ultra-High Performance Chassis Switches.
*   **Distribution Layer (Each Bldg):** 2x Aggregation Switches per building (10 total).
*   **Access Layer (Per Floor/IDF):** 2-3x Stackable PoE+ Switches per floor $\times$ 4 floors $\times$ 5 buildings ($\approx$ 40-60 switches).

---

### 3. High-Availability Design
*   **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension):** Implemented at the Core and Distribution layers. This provides an active-active control plane, allowing for hitless failover and eliminating STP reliance for inter-switch links.
*   **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework):** Implemented at the Access layer. Switches are logically stacked to be managed as a single entity, simplifying administration and allowing for cross-stack EtherChannel.
*   **LAG (LACP):** All uplinks are bundled into LACP aggregates (Multi-chassis LAG) to provide increased bandwidth and instantaneous failover.
*   **QoS:** 
    *   **Strict Priority Queuing:** Reserved for VoIP (EF) and Video Conferencing (AF41).
    *   **Weighted Round Robin (WRR):** Applied to Academic and Research data to prevent starvation.
    *   **Scavenger Class:** Applied to Guest Wi-Fi to ensure they do not impact critical operations.

---

### 4. Link Design
*   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** Dual 40Gbps SMF (Single Mode Fiber) links per building, bundled via LACP.
*   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** Dual 10Gbps SMF/MMF links per IDF, bundled via LACP.
*   **Access $\leftrightarrow$ End Device:** 1Gbps Copper (RJ45) / 2.5Gbps-5Gbps (Multi-gig) for Wi-Fi 6E APs.

### 6. Redundancy & Failover
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** VRRP/HSRP is bypassed by **VSX**, which provides a single virtual IP (VIP) and active-active forwarding for the default gateway.
*   **Dual-Homing:** Every Access stack is dual-homed to both Distribution switches via LACP.
*   **Pathing:** All fiber runs are routed through diverse physical conduits to prevent a "single backhoe" failure.

### Design Alignment Notes:
*   **High Availability:** The use of **CX 6400** and **CX 6300M** ensures the implementation of **VSX** (Virtual Switching Extension) at the Core and Distribution layers for active-active forwarding.
*   **Scalability:** The **CX 6300M** series at the access layer utilizes **VSF** (Virtual Switching Framework), allowing up to 10 switches to be managed as a single entity, as requested in the BOM.
*   **Performance:** Multi-gigabit (Smart Rate) ports on the access switches specifically support the higher throughput needs of Wi-Fi 6E Access Points, preventing bottlenecks at the edge.
*   **QoS:** All recommended CX switches support robust hardware-based QoS, enabling the Strict Priority and WRR queuing required for VoIP and Research data.
