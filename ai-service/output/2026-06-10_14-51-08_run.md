## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have analyzed your requirements. Based on the total endpoint count (~650+ devices) and the multi-building layout, I have designed the following high-availability architecture using the latest HPE Aruba Networking (AOS-CX) standards.

### 1. Topology Overview
**Chosen Model: 3-Tier (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access)**

**Justification:** 
With over 650 initial endpoints and a growth projection of up to 200 more, the network exceeds the recommended 500-endpoint threshold for a collapsed core. A 3-tier model is necessary to provide dedicated distribution blocks for each building, ensuring that failure domains are isolated and traffic from high-density areas (like the Computer Science Dept) does not congest the Core.

### 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Core Layer (MDF):** Deploy a pair of **Aruba CX 8360** switches utilizing **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This provides a distributed control plane, allowing for hitless upgrades and zero-second failover.
*   **Distribution Layer (Building IDFs):** Each building will have a pair of **Aruba CX 6400** switches in a VSX pair.
*   **Access Layer (Floor IDFs):** **Aruba CX 6300M** switches configured in **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacks.
*   **Link Design:**
    *   **Core to Distribution:** $2 \times 25\text{GbE}$ or $40\text{GbE}$ Fiber LAG bundles (LACP).
    *   **Distribution to Access:** $2 \times 10\text{GbE}$ Fiber uplinks from each VSF stack member to the VSX distribution pair (Multi-chassis LAG).
    *   **Smart Rate (Multi-Gig):** The **Computer Science Dept (B1-F2)** and **Research Labs (B2-F2)** will utilize **Aruba CX 6300M Smart Rate ports** (up to 5Gbps) to support Wi-Fi 6/6E APs and high-performance workstations.
*   **Cabling:** OS2 Single-mode Fiber for inter-building/backbone; Cat6a for horizontal runs to support 10Gbps and PoE++ for WAPs.

### 5. Redundancy, Failover & Security
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** Utilize **VSX Active-Gateway**. This provides a single virtual MAC/IP for the default gateway across the core/distribution pair, eliminating the need for VRRP and preventing suboptimal "hair-pinning" of traffic.
*   **RBAC & High-Security Zones:**
    *   **Principal & Finance Head:** Assigned to a dedicated "Executive" VLAN with a stateful firewall policy allowing access to the Finance Server, while blocking access from the Student/Staff VLANs.
    *   **Server Room:** Isolated via a dedicated VRF (Virtual Routing and Forwarding) instance to ensure complete routing table separation from the rest of the campus.
    *   **Library:** Implement **MAC-based authentication (802.1X)** for wired ports in the Library to prevent unauthorized hardware from connecting.
*   **QoS Implementation:** 
    *   **Strict Priority Queue:** Assigned to VOIP (EF) to eliminate jitter.
    *   **Weighted Round Robin (WRR):** Used to balance IPTV (Video) and General Data, ensuring video streams do not drop during peak student web usage.

### **Technical Justification & Calculations**

#### **1. Port Density & Growth**
*   **Calculation Formula:** $\text{Total Ports} = (\text{Users} + \text{VoIP} + \text{IPTV} + \text{APs}) \times 1.2$
*   **B1-F2 (CS Dept):** $(200+20+2+5+2 + 10\text{ APs}) = 239$ endpoints $\rightarrow 286$ ports. 
    *   *Provisioned:* $6 \times 48 = 288$ ports. (**Matches**)
*   **B2-F1 (Library):** $(200+10+1+2+1 + 9\text{ APs}) = 223$ endpoints $\rightarrow 268$ ports. 
    *   *Provisioned:* $6 \times 48 = 288$ ports. (**Matches**)

#### **2. Power over Ethernet (PoE)**
*   **High-Power Zones:** The **CX 6300M/L** series supports **IEEE 802.3bt (Class 8)** up to 90W per port. This is critical for the high-density Wi-Fi 6E APs in the Computer Science and Research areas, as well as modern IPTV/Security cameras.
*   **Redundancy:** All modular switches (6400, 8360) utilize hot-swappable redundant power supplies to meet the "Uptime is Important" requirement.

#### **3. High Availability (HA)**
*   **Core/Distribution:** **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** is used. Unlike traditional stacking, VSX maintains independent control planes, allowing for **Live Upgrades** (zero-downtime firmware updates) and active-active forwarding.
*   **Access:** **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** is used to manage floor switches as a single logical entity, simplifying management while providing link redundancy to the distribution layer.

#### **4. Speed & Performance**
*   **Backbone:** 100GbE links between Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution.
*   **Uplinks:** 25GbE/50GbE links between Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access (using SFP28/SFP56).
*   **Edge:** Smart Rate (1/2.5/5GbE) is deployed specifically in the CS Dept and Research Labs to eliminate bottlenecks for Wi-Fi 6 APs and high-end workstations.
