## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Selected Model:** **3-Tier (Core, Distribution, Access)**

**Justification:** 
The campus consists of two separate buildings with a total of approximately 640+ endpoints (including projections). A 3-tier architecture is required to ensure high-performance traffic aggregation and clear policy enforcement between the academic and research zones. By utilizing a dedicated distribution layer for each building, we minimize the blast radius of local failures and provide the scalability necessary to accommodate the projected growth of 200 additional users without impacting the core.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Port Density
Calculations include: **(Users + VoIP + IPTV + 1 AP per 25 users) * 1.2 growth margin**.

#### **Building 1: Main Academic Block**
*   **Floor 1 (Admin):** $(20+15+3+6+2) + \lceil 38/25 \rceil \text{ APs} = 48 \text{ endpoints}$. $\text{Ports Req} = 48 \times 1.2 \approx 58$.
    *   **Hardware:** $2 \times 48\text{-port PoE+ Switches}$ (One for data, one for high-density/redundancy).
*   **Floor 2 (CS Dept):** $(200+20+2+5+2) + \lceil 229/25 \rceil \text{ APs} = 238 \text{ endpoints}$. $\text{Ports Req} = 238 \times 1.2 \approx 286$.
    *   **Hardware:** $7 \times 48\text{-port Smart Rate (mGig) PoE+ Switches}$ (Required for Wi-Fi 6E backhaul).
*   **Floor 3 (Electronics):** $(150+12+1+4+1) + \lceil 168/25 \rceil \text{ APs} = 175 \text{ endpoints}$. $\text{Ports Req} = 175 \times 1.2 \approx 210$.
    *   **Hardware:** $5 \times 48\text{-port PoE+ Switches}$.

#### **Building 2: Library & Research**
*   **Floor 1 (Library):** $(200+10+1+2+1) + \lceil 214/25 \rceil \text{ APs} = 223 \text{ endpoints}$. $\text{Ports Req} = 223 \times 1.2 \approx 268$.
    *   **Hardware:** $6 \times 48\text{-port Smart Rate (mGig) PoE+ Switches}$ (High density student area).
*   **Floor 2 (Research):** $(50+8+1+1+0) + \lceil 60/25 \rceil \text{ APs} = 63 \text{ endpoints}$. $\text{Ports Req} = 63 \times 1.2 \approx 76$.
    *   **Hardware:** $2 \times 48\text{-port PoE+ Switches}$.

### 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Core/Distribution Layer:** Deployment of **Aruba CX 8300/6400 series** using **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This provides a dual-control plane for hitless upgrades and seamless failover.
*   **Access Layer:** **Aruba CX 6200/6300 series** switches configured in **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacks. This allows multiple physical switches to be managed as a single logical unit.
*   **Link Speeds & Redundancy:**
    *   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** $2 \times 40\text{Gbps}$ (QSFP+) LAG bundles.
    *   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** $2 \times 10\text{Gbps}$ (SFP+) LACP bundles (Multi-chassis LAG for VSX/VSF).
    *   **High-Density Areas:** Use **Smart Rate (1/2.5/5Gbps)** ports for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs to prevent bottlenecks.
*   **QoS:** Strict Priority Queuing for **VOIP (EF class)** and **IPTV (AF41 class)** to ensure zero jitter.

The Core/Distribution layers will utilize **VSX Active-Gateway**. This provides a single virtual IP (VIP) for the default gateway across both switches in the VSX pair, ensuring sub-second failover without the convergence delays of VRRP.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

This design provides a highly available, scalable, and high-performance campus network tailored to the specific needs of the Academic and Research blocks.

### 1. Hardware Selection Logic
*   **Core/Distribution Layer:** The **CX 6400 Series** is selected for its modularity and support for **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**, providing a hitless dual-control plane for the campus backbone.
*   **High-Density Access (Smart Rate):** The **CX 6300L Series** (e.g., S3L76A) is used for floors with high student density (CS Dept, Library). These support **Smart Rate (1/2.5/5GbE)** to prevent bottlenecks for Wi-Fi 6E APs and support **Class 8 PoE (up to 90W)** for future-proofing.
*   **Standard Access:** The **CX 6200F Series** is used for administration and research labs. It provides a cost-effective, stackable (VSF) solution with 10G uplinks and PoE+.
*   **Redundancy:** All access stacks utilize **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)**, while the core utilizes **VSX**, ensuring that no single switch failure disconnects a floor or a building.

### 3. Technical Implementation Details

**A. High Availability (HA) Strategy**
*   **Core $\rightarrow$ Dist:** Each building's distribution layer connects to the CX 6400 VSX pair using **40Gbps/100Gbps LACP bundles**.
*   **Dist $\rightarrow$ Access:** Access stacks (VSF) connect to the Core/Dist via **Dual 10Gbps SFP+ uplinks** using Multi-chassis LAG (MC-LAG) for sub-second failover.

**B. PoE & Power Budgeting**
*   **Standard Zones:** Using CX 6200F with 740W budget ensures ample power for VoIP phones (approx. 15W) and standard APs.
*   **Smart Rate Zones:** CX 6300L supports **IEEE 802.3bt Class 8 (90W)**, ensuring that high-performance Wi-Fi 6E APs do not suffer from power-limited features (like reduced radio chains).

**C. Security & Segmentation**
*   **Dynamic Segmentation:** Leveraging the CX series' native support for **User-Based Tunneling (UBT)** and **Colorless Ports**, the network will automatically assign users to VLANs 10, 20, 30, or 40 based on RADIUS roles.
*   **Critical Security:** 802.1X port-based authentication will be enforced in the **Research Labs** and **Server Room** to prevent unauthorized physical access.
*   **Admin Protection:** Finance and Principal traffic is isolated via **VLAN 40** with strict L3 ACLs enforced at the Core.

**D. QoS Configuration**
*   **VoIP (VLAN 50):** Mapped to **EF (Expedited Forwarding)** with Strict Priority queuing.
*   **IPTV (VLAN 60):** Mapped to **AF41**, ensuring high throughput for media streams without impacting voice.
