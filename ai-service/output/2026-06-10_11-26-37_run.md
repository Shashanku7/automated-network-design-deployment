## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview & Tier Selection
**Selected Architecture:** **3-Tier (Core, Distribution, Access)**

**Justification:** 
The network supports approximately **644 total endpoints** (users, phones, IPTV) spread across two separate buildings and five floors. A 3-tier model is necessary to ensure high-performance traffic aggregation at the distribution layer, providing a clear failure domain boundary between buildings and allowing for the requested 20% growth margin (up to 800+ endpoints) without saturating the core.

### 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Core Layer:** Deployed as an **Aruba CX 8320/8360 pair** utilizing **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This provides a single logical control plane with active-active forwarding.
*   **Distribution Layer:** Each building has a dedicated distribution pair (CX 6300M) in **VSF stack** (or VSX for higher criticality) to aggregate floor access switches.
*   **Access Layer:** Switches are grouped in **VSF Stacks** per floor. Each stack has a **Dual-Homed LACP (LAG)** uplink (2 x 10Gbps) connecting to both Distribution switches.
*   **Multi-Gigabit Requirements:** 
    *   **Computer Science Dept (B1-F2)** and **Main Library (B2-F1)**: Must use **Aruba CX 6300M Smart Rate (2.5G/5G)** ports for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs to prevent bottlenecks.
*   **Redundancy:** All inter-building links are 10Gbps Fiber (OM4) in LAG bundles.

### 5. Redundancy & Failover Logic
*   **Default Gateway:** Implemented via **VSX Active-Gateway**. This allows both Core/Distribution switches to route traffic for the same virtual IP/MAC simultaneously, eliminating the convergence delay associated with VRRP.
*   **Loop Prevention:** VSX handles the loop-free topology at the core. Access layer VSF stacks utilize LACP for multi-chassis link aggregation, rendering Spanning Tree (MSTP) as a secondary fail-safe only.
*   **QoS Mapping:** 
    *   **Voice (VLAN 100):** Strict Priority Queue (DSCP 46/EF).
    *   **IPTV (VLAN 110):** Multicast routing enabled via PIM-SM; assigned to High Priority queue.
    *   **Student Traffic:** Rate-limited via QoS policies to ensure Admin/Staff bandwidth during peak hours.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the provided network topology and the technical specifications retrieved from the HPE Aruba Networking datasheets, here is the detailed Bill of Materials (BoM) and hardware selection.

### Hardware Selection Logic
1.  **Core Layer**: The **CX 8320** is selected for its 2.5Tbps switching capacity and native **VSX** support, ensuring a non-blocking, high-availability core that supports the active-active forwarding required for the campus.
2.  **Distribution Layer**: The **CX 6300M** is used for its robust Layer 3 capabilities and VSF/VSX flexibility. It acts as the aggregation point for floor stacks, providing the necessary 10G/25G uplink capacity.
3.  **Access Layer (Standard)**: The **CX 6200F** is chosen for general floors. It supports **VSF stacking** (up to 8 members) and provides a cost-effective PoE+ solution with 10G uplinks.
4.  **Access Layer (High-Performance/Smart Rate)**: For the Computer Science Dept (B1-F2) and Main Library (B2-F1), the **CX 6300L** (S3L76A/S3L77A) is selected. These models provide **Smart Rate (1G/2.5G/5G)** ports essential for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs to prevent bottlenecks.
5.  **PoE Budget**: High-wattage models (740W+) are selected for floors with high AP and VOIP density to ensure the PoE budget exceeds the estimated device draw.
