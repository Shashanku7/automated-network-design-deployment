## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed the following infrastructure for the PG Block. Given the high density of the upper floors (CSE, AIML, Data Science), this design utilizes a **Collapsed Core/Distribution Architecture** using **HPE Aruba** standards (VSX/VSF) to ensure non-blocking performance and high availability.

---

### 1. Topology Overview
*   **Topology Type:** Hierarchical Star (Collapsed Core).
*   **Physical Structure:** A redundant Core/Distribution pair located in the Building 1 MDF (Main Distribution Frame), with a dedicated IDF (Intermediate Distribution Frame) on every floor.
*   **Logic:** Every floor acts as an isolated Access Block, uplinking via redundant fiber to the Core.

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Hardware Sizing
To account for the **20% growth margin** and the mix of devices (Laptops, PCs, VoIP, IPTV/Cameras), the access layer is sized as follows:

| Floor | Dept | Total Users | Port Requirement (+20%) | Access Switch Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | Admin | 32 | $\approx 40$ | 1x 48-Port PoE+ Switch |
| **2** | Library | 64 | $\approx 80$ | 2x 48-Port PoE+ Switches (VSF Stack) |
| **3** | 1st Year | 101 | $\approx 125$ | 3x 48-Port PoE+ Switches (VSF Stack) |
| **4** | ECE | 128 | $\approx 155$ | 4x 48-Port PoE+ Switches (VSF Stack) |
| **5** | CSE | 216 | $\approx 260$ | 6x 48-Port PoE+ Switches (VSF Stack) |
| **6** | AIML | 326 | $\approx 395$ | 9x 48-Port PoE+ Switches (VSF Stack) |
| **7** | Data Science | 366 | $\approx 440$ | 10x 48-Port PoE+ Switches (VSF Stack) |

### 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Core Redundancy (VSX):** Two Enterprise Core Switches configured in **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This allows for a single logical chassis with active-active forwarding, eliminating Spanning Tree (STP) blocked ports.
*   **Access Redundancy (VSF):** Access switches on each floor are configured in **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacks. This allows multiple physical switches to be managed as one, providing slot-level redundancy.
*   **Link Aggregation:** 
    *   **Access to Core:** Each floor stack connects to *both* Core switches via **LACP (802.3ad) Multi-chassis EtherChannel**.
    *   **Speeds:** 2x 10Gbps SFP+ Fiber uplinks per stack (20Gbps aggregate) to handle high-density traffic from AIML/DS floors.
*   **Cabling:** OM4 Multimode Fiber for vertical risers; Cat6A for horizontal runs to endpoints.

### 4. VLAN & Subnetting Plan
To ensure isolation and security, we use a **Per-Floor-Per-Department** VLAN strategy. This limits broadcast domains and prevents a broadcast storm on Floor 7 from affecting the Admin office on Floor 1.

**Global QoS Marking (HPE/Aruba Standard):**
*   **VoIP:** EF (Expedited Forwarding / CoS 5)
*   **IPTV/Video:** AF41 (CoS 4)
*   **Critical Admin:** AF31 (CoS 3)
*   **Students/General:** Default (CoS 0)

| Floor | Dept | Student VLAN | Staff VLAN | Admin VLAN | VoIP VLAN | IPTV/Cam VLAN | Subnet Mask |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Admin | N/A | 110 | 100 | 101 | 102 | /24 |
| 2 | Library | 210 | 220 | 200 | 201 | 202 | /23 (Stud) |
| 3 | 1st Year | 310 | 320 | 300 | 301 | 302 | /23 (Stud) |
| 4 | ECE | 410 | 420 | 400 | 401 | 402 | /23 (Stud) |
| 5 | CSE | 510 | 520 | 500 | 501 | 502 | /22 (Stud) |
| 6 | AIML | 610 | 620 | 600 | 601 | 602 | /22 (Stud) |
| 7 | DS | 710 | 720 | 700 | 701 | 702 | /22 (Stud) |

*Subnet Sizing: /22 provides 1022 hosts, accommodating the ~440 users in Data Science plus growth and mobile devices.*

### 5. Redundancy & Failover Logic
*   **Gateway Redundancy:** **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol)** or **VSX Synchronized Gateway** is used at the Core. If Core A fails, Core B assumes the Gateway IP instantly without dropping client sessions.
*   **Power Redundancy:** All Core and Access switches must utilize dual hot-swappable Power Supply Units (PSUs) connected to separate UPS circuits.
*   **WiFi Failover:** WAPs are deployed in a "honeycomb" pattern. If one WAP fails, the adjacent WAPs automatically increase transmission power (Adaptive Radio Management) to cover the dead zone.

### 6. Security Implementation
*   **Sensitive Zone (Library & Server Room):**
    *   **Micro-segmentation:** Use ACLs at the Distribution layer to block Student VLANs from accessing Admin/Server VLANs.
    *   **MAC-Based Authentication:** Only pre-approved Library kiosks and Server consoles are allowed on these specific ports.
*   **Edge Security:** 
    *   **802.1X (RADIUS/TACACS+):** Mandatory authentication for all wired and wireless ports. Users are dynamically assigned to their VLAN based on their credentials.
    *   **DHCP Snooping & Dynamic ARP Inspection (DAI):** Enabled on all access ports to prevent Rogue DHCP servers and Man-in-the-Middle attacks.
    *   **Port Security:** Limit each access port to a maximum of 2 MAC addresses to prevent unauthorized hub/switch attachment.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

As a Network Hardware Specialist, I have analyzed your collapsed core topology for the PG Block. Based on the retrieved HPE Aruba datasheets, I have selected hardware that balances high-performance non-blocking throughput for the high-density floors (AIML, Data Science) with cost-effectiveness for the lower-density floors.

### Hardware Selection Logic
1.  **Core Layer:** I have selected the **Aruba CX 6300M (S0G03A)**. It supports **VSX** (Virtual Switching Extension), providing the required high availability and active-active forwarding. Its 10G SFP+ ports perfectly match the required 20Gbps aggregate uplinks.
2.  **Access Layer (High Density):** For floors 5, 6, and 7, I have selected the **Aruba CX 6300M (JL661A)**. These support **VSF stacking** (up to 10 members) and provide Class 4 PoE for VoIP/Cameras, with the scalability needed for 400+ users.
3.  **Access Layer (Low/Medium Density):** For floors 1–4, I have selected the **Aruba CX 6200F (JL728B)**. These are more cost-effective but still support VSF stacking and high-power PoE (740W) to ensure all phones and APs are powered without bottlenecking.
4.  **Wireless:** Based on the user count (~1233 total), I have calculated the AP quantity using a ratio of **~30 users per AP**. This ensures high-quality coverage and accommodates the "honeycomb" failover pattern mentioned in the topology.
