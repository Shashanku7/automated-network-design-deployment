## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed the following campus network for BMS College of Engineering (BMSCE).

### 1. Topology Overview & Tier Selection
**Chosen Model:** **3-Tier Architecture (Core, Distribution, Access)**

**Justification:** 
The campus exceeds 1,900 total endpoints across three separate buildings and nine floors. A 3-tier model is mandatory here to isolate fault domains per building, provide dedicated distribution blocks to aggregate high-density student traffic, and ensure the Core remains a high-speed L3 transport without being burdened by L2 processing or local policy enforcement.

### 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Core Layer:** Deploy a pair of **HPE Aruba 8300/8400 series** switches using **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This provides a dual-control plane for near-zero downtime during upgrades.
*   **Distribution Layer:** Each building will have a VSX pair of **Aruba 8320 series** switches.
*   **Access Layer:** Switches will be stacked using **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** to simplify management and provide link redundancy.
*   **Link Speeds:**
    *   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** $2 \times 25\text{Gbps}$ or $40\text{Gbps}$ L3 LAG bundles (Single-mode Fiber).
    *   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** $2 \times 10\text{Gbps}$ LACP bundles.
    *   **Smart Rate (Multi-Gig):** In Building 2 (AI & DS, ECE) and Building 1 (MCA), access switches must support **Smart Rate (1/2.5/5Gbps)** ports to prevent bottlenecks for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs.

### 5. Redundancy & Failover Strategy
*   **Default Gateway:** Use **VSX Active-Gateway** at the Distribution layer. This provides a virtual IP (VIP) and virtual MAC (vMAC) shared between the VSX pair, allowing active-active forwarding.
*   **Loop Prevention:** Since VSX LAGs are used, Spanning Tree is disabled on those links. **Loop-Protect** will be enabled on all access ports to prevent accidental loops from student-connected hubs/switches.
*   **L3 Routing:** OSPF will be used between the Core and Distribution layers for rapid convergence and ECMP (Equal-Cost Multi-Path) load balancing.

### 6. Wireless Strategy
*   **High-Density Placement:** 
    *   **Building 2 (AI & DS/Library):** Deploy APs in a "honeycomb" pattern with a maximum radius of 15 meters to handle the 400+ student density.
    *   **Building 3 (Workshops):** Deploy ruggedized APs in workshop areas to handle interference from machinery.
*   **Onboarding:** Implement a **Captive Portal** via Aruba ClearPass for Guest Wi-Fi and 802.1X (WPA3-Enterprise) for Students/Staff.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

As a Senior Network Architect, I have finalized the hardware selection and Bill of Materials (BOM) for the BMS College of Engineering (BMSCE) campus network. This design adheres to the 3-Tier hierarchical model, ensuring high availability via VSX and VSF, and provides the necessary port density and PoE budgets to support the calculated endpoint and AP requirements.

### 1. Logical Topology Description
*   **Core Layer:** A high-performance pair of **Aruba CX 8360** switches configured in **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This creates a redundant, non-blocking L3 backbone. All Distribution blocks connect to this core via 25Gbps/40Gbps L3 LAG bundles.
*   **Distribution Layer:** Each building is served by a VSX pair of **Aruba CX 8360** (or 8320) switches. These act as the L3 gateway (Active-Gateway) for all floor VLANs, isolating broadcast domains and aggregating traffic from the access layer.
*   **Access Layer:** Switches are deployed in each floor's IDF. They are stacked using **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** to provide a single management IP and link redundancy. High-density areas (AI & DS, ECE, MCA) utilize **Smart Rate** switches to support Wi-Fi 6/6E APs.
