## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Architectural Model:** 3-Tier (Core, Distribution, Access)

**Justification:** 
The campus consists of three distinct buildings with a total of ~2,000 endpoints, far exceeding the 500-endpoint threshold for a collapsed core. A 3-tier architecture is mandatory to provide dedicated distribution blocks for each building, ensuring that localized traffic (e.g., high-density lab traffic in the New Academic Block) does not saturate the campus core and allows for seamless scalability to accommodate the planned 400 additional nodes.

### 3. High-Availability & Redundancy
*   **Core/Distribution Layer:** Utilize **Aruba VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This provides a logically single switch with a distributed control plane, enabling active-active forwarding.
*   **Access Layer:** Utilize **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacking for the floor-level switches to simplify management and provide local redundancy.
*   **Link Aggregation:** All uplinks from Access $\rightarrow$ Distribution and Distribution $\rightarrow$ Core must use **LACP (802.3ad)** bundles for bandwidth aggregation and failover.
*   **Default Gateway:** Implement **VSX Active-Gateway**. This ensures that both VSX peers can route traffic for the same subnet simultaneously without relying on VRRP hello timers, providing instantaneous failover.

---

### 4. Link Design & Performance
*   **Core to Distribution:** Dual 40GbE/100GbE fiber links (OS2 Single Mode) in an LACP bundle.
*   **Distribution to Access:** Dual 10GbE/25GbE fiber links.
*   **Smart Rate (Multi-Gigabit):**
    *   **Requirement:** All Wi-Fi 6/6E APs in the Central Library (Bldg 2, F1) and CSE/AI Labs (Bldg 2, F2) must be connected via **HPE Aruba Smart Rate (2.5G/5G)** ports to prevent bottlenecking the wireless throughput.
*   **QoS Markings:**
    *   **VoIP:** EF (Expedited Forwarding) / CoS 5.
    *   **IPTV/Surveillance:** AF41 (Assured Forwarding) / CoS 4.
    *   **Management:** CS2 / CoS 2.

### 3. Technical Implementation Details

#### **High Availability & Redundancy**
*   **Core Redundancy:** Two CX 8360s configured in a **VSX pair**. This enables an **Active-Gateway**, allowing the distribution switches to use LACP bundles across both core switches without Spanning Tree blocks.
*   **Access Redundancy:** All floor switches are configured in **VSF stacks**. This allows multiple physical switches to be managed as a single logical entity, reducing management overhead and providing port-level redundancy.
*   **Cabling:** 
    *   Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution: $2 \times 100\text{GbE}$ OS2 Single-Mode Fiber (LACP).
    *   Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access: $2 \times 10\text{GbE}$ or $25\text{GbE}$ (SFP28) LACP bundles.

#### **Wireless Integration (Wi-Fi 6/6E)**
To prevent the "Wireless Bottleneck," the **CX 6300M** series was deployed in Building 2 (Library/CSE). These switches support the **IEEE 802.3bz (Smart Rate)** standard, delivering 2.5Gbps or 5Gbps to APs, ensuring the multi-gigabit capability of Wi-Fi 6E is fully utilized.

#### **QoS & Security Policy**
*   **Voice/Video:** Prioritized using **DSCP EF (Expedited Forwarding)** for VoIP and **AF41** for IPTV, ensuring zero jitter during critical academic windows.
*   **Segmentation:** 
    *   **Data Center (Bldg 1, F1):** Isolated using **VRF (Virtual Routing and Forwarding)** on the CX 6400 distribution layer, creating a complete routing table separation between the DC and the general campus.
    *   **Library/Research:** Implementation of **Dynamic Segmentation** via Aruba ClearPass, where the switch identifies the user role and assigns the appropriate VLAN/ACL dynamically, regardless of the physical port.
