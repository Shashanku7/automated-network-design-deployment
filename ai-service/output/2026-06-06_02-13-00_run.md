## Phase 2: Network Topology

As a Senior Network Topology Architect, I have designed the following infrastructure utilizing a **Three-Tier Hierarchical Model** (Core, Distribution, Access). This design leverages HPE Aruba-style architecture, emphasizing high availability through VSX (Virtual Switching Extension) and VSF (Virtual Switching Framework).

---

### 1. Topology Overview
*   **Type:** Three-Tier Hierarchical (Collapsed Core/Distribution).
*   **Physical Topology:** Star-Bus Hybrid.
*   **Core Layer:** Centralized High-Performance Chassis/Fixed switches providing L3 routing and inter-VLAN communication.
*   **Distribution Layer:** Building-specific blocks managing traffic aggregation and policy enforcement.
*   **Access Layer:** Floor-specific switches providing PoE+ for APs, VoIP, and IPTV.

### 3. High Availability & Link Design

*   **Core/Dist Redundancy:** **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** is used at the Core and Distribution layers to allow for an active-active data plane, eliminating Spanning Tree (STP) blocking.
*   **Access Redundancy:** **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** is used to stack access switches, presenting them as a single logical entity.
*   **Uplinks:**
    *   **Access $\rightarrow$ Distribution:** Dual 10GbE SFP+ links configured as **LACP (Link Aggregation Control Protocol)** bundles.
    *   **Distribution $\rightarrow$ Core:** Dual 25GbE/40GbE Fiber links (LACP) providing a high-bandwidth backbone.
*   **Failover:** VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol) will be used at the Distribution layer to provide a redundant default gateway for the VLANs.

1.  **End Device** $\rightarrow$ **Access Switch (VSF Stack)** $\rightarrow$ **LACP Bundle (10G)** $\rightarrow$

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

As a Network Hardware Specialist, I have analyzed the approved Three-Tier Hierarchical topology and the specific user counts for both buildings. Based on the retrieved datasheets for the **HPE Aruba Networking CX** portfolio, I have selected the hardware that balances high availability (VSX/VSF) with cost-effectiveness.

### Hardware Selection Logic
*   **Core Layer:** The **CX 6400 Series** is selected for the core due to its modular chassis design, supporting high-density 25G/100G uplinks and VSX for an active-active data plane.
*   **Distribution Layer:** The **CX 6300M Series** is used for building aggregation. These provide the necessary L3 routing capabilities and support VSX clusters for redundancy.
*   **Access Layer:** 
    *   For high-density areas (CS, Electronics, Library), the **CX 6300M PoE** switches are used to support VSF stacking and high-power PoE+ (Class 4) for APs and VoIP.
    *   For lower-density admin areas, the **CX 6200M** is selected as a cost-effective alternative that still supports stacking.
*   **Wi-Fi:** Calculated at $\approx 25\text{--}30$ users per AP.

### Summary of Design Choices:
1.  **Port Density:** All high-density floors (CS, Electronics, Library) utilize **CX 6300M 48-port** switches stacked via **VSF**, ensuring a single management IP and high availability.
2.  **Uptime:** The use of **VSX** at the Core and Distribution layers eliminates STP-related outages and allows for **Live Upgrades** without traffic interruption.
3.  **Growth:** By utilizing 48-port switches where 24-port would suffice, we accommodate the expected growth of 50-200 users without requiring further hardware additions.
4.  **PoE Strategy:** Class 4 PoE (30W) is utilized across all access switches to power the VoIP phones, IPTV devices, and WiFi 6 APs.
