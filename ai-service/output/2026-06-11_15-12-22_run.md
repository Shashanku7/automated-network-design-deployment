## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Architectural Choice:** 3-Tier (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access)
**Justification:** With a total endpoint count exceeding 700 (excluding APs) spread across two distinct buildings and five floors, a 3-tier model is required. This ensures high-performance traffic aggregation at the building level (Distribution), isolates broadcast domains per floor, and provides the necessary scalability to accommodate the projected 200+ user growth without impacting the core.

### 3. High-Availability & Link Design
*   **Core Layer:** Deploy **Aruba CX 8360** switches in a **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** pair. This provides a dual-control plane for non-stop forwarding and in-service software upgrades (ISSU).
*   **Distribution Layer:** Each building will have a pair of **Aruba CX 6300** switches configured as a VSX pair to aggregate floor access switches.
*   **Access Layer:** Floor switches will be grouped using **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** to act as a single logical entity, simplifying management and providing link redundancy.
*   **Linkage:**
    *   **Core $\leftrightarrow$ Distribution:** $2 \times 10\text{Gbps}$ (LACP Bundle) per distribution pair.
    *   **Distribution $\leftrightarrow$ Access:** $2 \times 10\text{Gbps}$ (LACP Bundle) per VSF stack.
    *   **Smart Rate (Multi-Gig):** Building 1 (Floor 2) and Building 2 (Floor 1) will utilize **Aruba CX 6300M (Smart Rate)** switches to provide 2.5/5Gbps links to Wi-Fi 6E APs, preventing wireless bottlenecks in high-density areas.

### 5. Redundancy & Failover Strategy
*   **Default Gateway:** Utilize **VSX Active-Gateway**. This allows both Core/Distribution switches to act as the gateway for a VLAN simultaneously, providing instantaneous failover without the convergence delays associated with VRRP.
*   **Loop Prevention:** Use LACP (Link Aggregation Control Protocol) for all inter-switch links to eliminate the need for Spanning Tree (STP) blocking on primary paths.
*   **Power Redundancy:** All Distribution and Core switches must be equipped with dual hot-swappable power supplies.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the approved topology and the retrieved HPE Aruba Networking datasheets, here is the detailed Bill of Materials (BOM) for the campus network design.

### **Core Layer Selection**
*   **Model:** **Aruba CX 8360-48Y6C v2 (JL 704C)**
*   **Justification:** Required for the core to support **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for non-stop forwarding. The 8360 series provides high-density 10G/25G SFP+ ports and 100G QSFP28 uplinks, ensuring the core can aggregate traffic from both buildings without bottlenecks.

### **Distribution Layer Selection**
*   **Model:** **Aruba CX 6300M (JL 661A/JL 663A)**
*   **Justification:** The CX 6300M is a high-performance Layer 3 stackable switch that supports **VSF** and **VSX** (in specific configurations) and provides the 10G/25G/50G uplinks necessary to connect to the CX 8360 core. Modular power supplies ensure the "important" uptime requirement.

### **Access Layer Selection**
*   **Standard PoE Floors:** **Aruba CX 6200F 48G Class 4 PoE 4SFP+ 740W (JL 728B)**. This provides the necessary port density for students/staff and a high PoE budget (740W) to support VoIP phones and standard APs.
*   **High-Density Wireless Zones:** **Aruba CX 6300M 48p HPE Smart Rate (JL 659A)**. Per the topology, Building 1 Floor 2 and Building 2 Floor 1 require **Smart Rate (Multi-Gig)** to prevent wireless bottlenecks for Wi-Fi 6E APs.
