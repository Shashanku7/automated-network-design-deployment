**Role:** Expert Network Topology Designer & Infrastructure Architect

**Objective:** Design a comprehensive, scalable, and secure campus network topology for an educational organization. The design must ensure high availability, seamless roaming, and robust segmentation based on the specific departmental needs provided in the building breakdown.

**1. Infrastructure Requirements & Scope**
The network must support a multi-floor environment within a single building, catering to a diverse user base of students, faculty, and administrators.

**2. Device & Connectivity Profile**
Each floor must be equipped to handle the following device types:
*   **End-User Devices:** Laptops (WiFi/Wired) and Smartphones.
*   **Peripherals:** Network printers and VoIP phones.
*   **Infrastructure/IoT:** IP Security Cameras and Wireless Access Points (WAPs).
*   **Growth Projection:** The design must accommodate a future growth of 200 to 500 additional users without requiring a complete architectural overhaul.

**3. Performance & Availability Specifications**
*   **Uptime:** High availability is critical. Implement redundancy at the core and distribution layers to eliminate single points of failure.
*   **Bandwidth:** Optimize for high-density environments, particularly on Floor 2 (Machine Learning) and Floor 3 (Information Science), where high-compute and data-heavy workloads are expected.
*   **QoS:** Implement Quality of Service (QoS) to prioritize VoIP traffic and critical administrative data over general student traffic.

**4. Security & Segmentation**
*   **Sensitive Zones:** Create isolated, high-security VLANs and strict Access Control Lists (ACLs) for the following areas:
    *   Finance Department
    *   Examination Centers
    *   Server Room/Data Center
    *   Library Systems
*   **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):** Define specific permission tiers for privileged roles:
    *   **Principal:** Full network visibility and administrative access.
    *   **Exam Controller:** Restricted, secure access to examination servers.
    *   **Finance Head:** Encrypted, isolated access to financial databases.
*   **General Traffic:** Separate VLANs for Students, Staff, and Guests to prevent lateral movement and enhance security.

**5. Deliverables Required**
*   **Logical Topology Diagram:** Showing VLANs, Subnets, and the relationship between Core, Distribution, and Access layers.
*   **Physical Topology Diagram:** Showing the cabling strategy (Fiber/Copper) between floors and the placement of IDF/MDF.
*   **Hardware Recommendation:** Suggested specifications for Switches (Layer 2/3), Routers, and WAPs.

*   **Security Matrix:** A table mapping users/roles to their respective network permissions and zones.

## Phase 2: Network Topology

### Updated Campus Network Topology Design: PJ Building
**Revision:** Capacity increase for Machine Learning (ML) Department to 500 students.

#### 1. Topology Overview
**Type:** Hierarchical Three-Tier Architecture (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access).
The design remains a **Collapsed Core/Distribution** model, but the Access layer for Floor 2 has been scaled up to accommodate the increased density and the high-compute nature of ML workloads.

---

#### 2. Updated Layer Breakdown & Sizing
**Core/Distribution Layer (MDF - Floor 1):**
*   **Block:** PJ-DIST-01.
*   **Hardware:** Pair of L3 Chassis switches in **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**.
*   **Sizing:** Backplane capacity increased to support higher aggregate throughput from the expanded ML access stack.

**Access Layer (IDF - Per Floor):**
*   **Floor 1 (Office):** 1x 48-port PoE+ Switch.
*   **Floor 2 (ML):** **5x 48-port PoE+ Multi-Gigabit Switches** stacked via **VSF**.
    *   *Calculation:* 500 Students + 25 Staff + 1 Admin + $\sim$20 WAPs/Cameras $\approx$ 546 ports.
    *   *Provisioning:* 5 switches provide 240 ports. To accommodate 500+ users, the design now assumes a **High-Density Wireless-First** approach (WAPs handling the bulk of student devices) complemented by high-density wired drops for ML workstations.
*   **Floor 3 (IS):** 2x 48-port PoE+ Multi-Gigabit Switches stacked via **VSF**.

---

#### 3. High Availability & Link Design
*   **Core Redundancy:** VSX active-active forwarding.
*   **Access Redundancy:** 
    *   **Floor 2 (ML):** Uplink capacity increased to **40Gbps Aggregate** (4x10G LACP Bundle) to prevent bottlenecks during large dataset transfers.
    *   **Floor 3 (IS):** 20Gbps Aggregate (2x10G LACP Bundle).
*   **Link Speeds:**
    *   **Core to ML Stack:** 40Gbps (Fiber OM4).
    *   **End-Device:** 1Gbps/2.5Gbps (Multi-Gig for high-performance ML workstations and WiFi-6 APs).

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

To upgrade the core layer for maximum performance and future-proofing (especially considering the high-compute requirements of the Machine Learning department and potential growth of 500+ users), I am upgrading the Core/Distribution layer from the CX 5420 to the **HPE Aruba Networking CX 6400 Series**.

The CX 6400 is a high-performance modular chassis system. While the CX 5420 is excellent for "small cores," the CX 6400 provides a massive jump in switching capacity (up to 28 Tbps compared to 960 Gbps) and significant scalability through line cards.

### Core Layer Upgrade Analysis
*   **Previous Recommendation:** CX 5420 (Modular L3 Chassis, 960 Gbps capacity).
*   **New Recommendation:** **CX 6400 Chassis (R0X26C/R0X27C)**.
    *   **Performance:** The CX 6400 series provides a system switching capacity of **14 Tbps to 28 Tbps**, ensuring that the 40Gbps aggregate uplinks from the ML floor will never bottleneck the core.
    *   **Scalability:** The modular nature allows you to add high-density 10G, 25G, or 100G line cards as the campus grows.
    *   **High Availability:** Fully supports **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** for active-active forwarding and non-disruptive live upgrades.

### Technical Impact of the Upgrade
1.  **Backplane Throughput:** The shift to the CX 6400 removes any potential congestion at the core. The "ML Students" VLAN (10.1.20.0/22) can now push massive datasets across the network without impacting the "Office" or "IS" floors.
2.  **Future-Proofing:** If the organization grows by the expected 200-500 users or adds a new building, you can simply add new line cards to the CX 6400 chassis rather than replacing the core switches.
3.  **Resiliency:** With two CX 6405 chassis in VSX, the network maintains full operationality even during a complete chassis failure or a major firmware update.
