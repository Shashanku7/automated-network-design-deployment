## Phase 2: Network Topology

# Campus Network Topology Design Document

## 1. Topology Overview
**Chosen Architecture:** 3-Tier (Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access)

**Justification:**
The campus consists of two separate buildings with a total of **643 physical endpoints** (excluding APs). A 3-tier model is required to maintain high performance and scalability, ensuring that Building 1 (high-density academic) and Building 2 (research/library) have dedicated distribution blocks to localize traffic and enforce security policies before hitting the core. This architecture prevents a failure in one building from impacting the other and provides the necessary headroom for the requested growth of up to 200 additional users.

## 3. High-Availability & Link Design

### Core & Distribution Layer (The Backbone)
*   **Core Layer:** Deploy two **Aruba CX 8360/8400 series** switches in a **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** pair. This provides a non-stop forwarding plane and a synchronized control plane.
*   **Distribution Layer:** Each building's distribution block will consist of a VSX pair of **Aruba CX 6400 series** switches.
*   **Access Layer:** Switches (Aruba CX 6300M) will be grouped using **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacking to simplify management and provide link redundancy.

### Connectivity Matrix
| Link Path | Technology | Speed | Redundancy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Access $\rightarrow$ Dist** | LACP / Multi-chassis LAG | 2x10Gbps (SFP+) | Dual-homed to VSX Pair |
| **Dist $\rightarrow$ Core** | LACP / Multi-chassis LAG | 2x25Gbps (SFP28) | Dual-homed to Core VSX |
| **Core $\rightarrow$ Edge/FW** | LACP | 2x10Gbps | Redundant Firewalls |

**Special Requirement:** All high-density student areas (CS Dept, Library) will utilize **Smart Rate (Multi-Gigabit)** ports (1/2.5/5Gbps) for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs to prevent bottlenecks at the access layer.

## 5. Redundancy, Failover & QoS Strategy

### Redundancy Mechanism
*   **VSX Active-Gateway:** Used at Core and Distribution. Both switches in the pair share a virtual MAC and IP for the gateway. This allows seamless failover without the convergence delays of VRRP.
*   **LACP (802.3ad):** All uplinks use Link Aggregation to ensure that a single cable failure does not cause a disconnect.

### QoS Strategy (Quality of Service)
To ensure voice and video clarity, the following marking strategy is implemented:
1.  **Voice (VoIP):** Traffic is tagged with **DSCP EF (Expedited Forwarding)**. Strict Priority Queuing is enabled on all switches to ensure voice packets are processed first.
2.  **Video (IPTV):** Traffic is tagged with **DSCP AF41**. Multicast is managed via **IGMP Snooping** at the access layer to prevent flooding of student data ports.
3.  **Student Data:** Tagged as **Best Effort**. Rate-limiting (policing) is applied to student VLANs to prevent a single user from consuming all backbone bandwidth.

### **Technical Implementation Details**
*   **Redundancy:**
    *   **Core/Dist:** VSX (Virtual Switching Extension) is used at the Core and Distribution layers to ensure an active-active data plane and hitless failover.
    *   **Access:** VSF (Virtual Switching Framework) is used to stack the access switches, reducing the number of managed entities and providing link redundancy via LACP.
*   **Power (PoE) Strategy:**
    *   The **CX 6300M/L** series provides Class 4 (30W) and Class 6/8 (up to 90W) PoE, ensuring that high-power Wi-Fi 6E APs and IPTV devices are fully powered.
*   **Throughput:**
    *   **Access $\rightarrow$ Distribution:** 2x10Gbps SFP+ (LACP).
    *   **Distribution $\rightarrow$ Core:** 2x25Gbps SFP28 (LACP).
*   **Security:**
    *   **Dynamic Segmentation:** Implemented across all CX switches using User-Based Tunneling (UBT) to enforce role-based access for the Principal, Finance Head, and Students.
    *   **MACsec:** Enabled on the CX 6300L/M uplinks to secure traffic between access and distribution layers.
