## Phase 2: Network Topology

This design follows a **Hierarchical Three-Tier Campus Architecture** (Core, Distribution, Access), optimized for HPE Aruba networking standards.

### 1. Topology Overview
The network is designed as a **Star-of-Stars (Hub-and-Spoke)** topology. 
*   **Core Layer:** High-speed packet switching and routing.
*   **Distribution Layer:** One distribution block per building, aggregating floor switches.
*   **Access Layer:** Floor-specific switches providing PoE+ connectivity to end-devices.

---

### 2. Layer Breakdown & Hardware Mapping

#### **Building 1: Main Academic Block (Distribution Block A)**
*   **Distribution Layer:** 2x High-performance Chassis or Fixed Switches in **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** mode for a loop-free topology.
*   **Access Layer:**
    *   **Floor 1 (Admin):** 1x 48-port PoE+ Switch (Low density, but high security requirement).
    *   **Floor 2 (CS Dept):** 2x 48-port PoE+ Switches (High density: 229 total devices + WAPs).
    *   **Floor 3 (Electronics):** 2x 48-port PoE+ Switches (Medium-High density: 167 total devices + WAPs).

#### **Building 2: Library & Research (Distribution Block B)**
*   **Distribution Layer:** 2x Switches in **VSX** mode.
*   **Access Layer:**
    *   **Floor 1 (Library):** 2x 48-port PoE+ Switches (High student density).
    *   **Floor 2 (Research):** 1x 48-port PoE+ Switch (Low density, high performance).

---

### 3. High Availability & Link Design

*   **Core-to-Distribution:** Dual 10Gbps Fiber uplinks using **LACP (Link Aggregation)**.
*   **Distribution-to-Access:** Dual 1Gbps or 10Gbps Fiber uplinks.
*   **Redundancy Mechanisms:**
    *   **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension):** Used at Core and Distribution to allow multi-chassis link aggregation (MC-LAG), eliminating Spanning Tree blocked ports.
    *   **VRRP (Virtual Router Redundancy Protocol):** Implemented at the Distribution layer to provide a redundant default gateway for all VLANs.
    *   **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework):** Used at the Access layer to stack switches on the CS and Electronics floors for simplified management.

### 5. Security Matrix & Access Control

To ensure the "Sensitive Areas" and "RBAC" requirements are met, the following ACL policy is implemented at the Distribution/Core layer:

| Source VLAN | Destination VLAN | Permission | Logic/Security Control |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Student | Admin/Staff | **DENY** | Strict Layer 3 ACL |
| Student | VOIP/IPTV | **DENY** | Prevent eavesdropping/interruption |
| Staff | Students | **ALLOW** | Management access |
| Admin | All VLANs | **ALLOW** | Full routing access |
| Finance/Principal| Server Room | **ALLOW** | Restricted to specific MAC/IP |
| All | Internet | **ALLOW** | via Firewall (Web Filtering) |
| Library | Research | **ALLOW** | Shared academic resources |

**Enhanced Security Implementation:**
1.  **Port Security:** Enabled on all Admin and Finance ports (Sticky MAC) to prevent unauthorized device plugging.
2.  **DHCP Snooping & ARP Inspection:** Enabled on all Access switches to prevent Man-in-the-Middle attacks.
3.  **Private VLANs (PVLAN):** Implemented in the Student VLANs on the CS and Library floors to prevent peer-to-peer attacks.
4.  **Dedicated Firewall Zone:** A "DMZ-style" zone created specifically for the Server Room, requiring Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for any admin access.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the provided topology and hardware datasheets, here is the detailed Bill of Materials (BOM).

### Hardware Selection Logic
*   **Core/Distribution:** The **Aruba CX 6300M** series is selected for its **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)** capability, providing the required high availability and loop-free topology. The SFP+ models are used for high-speed aggregation.
*   **Access Layer:** **Aruba CX 6300F** and **6200F** models are selected. The 6300F provides high-density PoE+ and supports **VSF stacking**, which is critical for the high-user counts in the Computer Science and Electronics departments.
*   **Wi-Fi Calculation:** Based on the requirement of ~25-30 users per AP:
    *   Building 1: 423 total users $\approx$ 15 APs.
    *   Building 2: 270 total users $\approx$ 9 APs.
*   **Port Density:** Access switches are sized at 48 ports to accommodate students, staff, VOIP phones, IPTV, and growth (50-200 users).
