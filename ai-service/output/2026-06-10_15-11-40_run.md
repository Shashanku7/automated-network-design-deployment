## Phase 2: Network Topology

### 1. Topology Overview
**Selected Model:** **3-Tier (Core, Distribution, Access)**

**Justification:** 
The campus exceeds 600 total endpoints across five distinct floors in two separate buildings. A 3-tier architecture is required to maintain high performance, ensure clear traffic separation between academic departments and administration, and provide the necessary scalability to absorb the projected growth of 200 additional users without impacting the core. By utilizing a dedicated Distribution layer per building, we localize spanning-tree domains and optimize routing efficiency.

### 3. High-Availability & Redundancy
*   **Core/Distribution Layer:** Implement **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**. This provides a dual-control plane architecture, allowing for **In-Service Software Upgrades (ISSU)** and eliminating the reliance on STP for convergence.
*   **Access Layer:** Implement **VSF (Virtual Switching Framework)** stacking. Access switches on each floor will be stacked into a single logical entity to simplify management and provide cross-stack EtherChannel.
*   **Active Gateway:** Utilize **VSX Active-Gateway** for the default gateways. This allows both Core/Distribution switches to route traffic simultaneously without the "blocked port" limitation of VRRP.
*   **LACP (802.3ad):** All uplinks will be configured as Multi-chassis Link Aggregation (MLAG/LACP) bundles to ensure no single link failure causes an outage.

---

### 4. Link Design & Performance
*   **Core to Distribution:** Dual 10Gbps (SFP+) Fiber links in an LACP bundle.
*   **Distribution to Access:** Dual 10Gbps Fiber uplinks per stack.
*   **Smart Rate (Multi-Gigabit):** High-density student areas (CS Dept, Main Library) will utilize **HPE Smart Rate (2.5G/5G)** ports for Wi-Fi 6/6E Access Points to prevent the 1Gbps bottleneck during peak usage.
*   **QoS Strategy:** 
    *   **Platinum (EF):** VoIP traffic (Strict Priority).
    *   **Gold (AF41):** IPTV and Video Conferencing.
    *   **Silver (BE):** Standard student/staff data.
    *   **Bronze:** Guest Wi-Fi.

---

### 6. Redundancy & Failover Summary
*   **Default Gateway:** Handled by VSX Active-Gateway at the Core/Distribution layer; this avoids VRRP overhead and ensures seamless failover.
*   **Physical Path:** Every access stack has a dual-homed connection (one link to each member of the VSX pair) to ensure a total cable/switch failure does not isolate a floor.
*   **Loop Prevention:** While VSX/VSF minimizes STP, **MSTP (Multiple Spanning Tree Protocol)** will be enabled as a safety fail-safe to prevent loops from accidental user-end patches.
*   **Routing:** **OSPF (Open Shortest Path First)** will be used as the Interior Gateway Protocol (IGP) between the Core and Distribution blocks for fast convergence and scalability.

## Phase 3: Device Selection & BOM

Based on the approved 3-tier topology and the technical specifications of the HPE Aruba Networking CX portfolio, here is the detailed Bill of Materials (BOM) and design implementation.

### 1. Hardware Selection Strategy

*   **Core Layer (Building 1 MDF):** The **CX 6400** series is selected for the Core. It is a modular chassis supporting **VSX (Virtual Switching Extension)**, which is mandatory for the requested "Active-Gateway" and hitless ISSU (In-Service Software Upgrades). It provides the high-density 10G/25G/100G connectivity required to aggregate distribution blocks.
*   **Distribution Layer (Building 2):** The **CX 6300M** is selected. It supports **VSX**, allowing the Building 2 distribution block to act as a single logical entity with dual-homed uplinks to the Core in Building 1.
*   **Access Layer:** 
    *   **High-Density/Wireless Zones (CS Dept, Library):** The **CX 6300L** is selected. It features **Smart Rate (1G/2.5G/5G)** ports to prevent bottlenecks for Wi-Fi 6/6E APs and supports **VSF stacking** for simplified management.
    *   **Standard Zones (Admin, Electronics, Research):** The **CX 6200** is selected. It provides high-performance PoE+ and **VSF stacking**, offering a cost-effective solution for standard endpoint densities.

### 3. Technical Validation

#### **Port Density & Growth**
*   **Building 1, Floor 2 (CS):** Req $\approx 286$ ports $\rightarrow$ $6 \times 48 = 288$ ports. (Meets requirement).
*   **Building 1, Floor 3 (Elect):** Req $\approx 210$ ports $\rightarrow$ $5 \times 48 = 240$ ports. (Exceeds requirement).
*   **Building 2, Floor 1 (Lib):** Req $\approx 268$ ports $\rightarrow$ $6 \times 48 = 288$ ports. (Meets requirement).

#### **PoE Budget & Power**
*   All selected access switches provide **Class 4 (30W)** or **Class 8 (up to 90W)** PoE. 
*   The **CX 6300L** is specifically deployed in the CS Dept and Library to support the higher power draw and multi-gigabit throughput of the predicted 18+ Wi-Fi APs in those zones.

#### **High Availability (HA) Mapping**
*   **Core $\rightarrow$ Distribution:** VSX Cluster (CX 6400 $\leftrightarrow$ CX 6300M) using LACP bundles.
*   **Distribution $\rightarrow$ Access:** Dual-homed LACP uplinks from each VSF stack to both members of the VSX distribution pair.
*   **Access Layer:** VSF Stacking reduces the number of managed entities and eliminates STP blocking on inter-switch links.

#### **Performance & Uplinks**
*   **Core/Dist:** 50GbE (SFP56) and 25GbE (SFP28) links ensure the backplane can handle the aggregated traffic of $\approx 900+$ endpoints.
*   **Access Uplinks:** 10Gbps SFP+ used throughout the access layer to the distribution/core, matching the requested link design.
